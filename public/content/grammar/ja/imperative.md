# Imperative & Prohibitive (食べろ、食べるな)

The imperative form gives direct commands, while the prohibitive form expresses "don't do ~." These forms range from rough and masculine to polite, depending on context.

## Imperative Form (命令形 / めいれいけい)

### Formation

#### Group 1 (u-verbs)

Change the final **u-sound** to the **e-sound**:

| Dictionary | Imperative | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 行(い)く | 行け | Go! |
| 書(か)く | 書け | Write! |
| 話(はな)す | 話せ | Speak! |
| 読(よ)む | 読め | Read! |
| 待(ま)つ | 待て | Wait! |
| 飲(の)む | 飲め | Drink! |
| 走(はし)る | 走れ | Run! |
| 買(か)う | 買え | Buy! |

#### Group 2 (ru-verbs)

Drop the final **る** and add **ろ** (colloquial) or **よ** (literary):

| Dictionary | Imperative | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 食(た)べる | 食べろ | Eat! |
| 見(み)る | 見ろ | Look! |
| 起(お)きる | 起きろ | Wake up! |
| 寝(ね)る | 寝ろ | Sleep! |
| 逃(に)げる | 逃げろ | Run away! |

> 💡 The **ろ** ending is standard in speech. The **よ** ending (食べよ) appears in writing, older Japanese, and some dialects.

#### Group 3 (Irregular)

| Dictionary | Imperative |
|---|---|
| する | **しろ** (spoken) / **せよ** (written) |
| 来(く)る | **来(こ)い** |

## Prohibitive Form (禁止形 / きんしけい)

The prohibitive is much simpler — just add **な** to the **dictionary form**:

| Dictionary | Prohibitive | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 行(い)く | 行くな | Don't go! |
| 食(た)べる | 食べるな | Don't eat! |
| 見(み)る | 見るな | Don't look! |
| 触(さわ)る | 触るな | Don't touch! |
| する | するな | Don't do it! |
| 来(く)る | 来るな | Don't come! |
| 動(うご)く | 動くな | Don't move! |

> ⚠️ Don't confuse the prohibitive な with the sentence-ending particle な (which expresses emotion or emphasis). Context and tone make the difference clear.

## When Is the Imperative Used?

The plain imperative (食べろ、行け) is considered **rough and forceful**. It's commonly used in:

### 1. Emergencies and urgency

> 逃(に)げろ！
> *Nigero!*
> Run away! (Flee!)

> 止(と)まれ！
> *Tomare!*
> Stop!

> 早(はや)くしろ！
> *Hayaku shiro!*
> Hurry up!

### 2. Sports, cheering, and encouragement

> 頑張(がんば)れ！
> *Ganbare!*
> Do your best! / Go for it!

> 走(はし)れ！
> *Hashire!*
> Run!

> 打(う)て！
> *Ute!*
> Hit it!

### 3. Manga, anime, and drama

> 待(ま)て！
> *Mate!*
> Wait!

> 黙(だま)れ！
> *Damare!*
> Shut up!

### 4. Signs and formal written instructions

Some official signs use the imperative or written forms:

> 止(と)まれ (Stop — on road signs)
> 注意(ちゅうい)せよ (Be cautious — formal/written)

### 5. Angry or rough speech

Between close male friends or in arguments:

> うるさい、黙(だま)れ！
> *Urusai, damare!*
> Be quiet, shut up!

> ここに来(こ)い！
> *Koko ni koi!*
> Come here!

> 💡 The imperative is generally associated with **male speech** in casual contexts. Women can and do use it, but it sounds rougher. Both genders use it in emergencies.

## Softer Alternatives to the Imperative

In everyday life, Japanese speakers usually prefer softer ways to give instructions:

### 〜てください (Please do ~)

> ここに座(すわ)ってください。
> *Koko ni suwatte kudasai.*
> Please sit here.

### 〜て (Casual request)

> ちょっと待(ま)って。
> *Chotto matte.*
> Wait a moment.

### 〜なさい (Authoritative but softer)

Used by parents, teachers, and authority figures:

> 宿題(しゅくだい)をしなさい。
> *Shukudai wo shinasai.*
> Do your homework.

> 早(はや)く寝(ね)なさい。
> *Hayaku nenasai.*
> Go to bed early.

| Form | Strength | Example |
|---|---|---|
| 〜て | Casual request | 見て |
| 〜てください | Polite request | 見てください |
| 〜なさい | Parental / authoritative | 見なさい |
| Imperative (〜ろ/〜え) | Strong command | 見ろ |

## Softer Alternatives to the Prohibitive

### 〜ないでください (Please don't ~)

> ここで写真(しゃしん)を撮(と)らないでください。
> *Koko de shashin wo toranai de kudasai.*
> Please don't take photos here.

### 〜ないで (Casual: Don't ~)

> 心配(しんぱい)しないで。
> *Shinpai shinai de.*
> Don't worry.

### 〜てはいけない / 〜ちゃだめ (Must not ~)

> ここで泳(およ)いではいけません。
> *Koko de oyoide wa ikemasen.*
> You must not swim here.

> 触(さわ)っちゃだめ！
> *Sawaccha dame!*
> Don't touch! (casual)

| Form | Strength | Example |
|---|---|---|
| 〜ないで | Casual request | 見ないで |
| 〜ないでください | Polite request | 見ないでください |
| 〜てはいけない | Rule/prohibition | 見てはいけない |
| Prohibitive (〜な) | Strong command | 見るな |

## Common Imperative & Prohibitive Expressions

| Japanese | Romaji | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 頑張(がんば)れ | Ganbare | Do your best! |
| 気(き)をつけろ | Ki wo tsukero | Be careful! |
| 待(ま)て | Mate | Wait! |
| 逃(に)げろ | Nigero | Run! |
| 黙(だま)れ | Damare | Shut up! |
| 触(さわ)るな | Sawaru na | Don't touch! |
| 動(うご)くな | Ugoku na | Don't move! |
| 諦(あきら)めるな | Akirameru na | Don't give up! |
| 心配(しんぱい)するな | Shinpai suru na | Don't worry! |

> 諦(あきら)めるな！最後(さいご)まで頑張(がんば)れ！
> *Akirameru na! Saigo made ganbare!*
> Don't give up! Do your best until the end!

## Summary

| Form | Formation | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Imperative (Group 1) | u → e (行く → 行け) | Strong command |
| Imperative (Group 2) | る → ろ (食べる → 食べろ) | Strong command |
| Prohibitive | Dictionary + な (食べるな) | Strong prohibition |
| 〜なさい | Stem + なさい | Parental / soft command |
| 〜てください | て-form + ください | Polite request |
| 〜ないでください | ない-form + でください | Polite prohibition |

The imperative and prohibitive forms are powerful tools. While you may not use them often in polite conversation, understanding them is essential for comprehending anime, manga, sports commentary, emergency situations, and everyday expressions like 頑張れ!
