# Verb Forms (ます、て、た)

Japanese verbs change form to express tense, politeness, and connection between clauses. Understanding the three core forms — **ます form**, **て form**, and **た form** — will unlock a huge range of expression.

## Dictionary Form vs ます Form

The **dictionary form** is the plain form you find in dictionaries. The **ます form** is the polite present/future tense used in everyday conversation.

| Group | Dictionary | ます Form | Meaning |
|-------|-----------|----------|---------|
| Group 1 (u-verbs) | 書く (kaku) | 書きます (kakimasu) | to write |
| Group 1 | 飲む (nomu) | 飲みます (nomimasu) | to drink |
| Group 2 (ru-verbs) | 食べる (taberu) | 食べます (tabemasu) | to eat |
| Group 2 | 見る (miru) | 見ます (mimasu) | to see |
| Irregular | する (suru) | します (shimasu) | to do |
| Irregular | 来る (kuru) | 来ます (kimasu) | to come |

**Group 1** verbs: change the final -u sound to -i and add ます.
**Group 2** verbs: drop る and add ます.

## て Form (Te-form)

The **て form** is one of the most versatile forms. It is used to connect actions, make requests, describe ongoing actions, and more.

### Group 1 (u-verbs) — Te-form Rules

| Ending | Change | Example |
|--------|--------|---------|
| う、つ、る | → って | 買う → 買って |
| む、ぶ、ぬ | → んで | 飲む → 飲んで |
| く | → いて | 書く → 書いて |
| ぐ | → いで | 泳ぐ → 泳いで |
| す | → して | 話す → 話して |

**Exception:** 行く (iku) → 行って (itte), not 行いて.

### Group 2 (ru-verbs) — Te-form

Simply drop る and add て: 食べる → 食べて, 見る → 見て.

### Irregular Verbs

- する → して
- 来る → 来て (kite)

### Common Uses of て Form

1. **Connecting actions:** 朝起きて、顔を洗います。(I wake up and wash my face.)
2. **Requests:** 待ってください。(Please wait.)
3. **Ongoing action (ている):** 本を読んでいます。(I am reading a book.)

## た Form (Past Tense)

The **た form** follows the exact same pattern as the て form — just replace て with た (and で with だ).

| て Form | た Form | Meaning |
|---------|--------|---------|
| 食べて | 食べた | ate |
| 飲んで | 飲んだ | drank |
| 書いて | 書いた | wrote |
| して | した | did |

- **昨日、映画を見た。** (Kinō, eiga o mita.) — I watched a movie yesterday.
- **友達が来た。** (Tomodachi ga kita.) — A friend came.

## Practice Tips

1. Start by memorizing the て form rules — た form comes free once you know て form.
2. Practice converting verbs from dictionary form to ます, て, and た forms daily.
3. Listen for these forms in native content — they appear constantly in conversation.
