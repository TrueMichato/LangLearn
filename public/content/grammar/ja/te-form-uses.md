# て-Form Advanced Uses (ている、てある、ておく)

The て form is one of the most powerful connectors in Japanese. Beyond simple requests and sequencing, it combines with auxiliary verbs to express ongoing actions, resultant states, preparation, and directional continuity. Mastering these patterns will dramatically expand your expressive range.

## Review: て Form Basics

Before diving into advanced uses, make sure you know how to form the て form:

| Group | Rule | Example |
|-------|------|---------|
| Group 1 (u-verbs) | varies by ending | 書(か)く → 書いて, 飲(の)む → 飲んで |
| Group 2 (ru-verbs) | drop る + て | 食(た)べる → 食べて |
| Irregular | memorize | する → して, 来(く)る → 来(き)て |

## ている — Ongoing Action & Resultant State

ている is formed by adding **いる** to the **て form**. It has two major uses that depend on the type of verb.

### 1. Ongoing Action (Progressive)

With **action verbs** (verbs that describe activities), ている expresses an action in progress — similar to English "-ing":

> **今(いま)、本(ほん)を読(よ)んでいます。**
> *Ima, hon o yonde imasu.*
> I am reading a book right now.

> **子供(こども)たちが公園(こうえん)で遊(あそ)んでいます。**
> *Kodomo-tachi ga kōen de asonde imasu.*
> The children are playing in the park.

> **母(はは)は台所(だいどころ)で料理(りょうり)をしています。**
> *Haha wa daidokoro de ryōri o shite imasu.*
> My mother is cooking in the kitchen.

> **友達(ともだち)を待(ま)っています。**
> *Tomodachi o matte imasu.*
> I am waiting for a friend.

> **雨(あめ)が降(ふ)っています。**
> *Ame ga futte imasu.*
> It is raining.

### 2. Resultant State

With **instantaneous verbs** (verbs that describe a change of state), ている expresses the **result** of a completed action:

| Verb | ている meaning | Note |
|------|--------------|------|
| 知(し)る (to get to know) | 知っている = know | NOT "am getting to know" |
| 結婚(けっこん)する (to marry) | 結婚している = is married | NOT "is marrying" |
| 死(し)ぬ (to die) | 死んでいる = is dead | NOT "is dying" |
| 住(す)む (to settle) | 住んでいる = lives (in) | NOT "is settling" |
| 持(も)つ (to grab) | 持っている = has/holds | NOT "is grabbing" |

> **東京(とうきょう)に住(す)んでいます。**
> *Tōkyō ni sunde imasu.*
> I live in Tokyo.

> **彼(かれ)はもう結婚(けっこん)しています。**
> *Kare wa mō kekkon shite imasu.*
> He is already married.

> **この言葉(ことば)を知(し)っていますか。**
> *Kono kotoba o shitte imasu ka.*
> Do you know this word?

> 💡 The negative of 知っている is 知**らない** (not 知っていない). This is an important exception!

> **彼女(かのじょ)は眼鏡(めがね)をかけています。**
> *Kanojo wa megane o kakete imasu.*
> She wears glasses. (resultant state)

### Casual Forms of ている

In casual speech, ている is often shortened:

| Formal | Casual | Example |
|--------|--------|---------|
| ている | てる | 食べている → 食べてる |
| ています | てます | 読んでいます → 読んでます |
| ていた | てた | 見ていた → 見てた |
| ていない | てない | 知っていない → 知ってない |

> **何(なに)してる？**
> *Nani shiteru?*
> What are you doing? (casual)

## てある — Resultant State of Transitive Verbs

てある is formed by adding **ある** to the **て form** of a **transitive verb**. It describes a state that exists as the result of someone's deliberate action.

### Formation

> Transitive verb (て form) + ある

The object of the action is marked with **が** (not を):

| Action (someone did) | Resultant State (てある) |
|---------------------|------------------------|
| 窓(まど)を開(あ)ける (open the window) | 窓**が**開けてある (the window is open — someone opened it) |
| 名前(なまえ)を書(か)く (write a name) | 名前**が**書いてある (a name is written) |
| エアコンをつける (turn on the AC) | エアコン**が**つけてある (the AC is on — someone turned it on) |

> **壁(かべ)にポスターが貼(は)ってあります。**
> *Kabe ni posutā ga hatte arimasu.*
> A poster is put up on the wall. (someone put it there)

> **テーブルの上(うえ)にお皿(さら)が並(なら)べてあります。**
> *Tēburu no ue ni o-sara ga narabete arimasu.*
> Plates are arranged on the table.

> **もうホテルが予約(よやく)してあります。**
> *Mō hoteru ga yoyaku shite arimasu.*
> The hotel has already been reserved.

> **ドアに「押(お)す」と書いてあります。**
> *Doa ni "osu" to kaite arimasu.*
> "Push" is written on the door.

### ている vs てある

| Expression | Meaning | Focus |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| 窓が開(あ)いている | The window is open | State (intransitive) |
| 窓が開けてある | The window has been opened (by someone) | Deliberate result (transitive) |
| 電気(でんき)がついている | The light is on | State |
| 電気がつけてある | The light has been turned on (on purpose) | Deliberate result |

> 💡 てある always implies that someone **intentionally** performed the action, and the result remains.

## ておく — Doing Something in Advance

ておく is formed by adding **おく** to the **て form**. It means doing something **in preparation** or **in advance** for a future purpose.

### Formation

> Verb (て form) + おく

### Usage & Examples

> **パーティーの前(まえ)に、飲(の)み物(もの)を買(か)っておきます。**
> *Pātī no mae ni, nomimono o katte okimasu.*
> I'll buy drinks in advance (before the party).

> **明日(あした)のために資料(しりょう)を準備(じゅんび)しておきます。**
> *Ashita no tame ni shiryō o junbi shite okimasu.*
> I'll prepare the materials in advance for tomorrow.

> **旅行(りょこう)の前にパスポートを確認(かくにん)しておいてください。**
> *Ryokō no mae ni pasupōto o kakunin shite oite kudasai.*
> Please check your passport before the trip.

> **この本(ほん)を読(よ)んでおいたほうがいいですよ。**
> *Kono hon o yonde oita hō ga ii desu yo.*
> You should read this book (in advance).

> **冷蔵庫(れいぞうこ)にケーキを入(い)れておきました。**
> *Reizōko ni kēki o irete okimashita.*
> I put the cake in the fridge (for later).

### Casual Contraction: とく

In casual speech, ておく contracts to **とく**:

| Full form | Casual |
|-----------|--------|
| 買っておく | 買っとく |
| 読んでおく | 読んどく |
| しておく | しとく |
| 言(い)っておく | 言っとく |

> **先(さき)に言(い)っとくけど、明日(あした)は忙(いそが)しいよ。**
> *Saki ni ittoku kedo, ashita wa isogashii yo.*
> Just letting you know in advance, I'm busy tomorrow.

### ておく for "Leaving as is"

ておく can also mean to **leave something** in a certain state:

> **そのままにしておいてください。**
> *Sono mama ni shite oite kudasai.*
> Please leave it as it is.

> **窓(まど)を開(あ)けておきましょう。**
> *Mado o akete okimashō.*
> Let's leave the window open.

## ていく & てくる — Directional Continuity

These patterns combine the て form with 行(い)く (to go) and 来(く)る (to come) to express actions that continue in a direction — either away from or toward the present.

### ていく — Continuing Away / Into the Future

> Verb (て form) + いく

Describes an action that **moves away** from the speaker or **continues into the future**:

> **気温(きおん)がどんどん上(あ)がっていきます。**
> *Kion ga dondon agatte ikimasu.*
> The temperature keeps going up (and will continue).

> **人口(じんこう)が減(へ)っていくでしょう。**
> *Jinkō ga hette iku deshō.*
> The population will probably keep decreasing.

> **鳥(とり)が飛(と)んでいきました。**
> *Tori ga tonde ikimashita.*
> The bird flew away.

> **お弁当(べんとう)を持(も)っていきます。**
> *Obentō o motte ikimasu.*
> I'll take a lunch box (with me).

### てくる — Coming Toward / From the Past

> Verb (て form) + くる

Describes an action that **moves toward** the speaker or has **continued from the past until now**:

> **日本語(にほんご)が上手(じょうず)になってきました。**
> *Nihongo ga jōzu ni natte kimashita.*
> My Japanese has been getting better (up to now).

> **雨(あめ)が降(ふ)ってきた！**
> *Ame ga futte kita!*
> It started raining! (rain came toward me)

> **寒(さむ)くなってきましたね。**
> *Samuku natte kimashita ne.*
> It's gotten cold, hasn't it? (gradual change until now)

> **お土産(みやげ)を買(か)ってきました。**
> *Omiyage o katte kimashita.*
> I went and bought souvenirs (and came back).

> **ちょっとコンビニに行(い)ってきます。**
> *Chotto konbini ni itte kimasu.*
> I'll just pop out to the convenience store (and come back).

> 💡 行ってきます is the standard phrase said when leaving the house — literally "I'll go and come back."

### Summary Table: ていく vs てくる

| Pattern | Direction | Time sense | Example |
|---------|-----------|------------|---------|
| ていく | Away from speaker | Into the future | 持っていく (take with) |
| てくる | Toward speaker | From past to now | 持ってくる (bring here) |

## All Patterns at a Glance

| Pattern | Meaning | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| ている | Ongoing action / resultant state | 食べている (is eating / has eaten) |
| てある | Resultant state (deliberate) | 書いてある (is written — by someone) |
| ておく | Do in advance / leave as is | 買っておく (buy in advance) |
| ていく | Continue away / into future | 減っていく (keep decreasing) |
| てくる | Continue toward / from past | なってきた (has become) |

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Confusing ている with てある

- ❌ ドアが開いてある (開く is intransitive — use ている)
- ✅ ドアが開いている (the door is open)
- ✅ ドアが開けてある (the door has been opened — deliberate, transitive)

### 2. Using を with てある

- ❌ 窓を開けてあります
- ✅ 窓**が**開けてあります

### 3. Forgetting the nuance of ておく

ておく is not just "do beforehand" — it also carries the sense of "for a reason" or "for later."
