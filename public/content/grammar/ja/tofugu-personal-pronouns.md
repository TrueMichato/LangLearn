# Personal Pronouns

> **Source:** Content from [Tofugu](https://www.tofugu.com/japanese-grammar/personal-pronouns/). For personal study.

Japanese personal pronouns work like regular nouns — they don't change form based on grammatical role (I/me/my). Instead, **particles** assign the role.

## Pronouns Don't Change Form

Unlike English, 私 serves as "I," "me," and "my":

- **私は**ジェニファーです。 — **I** am Jennifer. (は = topic)
- **私を**ジェニーと呼んでください。 — Please call **me** Jenny. (を = object)
- **私の**ニックネームです。 — It's **my** nickname. (の = possessive)

## Pronouns Are Often Omitted

Japanese sentences function without explicit pronouns when context is clear:

- ジェニファーです。 — (I am) Jennifer.
- どこの出身ですか？ — Where (are you) from?

Second-person pronouns in particular are often avoided to maintain politeness.

## First-Person Pronouns ("I")

| Pronoun | Reading | Nuance |
|---|---|---|
| 私 | わたし / わたくし | Neutral-formal; わたくし is more elegant |
| 僕 | ぼく | Boyish, polite, somewhat masculine |
| 俺 | おれ | Strongly masculine, can sound vulgar |
| うち | うち | Popular among young female speakers |

## Second-Person Pronouns ("You")

All second-person pronouns carry some directness and can seem rude. It's common to use names or social roles instead.

| Pronoun | Reading | Nuance |
|---|---|---|
| あなた | あなた | Standard textbook choice, relatively neutral |
| 君 | きみ | Can sound condescending; literary/poetic |
| お前 | おまえ | Rough, masculine; also shows closeness |

## Third-Person Pronouns ("He/She")

| Pronoun | Meaning | Note |
|---|---|---|
| 彼 (かれ) | He | Also means "boyfriend" |
| 彼女 (かのじょ) | She | Also means "girlfriend" |
| こいつ/そいつ/あいつ | This/that person | Very casual, like "this guy" |

## Plural Forms

Add a suffix to make pronouns plural:

- 私 + 達 = 私達 (we)
- Common suffixes: 〜達 (たち), 〜等 (ら), 〜方 (がた), 〜供 (ども)

## 自分 as a Pronoun

自分 (じぶん) can be used as both first and second person:

- **自分**も、そう思います。 — **I** think so too. (gender-neutral first person)
- **自分**、何してんの？ — What are **you** doing? (Kansai dialect, second person)

## Addressing Children

With children, adults often use role-based pronouns from the child's perspective:

- **ママ**もお腹すいたよ。 — **I'm** hungry too. (Mom referring to herself as "Mama")
- **僕**、迷子？ — Are **you** lost? (Using the boy's own first-person pronoun to mean "you")
