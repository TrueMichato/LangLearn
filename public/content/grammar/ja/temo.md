# Even if / Although (ても、のに、けど)

Japanese has several ways to express **contrast**, **concession**, and **"even if"** situations. These grammar points let you say things like "even though I studied, I failed" or "even if it rains, I'll go."

## ても / でも ("Even if ~" / "Even though ~")

**ても** expresses that the result is the same regardless of the condition. It can refer to hypothetical or real situations.

### Formation

| Type | Formation | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Verb (て-form) + も | 食べても | Even if I eat |
| い-adj → くても | 高くても | Even if it's expensive |
| な-adj → でも | 静かでも | Even if it's quiet |
| Noun → でも | 雨でも | Even if it's rain |
| Negative verb | 食べなくても | Even if I don't eat |
| Past verb | 食べても | Even though I ate |

> 雨(あめ)が降(ふ)っても、行(い)きます。
> *Ame ga futte mo, ikimasu.*
> Even if it rains, I'll go.

> どんなに高(たか)くても、買(か)いたい。
> *Donna ni takakute mo, kaitai.*
> No matter how expensive it is, I want to buy it.

> たくさん食(た)べても、太(ふと)らない。
> *Takusan tabete mo, futoranai.*
> Even though I eat a lot, I don't gain weight.

> 薬(くすり)を飲(の)んでも、治(なお)らなかった。
> *Kusuri wo nonde mo, naoranakatta.*
> Even though I took medicine, it didn't get better.

### 〜ても〜ても (No matter how much ~)

Repeating ても emphasizes persistence:

> 何度(なんど)やっても、やってもうまくいかない。
> *Nando yatte mo, yatte mo umaku ikanai.*
> No matter how many times I try, it doesn't work.

### Interrogative + ても (No matter wh-)

| Pattern | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 何(なに)を食べても | No matter what I eat |
| どこに行っても | No matter where I go |
| 誰(だれ)に聞(き)いても | No matter who I ask |
| いつ来(き)ても | No matter when you come |
| いくら頑張(がんば)っても | No matter how hard I try |

> どこに行(い)っても、日本語(にほんご)を使(つか)いたい。
> *Doko ni itte mo, nihongo wo tsukaitai.*
> No matter where I go, I want to use Japanese.

> 誰(だれ)に聞(き)いても、答(こた)えは同(おな)じだった。
> *Dare ni kiite mo, kotae wa onaji datta.*
> No matter who I asked, the answer was the same.

## のに ("Although ~" / "Even though ~")

**のに** expresses a **contrast between expectation and reality**, often with a sense of **disappointment, frustration, or surprise**.

### Formation

| Type | Formation | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Verb (plain) + のに | 行ったのに | Although I went |
| い-adj + のに | 高いのに | Although it's expensive |
| な-adj + **な** + のに | 静かなのに | Although it's quiet |
| Noun + **な** + のに | 日曜日なのに | Although it's Sunday |

> せっかく作(つく)ったのに、誰(だれ)も食(た)べなかった。
> *Sekkaku tsukutta noni, dare mo tabenakatta.*
> Even though I went to the trouble of making it, nobody ate it.

> 約束(やくそく)したのに、来(こ)なかった。
> *Yakusoku shita noni, konakatta.*
> Even though they promised, they didn't come.

> 高(たか)いのに、おいしくない。
> *Takai noni, oishikunai.*
> Even though it's expensive, it's not tasty.

> 日曜日(にちようび)なのに、仕事(しごと)がある。
> *Nichiyoubi na noni, shigoto ga aru.*
> Even though it's Sunday, I have work.

> 💡 のに carries an **emotional nuance** — the speaker feels that the outcome is unexpected or unfair. It's different from the neutral contrast of けど.

### Trailing のに (expressing frustration)

のに can end a sentence, leaving the contrast implied:

> せっかく買(か)ったのに…
> *Sekkaku katta noni...*
> I went to the trouble of buying it (and yet... [something disappointing happened])

> もっと早(はや)く言(い)ってくれればよかったのに。
> *Motto hayaku itte kurereba yokatta noni.*
> I wish you had told me sooner. (but you didn't)

## けど / けれど / けれども ("But" / "Although")

**けど** is a general-purpose contrast connector, less emotional than のに.

### Formation

Attach to the **plain form** (casual) or **ます form** (polite):

> 日本語(にほんご)は難(むずか)しいけど、面白(おもしろ)い。
> *Nihongo wa muzukashii kedo, omoshiroi.*
> Japanese is difficult, but interesting.

> 行(い)きたいですけど、時間(じかん)がない。
> *Ikitai desu kedo, jikan ga nai.*
> I want to go, but I don't have time.

> 安(やす)いけど、品質(ひんしつ)はいい。
> *Yasui kedo, hinshitsu wa ii.*
> It's cheap, but the quality is good.

### Formality levels

| Form | Formality |
|---|---|
| けど | Casual |
| けれど | Slightly formal |
| けれども | Formal |
| が (after ます/です) | Most formal |

> 参加(さんか)したいのです**が**、予定(よてい)があります。
> *Sanka shitai no desu **ga**, yotei ga arimasu.*
> I'd like to participate, but I have plans. (very polite)

## Comparing ても、のに、けど

| | ても | のに | けど |
|---|---|---|---|
| Meaning | Even if / even though | Although (frustrated) | But / although |
| Emotion | Neutral | Disappointed/surprised | Neutral |
| Hypothetical? | Yes (even if) | No (real situations) | Both |
| Emphasis | Condition doesn't matter | Unexpected result | Simple contrast |

### Same situation, different nuance:

> 勉強(べんきょう)し**ても**、テストに落(お)ちた。
> *Benkyou shite mo, tesuto ni ochita.*
> Even though I studied, I failed. (matter-of-fact)

> 勉強(べんきょう)した**のに**、テストに落(お)ちた。
> *Benkyou shita noni, tesuto ni ochita.*
> I studied and yet I failed. (frustrated/disappointed)

> 勉強(べんきょう)した**けど**、テストに落(お)ちた。
> *Benkyou shita kedo, tesuto ni ochita.*
> I studied, but I failed. (neutral contrast)

## Other Concessive Expressions

### 〜としても (Even assuming ~)

> 成功(せいこう)するとしても、時間(じかん)がかかる。
> *Seikou suru to shite mo, jikan ga kakaru.*
> Even assuming we succeed, it will take time.

### 〜にもかかわらず (Despite ~ / formal)

> 努力(どりょく)にもかかわらず、結果(けっか)は出(で)なかった。
> *Doryoku ni mo kakawarazu, kekka wa denakatta.*
> Despite the effort, there were no results.

### 〜くせに (Even though ~ / critical tone)

> 知(し)っている**くせに**、教(おし)えてくれない。
> *Shitte iru **kuse ni**, oshiete kurenai.*
> Even though you know, you won't tell me. (critical/accusatory)

## Summary

| Grammar | Use for | Tone |
|---|---|---|
| ても | "Even if" — result unchanged | Neutral |
| のに | "Although" — unexpected/frustrating result | Emotional |
| けど | "But" — simple contrast | Neutral |
| としても | "Even assuming" — hypothetical | Formal |
| にもかかわらず | "Despite" — formal writing | Very formal |
| くせに | "Even though" — blaming | Critical |

Mastering these contrast expressions adds depth and nuance to your Japanese. Pay attention to the emotional weight each one carries — choosing the right one reveals how you feel about the situation!
