# Conjunctions & Connectors

Japanese has many ways to connect sentences and clauses. This lesson covers the most important conjunctions for linking ideas, showing reasons, contrasts, and concessions.

## が — "But" (Polite Contrast)

**が** connects two sentences with a contrasting or unexpected relationship. It is relatively polite and formal.

- **日本語は難(むずか)しいですが、楽(たの)しいです。** — Japanese is difficult, but fun.
- **行きたいですが、時間(じかん)がありません。** — I want to go, but I don't have time.
- **すみませんが、もう一度(いちど)言(い)ってください。** — Excuse me, but please say that again.
- **高(たか)いですが、品質(ひんしつ)がいいです。** — It's expensive, but the quality is good.
- **調(しら)べましたが、わかりませんでした。** — I looked into it, but I couldn't figure it out.

> 💡 が can also be used as a soft introduction, not necessarily meaning "but": 山田ですが、田中さんはいらっしゃいますか。— This is Yamada; is Tanaka available?

## けど / けれど / けれども — "But" (Casual to Formal)

These are variants of the same conjunction, ranging from casual to formal:

| Form | Formality |
|------|-----------|
| けど | Casual |
| けれど | Neutral |
| けれども | Formal |

- **疲(つか)れたけど、まだ頑張(がんば)れます。** — I'm tired, but I can still keep going. (casual)
- **安(やす)いけど、おいしいです。** — It's cheap but delicious.
- **雨(あめ)だけど、出(で)かけましょう。** — It's raining, but let's go out.
- **知(し)っていますけれども、教(おし)えられません。** — I know, but I can't tell you. (formal)
- **小(ちい)さいけど、便利(べんり)な部屋(へや)です。** — It's small but a convenient room.

## から — "Because" (Subjective Reason)

**から** gives a reason or cause. It follows the plain or polite form and conveys a **personal/subjective** reason.

- **お腹(なか)が空(す)いたから、何か食(た)べたいです。** — Because I'm hungry, I want to eat something.
- **雨が降(ふ)っているから、傘(かさ)を持(も)って行(い)ってください。** — Because it's raining, please take an umbrella.
- **明日(あした)テストがあるから、今日は勉強(べんきょう)します。** — Because there's a test tomorrow, I'll study today.
- **暑(あつ)いから、窓(まど)を開(あ)けてもいいですか。** — Because it's hot, may I open the window?
- **時間がないから、急(いそ)ぎましょう。** — We don't have time, so let's hurry.

## ので — "Because" (Objective/Polite Reason)

**ので** also means "because" but sounds more **objective and polite** than から. It connects with the plain form (な-adjectives and nouns use な before ので).

- **電車が遅(おく)れたので、遅刻(ちこく)しました。** — Because the train was delayed, I was late.
- **体(からだ)の調子(ちょうし)が悪(わる)いので、休(やす)みます。** — Because I'm not feeling well, I'll take the day off.
- **静(しず)かなので、この図書館(としょかん)が好(す)きです。** — Because it's quiet, I like this library.
- **学生(がくせい)なので、割引(わりびき)があります。** — Because I'm a student, there's a discount.
- **危険(きけん)なので、触(さわ)らないでください。** — Because it's dangerous, please don't touch it.

### から vs ので

| | から | ので |
|---|------|------|
| Feeling | Subjective, direct | Objective, polite |
| Formality | Casual ↔ Polite | Polite ↔ Formal |
| With requests | Natural | Very natural |

- 疲れた**から**、帰ります。 — I'm going home because I'm tired. (direct, personal)
- 疲れた**ので**、帰ります。 — I'm going home because I'm tired. (softer, more polite)

## のに — "Even Though / Despite"

**のに** expresses frustration or surprise that the result is contrary to expectation.

- **たくさん勉強したのに、テストに落(お)ちました。** — Even though I studied a lot, I failed the test.
- **約束(やくそく)したのに、来ませんでした。** — Even though they promised, they didn't come.
- **高いのに、あまりおいしくないです。** — Even though it's expensive, it's not very good.
- **薬(くすり)を飲んだのに、まだ頭(あたま)が痛(いた)いです。** — Even though I took medicine, I still have a headache.
- **何度(なんど)も言ったのに、わかってくれません。** — Even though I've said it many times, they don't understand.

> 💡 のに carries an emotional tone — the speaker feels dissatisfied or surprised.

## ても — "Even If / Even Though"

**ても** is the て-form + も pattern, meaning "even if" or "even though."

### Formation

| Type | ても form |
|------|---------|
| Verb | 食べ**ても** |
| い-adj | 高く**ても** |
| な-adj | 静かで**も** |
| Noun | 学生で**も** |

- **雨が降っても、行きます。** — Even if it rains, I'll go.
- **高くても、買いたいです。** — Even if it's expensive, I want to buy it.
- **何度聞いても、わかりません。** — No matter how many times I hear it, I don't understand.
- **疲れていても、仕事(しごと)をしなければなりません。** — Even if I'm tired, I have to work.
- **子供(こども)でも、できます。** — Even a child can do it.

## し — "And What's More / And Also"

**し** lists multiple reasons or qualities, often building a case.

- **この店は安いし、おいしいし、最高(さいこう)です。** — This shop is cheap, delicious, and the best.
- **時間もないし、お金(かね)もないし、旅行できません。** — I don't have time or money, so I can't travel.
- **彼(かれ)は優(やさ)しいし、おもしろいし、人気(にんき)があります。** — He's kind, funny, and popular.
- **頭もいいし、スポーツもできるし、すごいです。** — They're smart, good at sports — amazing.
- **天気もいいし、暖(あたた)かいし、散歩(さんぽ)しましょう。** — The weather's nice and warm, so let's take a walk.

## それに / そのうえ — "Moreover / Furthermore"

These connect sentences to add information:

- **この部屋は広(ひろ)いです。それに、駅(えき)から近(ちか)いです。** — This room is spacious. Moreover, it's close to the station.
- **安いです。そのうえ、品質がいいです。** — It's cheap. Furthermore, the quality is good.

## だから / ですから — "Therefore / So"

Connects a reason to a conclusion:

- **明日テストがあります。だから、今日は勉強します。** — There's a test tomorrow. So, I'll study today.
- **電車が止(と)まりました。ですから、遅刻しました。** — The train stopped. Therefore, I was late.
- **雨が降っています。だから、傘を持っていきましょう。** — It's raining. So, let's take umbrellas.

## でも / しかし — "However / But"

Start a new sentence with a contrast:

- **日本語を勉強しています。でも、まだあまり話せません。** — I'm studying Japanese. But I can't speak it well yet. (casual)
- **努力(どりょく)しました。しかし、結果(けっか)は出(で)ませんでした。** — I made an effort. However, I didn't get results. (formal)

## Summary

| Conjunction | Meaning | Example |
|------------|---------|---------|
| が / けど | But | 難しいが、楽しい |
| から | Because (subjective) | 暑いから |
| ので | Because (polite) | 暑いので |
| のに | Even though (frustrated) | 勉強したのに |
| ても | Even if | 雨が降っても |
| し | And also (listing reasons) | 安いし、おいしいし |
| だから | Therefore | だから、勉強する |
| でも / しかし | However | でも、話せない |

Master these connectors and your Japanese sentences will flow naturally from one idea to the next!
