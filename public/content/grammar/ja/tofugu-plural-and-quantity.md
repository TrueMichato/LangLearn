# Plural and Quantity

> **Source:** Content from [Tofugu](https://www.tofugu.com/japanese-grammar/plural-and-quantity/). For personal study.

In Japanese, whether a noun is plural or singular often doesn't matter. Plurality is expressed through **quantifiers**, **plural suffixes**, and **repetition words** (畳語).

## Nouns Don't Change for Plural

Unlike English, Japanese nouns don't have distinct plural forms:

- リンゴがある。 — There's an apple. / There are apples.
- リンゴが好きです。 — I like apples.
- 日本人はよく風呂に入る。 — Japanese people take baths often.

## Quantifiers

Quantifiers express quantity. They can modify nouns with の or appear before the verb like adverbs:

**With の:**
- ３枚の紙 — three sheets of paper
- たくさんのリンゴ — many apples

**Before the verb:**
- 紙が３枚ある。 — There are three sheets of paper.
- リンゴをたくさん食べた。 — I ate many apples.

### Counters

Counters indicate specific quantities and vary by what you're counting:

- 紙が１枚ある。 — There is one sheet of paper. (〜枚 for flat things)
- 紙が５枚ある。 — There are five sheets of paper.

### Words for Unspecified Quantity

Common words:
- たくさん / 多い — many, much
- 少し / 少ない — a little, few
- 一部 (いちぶ) — partially
- 全部 (ぜんぶ) — all
- いくつか — a few

## Plural Suffixes

Used mainly for living things. Common suffixes:

| Suffix | Usage |
|---|---|
| 〜達 (たち) | Most flexible, common |
| 〜方 (がた) | Formal/respectful |
| 〜等 (ら) | Literary/humble |
| 〜供 (ども) | Humble |

Examples:
- 少年達 — boys
- 私達 — we
- ジェニー達 — Jenny and her companions

Plural suffixes are also used with personal pronouns:
- 私 + 達 = 私達 (we)

## Repetition Words (畳語/じょうご)

Formed by repeating a singular noun, often with the marker 々:

| Word | Reading | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 人々 | ひとびと | people |
| 日々 | ひび | days |
| 我々 | われわれ | we |
| 国々 | くにぐに | countries |
| 山々 | やまやま | mountains |
| 木々 | きぎ | trees |
| 花々 | はなばな | flowers |
| 神々 | かみがみ | gods |

Key rules:
- Only made from **wago** (native Japanese) words
- Never from proper nouns
- Cannot be used with specific counters (❌ 三人の人々)
- Can mean "each/every" with time words: 日々の暮らし (everyday life)
