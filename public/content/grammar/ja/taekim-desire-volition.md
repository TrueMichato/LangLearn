# Expressing Wants & Intentions (たい・ほしい・よう)

Japanese carefully distinguishes between wanting to do something yourself, wanting an object, wanting someone else to do something, and expressing your intentions. Each has its own grammar.

## たい — "I Want to Do"

Attach たい to the verb ます-stem. The result conjugates like an い-adjective.

| Verb | ます-stem | たい form |
|------|---------|----------|
| 食べる | 食べ | 食べたい |
| 行く | 行き | 行きたい |
| 読む | 読み | 読みたい |
| する | し | したい |
| 来る | き | 来たい |

- **新しい映画が見たい。** (Atarashii eiga ga mitai.) — I want to see a new movie.
- **日本に行きたいです。** (Nihon ni ikitai desu.) — I want to go to Japan.
- **今日は何もしたくない。** (Kyou wa nani mo shitakunai.) — I don't want to do anything today.

Because たい is an い-adjective, it conjugates fully: 食べたくない (don't want to eat), 食べたかった (wanted to eat), 食べたくなかった (didn't want to eat).

> 💡 The object particle を can become が with たい: 水**が**飲みたい and 水**を**飲みたい are both correct.

## たい Is First-Person Only

In Japanese, you can't directly state what someone else wants — that would presume to know their inner feelings. Use たがる (shows signs of wanting) for third persons.

- ✅ **私はケーキが食べたい。** — I want to eat cake.
- ❌ ~~彼はケーキが食べたい。~~ (unnatural)
- ✅ **彼はケーキを食べたがっている。** (Kare wa keeki wo tabetagatteiru.) — He seems to want to eat cake.

たがる conjugates as a godan verb: 行きたがる → 行きたがっている (currently showing desire to go).

## ほしい — "I Want a Thing"

ほしい is an い-adjective meaning "wanted/desired." The desired object takes が.

- **新しいパソコンがほしい。** (Atarashii pasokon ga hoshii.) — I want a new computer.
- **もっと時間がほしかった。** (Motto jikan ga hoshikatta.) — I wanted more time.
- **何もほしくない。** (Nani mo hoshikunai.) — I don't want anything.

Like たい, ほしい is first-person only. For third persons, use ほしがる: 彼女は猫をほしがっている (She seems to want a cat).

## てほしい — "I Want Someone to Do Something"

Attach ほしい to the て-form of a verb. The person you want to act takes に.

- **友達に来てほしい。** (Tomodachi ni kite hoshii.) — I want my friend to come.
- **先生に説明してほしい。** (Sensei ni setsumei shite hoshii.) — I want the teacher to explain.
- **もう少し静かにしてほしいです。** (Mou sukoshi shizuka ni shite hoshii desu.) — I'd like you to be a bit quieter.

## Volitional Form — "Let's / I Think I'll"

The volitional form expresses "let's do" or "I think I'll do." It is casual and spontaneous.

**Formation:**

| Type | Rule | Example |
|------|------|---------|
| Ichidan | Drop る, add よう | 食べる → 食べよう |
| Godan | Change last kana to おう-column | 行く → 行こう, 読む → 読もう |
| する | しよう | する → しよう |
| 来る | こよう | 来る → こよう |

- **映画を見よう。** (Eiga wo miyou.) — Let's watch a movie.
- **そろそろ帰ろう。** (Sorosoro kaerou.) — I think I'll head home soon.
- **明日早く起きよう。** (Ashita hayaku okiyou.) — Let's wake up early tomorrow.

The polite volitional uses ましょう: 食べましょう (let's eat), 行きましょう (let's go).

## つもり — "I Intend To"

つもり follows the plain form of a verb to express a firm intention or plan.

- **来年日本に行くつもりです。** (Rainen nihon ni iku tsumori desu.) — I intend to go to Japan next year.
- **大学で医学を勉強するつもりだ。** (Daigaku de igaku wo benkyou suru tsumori da.) — I plan to study medicine at university.
- **あの店には二度と行かないつもりです。** (Ano mise ni wa nido to ikanai tsumori desu.) — I intend to never go to that store again.

> 💡 つもり is stronger than よう. It expresses a decided plan, not a spontaneous suggestion.

## Summary

| Form | Meaning | Person |
|------|---------|--------|
| たい | Want to do | 1st person |
| たがる | Shows desire to do | 3rd person |
| ほしい | Want a thing | 1st person |
| てほしい | Want someone to do | 1st person request |
| よう/おう | Let's / I'll | Spontaneous |
| つもり | Intend to | Decided plan |
