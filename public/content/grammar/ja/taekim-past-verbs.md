# Past Tense Verbs (た form)

In lesson 5, you learned negative verb forms. Now it's time to talk about the past. Japanese forms the past tense by changing the ending of a verb to **た** (or **だ** for certain sound combinations). The rules differ between る-verbs and う-verbs.

## る-verbs (Ichidan): Simple Swap

For る-verbs, drop the final **る** and add **た**. That's it!

| Dictionary | Past | Meaning |
|------------|------|---------|
| 食べる | 食べた | ate |
| 見る | 見た | saw |
| 出る | 出た | went out |

- **昨日、寿司を食べた。** (Kinou, sushi o tabeta.) — I ate sushi yesterday.
- **映画を見た。** (Eiga o mita.) — I watched a movie.

## う-verbs (Godan): Sound Change Rules

う-verbs are trickier. The final kana determines which past ending to use. Here is the complete chart:

| Ending | Change | Example |
|--------|--------|---------|
| す | → した | 話す → 話した (talked) |
| く | → いた | 書く → 書いた (wrote) |
| ぐ | → いだ | 泳ぐ → 泳いだ (swam) |
| む | → んだ | 飲む → 飲んだ (drank) |
| ぶ | → んだ | 遊ぶ → 遊んだ (played) |
| ぬ | → んだ | 死ぬ → 死んだ (died) |
| つ | → った | 待つ → 待った (waited) |
| る | → った | 帰る → 帰った (returned) |
| う | → った | 買う → 買った (bought) |

A helpful way to remember: **す** is its own group, **く/ぐ** use **い** sounds, **む/ぶ/ぬ** become **ん**, and **つ/る/う** double up with **っ**.

- **手紙を書いた。** (Tegami o kaita.) — I wrote a letter.
- **友達と遊んだ。** (Tomodachi to asonda.) — I played with a friend.
- **電車を待った。** (Densha o matta.) — I waited for the train.

## The 行く Exception

The verb **行く** (to go) is the one major exception. You might expect 行いた based on the く rule, but the correct form is:

- **行く → 行った** (itta)

- **学校に行った。** (Gakkou ni itta.) — I went to school.

This is simply a historical irregularity you need to memorize.

## Irregular Verbs

Japanese has only two irregular verbs, and their past forms are straightforward:

| Dictionary | Past | Meaning |
|------------|------|---------|
| する | した | did |
| 来る (くる) | 来た (きた) | came |

- **宿題をした。** (Shukudai o shita.) — I did my homework.
- **友達が来た。** (Tomodachi ga kita.) — A friend came.

Note that the reading of 来 changes from **く** to **き** in the past tense.

## Negative Past Tense

To say something did **not** happen, take the negative form (ない) from lesson 5 and change **ない** to **なかった**:

- 食べない → **食べなかった** (didn't eat)
- 行かない → **行かなかった** (didn't go)

- **昨日、何も食べなかった。** (Kinou, nanimo tabenakatta.) — I didn't eat anything yesterday.
- **パーティーに行かなかった。** (Paatii ni ikanakatta.) — I didn't go to the party.

## Summary

| Type | Present | Past | Negative Past |
|------|---------|------|---------------|
| る-verb | 食べる | 食べた | 食べなかった |
| う-verb (す) | 話す | 話した | 話さなかった |
| う-verb (く) | 書く | 書いた | 書かなかった |
| う-verb (む) | 飲む | 飲んだ | 飲まなかった |
| う-verb (つ) | 待つ | 待った | 待たなかった |
| Irregular | する | した | しなかった |
| Irregular | 来る | 来た | 来なかった |

The past tense sound changes may seem overwhelming, but with practice they become second nature. Focus on the groupings: **した**, **いた/いだ**, **んだ**, and **った**.
