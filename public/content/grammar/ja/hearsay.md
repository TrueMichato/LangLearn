# Hearsay & Appearance (そうだ、ようだ、らしい)

Japanese has several ways to express that something **seems**, **appears**, or is **heard to be** a certain way. Mastering these forms lets you express degrees of certainty and the source of your information.

## Overview

| Expression | Meaning | Based on |
|---|---|---|
| 〜そうだ (hearsay) | I heard that ~ | Information from others |
| 〜そうだ (appearance) | It looks like ~ | Visual impression |
| 〜ようだ / 〜ような | It seems like ~ | Evidence / judgment |
| 〜らしい | Apparently ~ / seems like ~ | Indirect evidence / hearsay |
| 〜みたいだ | It looks like ~ (casual) | Casual version of ようだ |

## そうだ — Hearsay ("I heard that ~")

Attach **そうだ** to the **plain form** of verbs, adjectives, and nouns + だ:

| Base | + そうだ |
|---|---|
| 行(い)く | 行くそうだ |
| 美味(おい)しい | 美味しいそうだ |
| 静(しず)かだ | 静かだそうだ |
| 学生(がくせい)だ | 学生だそうだ |
| 行(い)った (past) | 行ったそうだ |

> 明日(あした)は雨(あめ)が降(ふ)るそうだ。
> *Ashita wa ame ga furu sou da.*
> I heard it will rain tomorrow.

> あの映画(えいが)はとても面白(おもしろ)いそうです。
> *Ano eiga wa totemo omoshiroi sou desu.*
> I heard that movie is very interesting.

> 田中(たなか)さんは来月(らいげつ)結婚(けっこん)するそうだ。
> *Tanaka-san wa raigetsu kekkon suru sou da.*
> I heard Tanaka is getting married next month.

> 💡 Hearsay そうだ reports what you've heard from someone else. You're not making a judgment yourself.

## そうだ — Appearance ("It looks like ~")

This そうだ attaches to the **verb stem** or **adjective stem** (not the plain form):

### Formation

| Type | Base | Stem | + そうだ |
|---|---|---|---|
| Verb | 降(ふ)る | 降り | 降りそうだ |
| Verb | 落(お)ちる | 落ち | 落ちそうだ |
| い-adj | 美味(おい)しい | 美味し | 美味しそうだ |
| い-adj | 高(たか)い | 高 | 高そうだ |
| な-adj | 元気(げんき)だ | 元気 | 元気そうだ |
| な-adj | 暇(ひま)だ | 暇 | 暇そうだ |

**Exceptions:**
- いい (good) → **よさそうだ** (not いさそうだ)
- ない (not exist) → **なさそうだ** (not なそうだ)

> 雨(あめ)が降(ふ)りそうだ。
> *Ame ga furi sou da.*
> It looks like it's going to rain. (looking at the sky)

> このケーキは美味(おい)しそうだ。
> *Kono keeki wa oishi sou da.*
> This cake looks delicious.

> 彼(かれ)は元気(げんき)そうだ。
> *Kare wa genki sou da.*
> He looks healthy / energetic.

> あの荷物(にもつ)は重(おも)そうだ。
> *Ano nimotsu wa omo sou da.*
> That luggage looks heavy.

## Key Difference: Hearsay vs Appearance そうだ

| | Hearsay | Appearance |
|---|---|---|
| Attaches to | Plain form (美味しい) | Stem (美味し) |
| Meaning | I heard it's delicious | It looks delicious |
| Source | Someone told me | My own observation |
| Example | 美味し**い**そうだ | 美味し**∅**そうだ |

> 美味(おい)し**い**そうだ = I **heard** it's delicious (someone told me)
> 美味(おい)し**∅**そうだ = It **looks** delicious (I'm looking at it)

This one-character difference completely changes the meaning!

## ようだ / ような ("It seems like ~")

**ようだ** expresses a judgment based on evidence or observation. It's more objective than そうだ (appearance).

### Formation

| Type | Example |
|---|---|
| Verb (plain) + ようだ | 雨が降るようだ |
| い-adj + ようだ | 寒いようだ |
| な-adj + **な** + ようだ | 静かなようだ |
| Noun + **の** + ようだ | 風邪のようだ |

> 誰(だれ)もいないようだ。
> *Dare mo inai you da.*
> It seems like nobody is here.

> 彼女(かのじょ)は怒(おこ)っているようだ。
> *Kanojo wa okotte iru you da.*
> She seems to be angry.

> 風邪(かぜ)のようです。
> *Kaze no you desu.*
> It seems like a cold.

### ような + Noun (comparison/resemblance)

> 夢(ゆめ)のような話(はなし)だ。
> *Yume no you na hanashi da.*
> It's a dream-like story.

> 子供(こども)のように走(はし)った。
> *Kodomo no you ni hashitta.*
> I ran like a child.

## らしい ("Apparently ~" / "Seems like ~")

**らしい** expresses information based on indirect evidence or what you've heard. It can overlap with both hearsay そうだ and ようだ.

### Formation

Attach **らしい** to the **plain form**:

| Type | Example |
|---|---|
| Verb | 行くらしい |
| い-adj | 寒いらしい |
| な-adj (drop だ) | 静からしい |
| Noun (drop だ) | 学生らしい |
| Past | 行ったらしい |

> 明日(あした)は雪(ゆき)が降(ふ)るらしい。
> *Ashita wa yuki ga furu rashii.*
> Apparently it's going to snow tomorrow.

> 彼(かれ)は会社(かいしゃ)を辞(や)めたらしい。
> *Kare wa kaisha wo yameta rashii.*
> It seems he quit the company.

> あの店(みせ)は有名(ゆうめい)らしい。
> *Ano mise wa yuumei rashii.*
> That store is apparently famous.

### らしい as "typical of ~"

When attached directly to a **noun**, らしい can mean "-like" or "typical of":

> 彼(かれ)は男(おとこ)らしい。
> *Kare wa otoko rashii.*
> He is manly. (typical of a man)

> 今日(きょう)は春(はる)らしい天気(てんき)だ。
> *Kyou wa haru rashii tenki da.*
> Today's weather is spring-like.

> 学生(がくせい)らしくしなさい。
> *Gakusei rashiku shinasai.*
> Behave like a student (should).

## みたいだ (Casual "seems like ~")

**みたいだ** is the casual equivalent of **ようだ**:

### Formation

| Type | Example |
|---|---|
| Verb (plain) + みたいだ | 怒っているみたいだ |
| い-adj + みたいだ | 寒いみたいだ |
| な-adj + みたいだ | 静かみたいだ |
| Noun + みたいだ | 嘘みたいだ |

> 雨(あめ)が止(や)んだみたいだ。
> *Ame ga yanda mitai da.*
> It seems like the rain stopped.

> 嘘(うそ)みたいな話(はなし)だ。
> *Uso mitai na hanashi da.*
> It's an unbelievable story. (Lit: a lie-like story)

## Comparison Chart

| Form | Source of info | Certainty | Formality | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| そうだ (hearsay) | Someone told me | Neutral | Neutral | 降るそうだ |
| そうだ (appearance) | Looking at it | Moderate | Neutral | 降りそうだ |
| ようだ | Evidence/judgment | Moderate | Formal | 降るようだ |
| らしい | Indirect evidence | Lower | Neutral | 降るらしい |
| みたいだ | Evidence/judgment | Moderate | Casual | 降るみたいだ |

## Putting It All Together

Imagine dark clouds are gathering and a friend told you about the forecast:

> 雨(あめ)が降(ふ)り**そうだ**。 — It looks like rain. (I see the clouds)
> 雨(あめ)が降(ふ)る**そうだ**。 — I heard it will rain. (someone told me)
> 雨(あめ)が降(ふ)る**ようだ**。 — It seems it will rain. (based on evidence)
> 雨(あめ)が降(ふ)る**らしい**。 — Apparently it will rain. (I heard/it seems)
> 雨(あめ)が降(ふ)る**みたいだ**。 — Looks like it'll rain. (casual observation)

## Summary

| Expression | Key point |
|---|---|
| 〜そうだ (plain form +) | Hearsay: "I heard ~" |
| 〜そうだ (stem +) | Appearance: "It looks ~" |
| 〜ようだ | Evidence-based: "It seems ~" (formal) |
| 〜らしい | Indirect evidence: "Apparently ~" |
| 〜みたいだ | Casual: "It seems ~" |

These expressions are essential for nuanced communication. They let you distinguish between what you observe, what you infer, and what you've been told — distinctions that Japanese speakers make regularly.
