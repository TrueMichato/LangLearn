# Verb Conjugation Groups (Godan/Ichidan/Irregular)

> **Source:** Content from [Tofugu](https://www.tofugu.com/japanese-grammar/verb-conjugation-groups/). For personal study.

Japanese verbs come in three types: **godan verbs** (五段動詞), **ichidan verbs** (一段動詞), and **irregular verbs** (変格動詞). Understanding verb types is essential because each type requires a different method of attaching conjugational verb endings.

## Godan Verbs (五段動詞)

Godan verbs are also called う-verbs, Group I verbs, or consonant-root verbs. Examples include:

- 読む (よむ) — to read
- 書く (かく) — to write
- 話す (はなす) — to speak
- 聞く (きく) — to listen

As these verbs conjugate, the final う-line character shifts through the five vowel sounds (あ, い, う, え, お). For example, with 聞く:

| Vowel Line | Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|---|
| あ /a/ | 聞**か**ない | ki**ka**nai | not listen |
| い /i/ | 聞**き**ます | ki**ki**masu | listen (polite) |
| う /u/ | 聞**く** | ki**ku** | listen (plain) |
| え /e/ | 聞**け**る | ki**ke**ru | can listen |
| お /o/ | 聞**こ**う | ki**ko**u | let's listen |

The root of a godan verb ends in a **consonant** — hence the alternate name "consonant-root verbs."

## Ichidan Verbs (一段動詞)

Ichidan verbs are also called る-verbs, Group II verbs, or vowel-root verbs. Examples include:

- 見る (みる) — to see
- 起きる (おきる) — to wake up
- 開ける (あける) — to open
- 食べる (たべる) — to eat

Conjugating these verbs is straightforward — replace る with the new ending. The character before る stays the same:

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| 食べない | tabenai | not eat |
| 食べます | tabemasu | eat (polite) |
| 食べる | taberu | eat (plain) |
| 食べられる | taberareru | can eat |
| 食べよう | tabeyou | let's eat |

The root ends in a **vowel** — hence "vowel-root verbs."

## Godan Verbs Disguised as Ichidan

Some godan verbs end in る and look like ichidan verbs. The trick:

- If the vowel before る is **/a/**, **/u/**, or **/o/** → **definitely godan**
- If the vowel before る is **/e/** or **/i/** → **probably ichidan** (but exceptions exist)

Common exceptions (godan verbs ending in /iru/ or /eru/): いる (to need), 入る (to enter), 走る (to run), 帰る (to return), 減る (to decrease), 喋る (to chat).

## Irregular Verbs (変格動詞)

Only two verbs are irregular: **する** (to do) and **来る (くる)** (to come). These are so common that you'll master them through practice.

## 音便 (Sound Convenience) in Conjugations

Sound changes occur in the て form and past tense for easier pronunciation:

### Double Consonant (っ)
When verb roots end in /w/, /t/, or /r/:
- 買う → 買って (not 買いて)
- 持つ → 持って (not 持ちて)

### Assimilation (ん)
When verb roots end in /m/, /n/, or /b/:
- 飲む → 飲んで (not 飲みて)
- 死ぬ → 死んで (not 死にて)

### Consonant Removal
When verb roots end in /k/ or /g/:
- 聞く → 聞いて (not 聞きて)
- 急ぐ → 急いで (not 急ぎて)
