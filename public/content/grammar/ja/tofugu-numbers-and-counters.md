# Numbers and Counters

> **Source:** Content from [Tofugu](https://www.tofugu.com/japanese-grammar/numbers-and-counters/). For personal study.

Japanese has two counting systems — **wago** (native Japanese) and **kango** (Sino-Japanese). Numbers are followed by **counters** (助数詞/じょすうし) that classify what is being counted.

## Counting Numbers

### Wago vs. Kango (1–10)

| Number | Kanji | Wago | Kango |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 一 | ひとつ | いち |
| 2 | 二 | ふたつ | に |
| 3 | 三 | みっつ | さん |
| 4 | 四 | よっつ | し/よん |
| 5 | 五 | いつつ | ご |
| 6 | 六 | むっつ | ろく |
| 7 | 七 | ななつ | なな/しち |
| 8 | 八 | やっつ | はち |
| 9 | 九 | ここのつ | きゅう |
| 10 | 十 | とお | じゅう |

For numbers above 10, use kango by combining: 11 = じゅういち, 99 = きゅうじゅうきゅう.

New numbers: 100 = 百 (ひゃく), 1000 = 千 (せん), 10000 = 万 (まん).

## What Are Counters?

Counters are suffixes that classify what you're counting. You can't just say "two pens" directly — you need the appropriate counter:

- ペンを **二本（にほん）** 買う。 — I'll buy **two pens**. (本 = long objects)
- 本を **二冊（にさつ）** 買う。 — I'll buy **two books**. (冊 = bound volumes)

### 〜つ / 〜個: Universal Counters

**〜つ** (wago, works 1–10) and **〜個 (こ)** (kango, any number) are wild cards for counting most things:

- ペンを **二つ（ふたつ）** 買う。 — I'll buy two pens.
- ミカンを **五つ（いつつ）** 食べた。 — I ate five oranges.
- レンガが **六個（ろっこ）** ある。 — There are six bricks.

### 〜人: Counter for People

Special readings for 1 and 2:
- 一人 = ひとり, 二人 = ふたり
- Then kango: 三人 (さんにん), 四人 (よにん), etc.

### 〜匹 / 〜頭: Counters for Animals

- 〜匹 (ひき) — small to medium animals: ネコが **二匹（にひき）** いる。
- 〜頭 (とう) — large animals: ゾウが **三頭（さんとう）** いる。

## Sentence Patterns with Counters

**Pattern 1:** Counter before the verb (most common)
- ネコが **二匹（にひき）** いる。 — There are two cats.

**Pattern 2:** Counter + の + Noun (treats items as a set)
- **二匹（にひき）の** ネコがいる。 — (The) two cats are there.

The difference is subtle: Pattern 1 = "cats, two of them happen to be there." Pattern 2 = "the specific set of two cats."
