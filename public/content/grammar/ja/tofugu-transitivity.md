# Transitive & Intransitive Verbs

> **Source:** Content from [Tofugu](https://www.tofugu.com/japanese-grammar/transitivity/). For personal study.

In Japanese, verbs are categorized as **transitive** (他動詞/たどうし — "other verb") or **intransitive** (自動詞/じどうし — "self verb"). This distinction is fundamental to understanding particle usage and sentence structure.

## What Is Transitivity?

A **transitive verb** involves the transfer of energy from a subject to an object — one entity acting upon another:

- ジェニーがドアを**開けた**。 — Jenny **opened** the door.

An **intransitive verb** expresses a change in the subject itself, with no transfer of energy:

- ドアが**開いた**。 — The door **opened**.

## Transitivity Pairs

Many Japanese verbs come in pairs sharing the same kanji root but with different endings:

| Transitive (他動詞) | Intransitive (自動詞) |
|---|---|
| 開ける (あける) — to open (something) | 開く (あく) — to open (by itself) |
| 始める (はじめる) — to start (something) | 始まる (はじまる) — to begin |
| つける — to turn on | つく — to come on |
| 壊す (こわす) — to break (something) | 壊れる (こわれる) — to break |

## Particles を and が

As a general pattern:

- **Transitive verbs** take an object marked with **を**
- **Intransitive verbs** take a subject marked with **が**

Examples:
- キャメロンが電気**を**つけた。 — Cameron turned on the lights.
- 電気**が**つきました。 — The lights came on.

However, subjects and objects are frequently omitted when clear from context.

## English vs. Japanese Transitivity

Some verbs that are transitive in English are **intransitive** in Japanese:

- ジェニーはフランス語**が**分かる。 — Jenny understands French.

Here, フランス語 takes が (not を) because French itself isn't affected by whether Jenny understands it.

Other examples:
- かなえはフランス語の教科書**が**あります。 — Kanae has a French textbook.
- まみはカナダ人の友達**が**いる。 — Mami has Canadian friends.

## Intransitive Verbs with を

Some intransitive verbs can take を in special cases:

### Verbs with Intention
When intention is involved, を becomes possible even with typically intransitive verbs:
- かなえがフランス語**を**分かるようになった。 — Kanae became able to understand French (through effort).

### Movement Verbs
Motion verbs use を to mark the **path** of movement:
- キャメロンは熊野古道**を**歩いた。 — Cameron walked the Kumano Pilgrimage.
- ジェニーはイギリス海峡**を**泳いだ。 — Jenny swam the English Channel.
