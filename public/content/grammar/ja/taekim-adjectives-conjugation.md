# Adjective Conjugation System — い and な Adjectives

Japanese adjectives are not mere descriptors — they **conjugate** like verbs. There are two types: **い-adjectives** (native Japanese adjectives) and **な-adjectives** (often Sino-Japanese or borrowed words). Each follows its own conjugation rules. Understanding these two systems is essential for building natural sentences.

## い-Adjectives

い-adjectives always end in **い** in their dictionary form. To conjugate them, you modify the ending い directly — no need for だ.

**Examples of い-adjectives:** 高い (takai, tall/expensive), 安い (yasui, cheap), 新しい (atarashii, new), 面白い (omoshiroi, interesting)

### Conjugation Rules

| Form | Rule | Example with 高い |
|------|------|-------------------|
| Affirmative | As-is | 高**い** (is tall) |
| Negative | Drop い → **くない** | 高**くない** (is not tall) |
| Past | Drop い → **かった** | 高**かった** (was tall) |
| Negative past | Drop い → **くなかった** | 高**くなかった** (was not tall) |

- **この本は面白い。** (Kono hon wa omoshiroi.) — This book is interesting.
- **あの映画は面白くない。** (Ano eiga wa omoshiroku nai.) — That movie is not interesting.
- **昨日は暑かった。** (Kinō wa atsukatta.) — Yesterday was hot.
- **テストは難しくなかった。** (Tesuto wa muzukashiku nakatta.) — The test was not difficult.

> **Important:** Notice that い-adjectives do NOT use だ. Saying 高いだ is incorrect. The い ending itself acts as the "is."

## な-Adjectives

な-adjectives look like nouns and behave like them too. When placed before a noun, they attach **な** (hence the name). For conjugation, they follow the same **だ system** you learned in the state-of-being lesson.

**Examples of な-adjectives:** 静か (shizuka, quiet), 元気 (genki, healthy), 好き (suki, liked), 有名 (yuumei, famous)

### Conjugation Rules

| Form | Rule | Example with 静か |
|------|------|-------------------|
| Affirmative | + **だ** | 静か**だ** (is quiet) |
| Negative | + **じゃない** | 静か**じゃない** (is not quiet) |
| Past | + **だった** | 静か**だった** (was quiet) |
| Negative past | + **じゃなかった** | 静か**じゃなかった** (was not quiet) |

- **この町は静かだ。** (Kono machi wa shizuka da.) — This town is quiet.
- **彼は有名じゃない。** (Kare wa yuumei ja nai.) — He is not famous.
- **昔ここは静かだった。** (Mukashi koko wa shizuka datta.) — Long ago this place was quiet.
- **あの店は便利じゃなかった。** (Ano mise wa benri ja nakatta.) — That shop was not convenient.

## Modifying Nouns

When adjectives appear **before a noun**, the attachment differs by type:

- **い-adjective:** attach directly → 高**い**ビル (takai biru — tall building)
- **な-adjective:** attach with な → 静か**な**町 (shizuka na machi — quiet town)

- **新しい車が欲しい。** (Atarashii kuruma ga hoshii.) — (I) want a new car.
- **有名な人に会った。** (Yuumei na hito ni atta.) — (I) met a famous person.

## The いい Exception

The adjective **いい** (good) is special. Its original form is **よい**, and ALL conjugations use the よ- base:

| Form | Conjugation |
|------|-------------|
| Affirmative | **いい** (not よい in modern speech) |
| Negative | **よくない** |
| Past | **よかった** |
| Negative past | **よくなかった** |

- **天気がいい。** (Tenki ga ii.) — The weather is good.
- **結果はよくなかった。** (Kekka wa yoku nakatta.) — The result was not good.
- **昨日の試合はよかった。** (Kinō no shiai wa yokatta.) — Yesterday's game was good.

> Common mistake: saying いくない or いかった — always use the よ- base for all forms except the plain affirmative.

The adjective **かっこいい** (cool/good-looking) follows the same pattern: かっこよくない, かっこよかった, かっこよくなかった.

## Summary

| | い-adjective | な-adjective |
|---|---|---|
| Dictionary form | 高い | 静か |
| Negative | 高くない | 静かじゃない |
| Past | 高かった | 静かだった |
| Neg. past | 高くなかった | 静かじゃなかった |
| Before nouns | 高い + noun | 静かな + noun |

Remember: い-adjectives conjugate by modifying the い ending; な-adjectives use the だ system. Keeping these two systems separate in your mind is the key to correct adjective usage.
