# Passive Voice (食べられる)

The passive voice in Japanese is used more frequently than in English. Beyond describing actions done to things, Japanese has a unique "suffering passive" that expresses being negatively affected by someone's action.

## Formation

### Group 1 (u-verbs)

Change the final **u-sound** to the **a-sound** and add **れる**:

| Dictionary | Passive |
|-----------|---------|
| 書(か)く | 書かれる |
| 読(よ)む | 読まれる |
| 話(はな)す | 話される |
| 飲(の)む | 飲まれる |
| 取(と)る | 取られる |
| 踏(ふ)む | 踏まれる |
| 呼(よ)ぶ | 呼ばれる |
| 待(ま)つ | 待たれる |

### Group 2 (ru-verbs)

Drop the final **る** and add **られる**:

| Dictionary | Passive |
|-----------|---------|
| 食(た)べる | 食べられる |
| 見(み)る | 見られる |
| 褒(ほ)める | 褒められる |
| 叱(しか)る | 叱られる |
| 開(あ)ける | 開けられる |

### Group 3 (Irregular)

| Dictionary | Passive |
|-----------|---------|
| する | **される** |
| 来(く)る | **来(こ)られる** |

> 💡 For Group 2 verbs, the passive form is identical to the potential form (食べられる). Context tells you which meaning is intended.

## Direct Passive

The direct passive describes an action performed on the subject by someone else. This is closest to the English passive voice.

**Pattern:** [receiver]は [doer]に [verb passive]

- **この本は多(おお)くの人に読まれています。** — This book is read by many people.
- **この歌(うた)は世界中(せかいじゅう)で歌(うた)われています。** — This song is sung around the world.
- **彼(かれ)はみんなに尊敬(そんけい)されています。** — He is respected by everyone.
- **この建物(たてもの)は１００年前(ねんまえ)に建(た)てられました。** — This building was built 100 years ago.
- **私は先生に褒められました。** — I was praised by the teacher.

### Common direct passive examples

- **日本語は多くの国(くに)で勉強(べんきょう)されています。** — Japanese is studied in many countries.
- **この映画は去年(きょねん)作(つく)られました。** — This movie was made last year.
- **会議(かいぎ)は来週(らいしゅう)行(おこな)われます。** — The meeting will be held next week.
- **新しいルールが発表(はっぴょう)されました。** — New rules were announced.
- **彼女は社長(しゃちょう)に選(えら)ばれました。** — She was chosen as company president.

## Indirect Passive (Suffering Passive)

This is unique to Japanese. The indirect passive expresses that someone is **negatively affected** by another person's action, even if the action is not directly done to them.

**Pattern:** [affected person]は [doer]に [verb passive]

- **電車(でんしゃ)で足(あし)を踏まれました。** — Someone stepped on my foot on the train.
- **雨に降(ふ)られました。** — I got rained on. (The rain "affected" me.)
- **隣(となり)の人にたばこを吸(す)われました。** — The person next to me smoked (and it bothered me).
- **彼女に泣(な)かれました。** — She cried on me. (Her crying affected me negatively.)
- **子供(こども)に夜中(よなか)に起(お)きられました。** — The child woke up in the middle of the night (and it was a problem for me).

> 💡 The suffering passive has no equivalent in English. The nuance is always that the subject was inconvenienced or troubled by the action.

### More suffering passive examples

- **友達(ともだち)に先(さき)に帰(かえ)られました。** — My friend went home first (leaving me behind).
- **犬に靴(くつ)を噛(か)まれました。** — A dog bit my shoes.
- **母(はは)に日記(にっき)を読まれました。** — My mother read my diary (without permission).
- **隣の部屋(へや)の人に夜中(よなか)に音楽(おんがく)を聞(き)かれました。** — The person next door played music at night (bothering me).
- **パーティーでワインをこぼされました。** — Someone spilled wine (on me) at the party.

## Passive with に and によって

- **に** marks the doer in most passive sentences (especially with animate doers):
  - **犬に噛まれた。** — I was bitten by a dog.

- **によって** is used for more formal/written contexts or when describing creation:
  - **この小説(しょうせつ)は夏目漱石(なつめそうせき)によって書かれました。** — This novel was written by Natsume Soseki.
  - **新しい法律(ほうりつ)は国会(こっかい)によって決(き)められました。** — The new law was decided by the parliament.

## Passive in Formal Speech

The passive voice is frequently used in formal Japanese, news, and business:

- **会議は３時に開(ひら)かれます。** — The meeting will be held at 3 o'clock.
- **新商品(しんしょうひん)が発売(はつばい)されました。** — A new product was released.
- **この問題(もんだい)は調査(ちょうさ)されています。** — This problem is being investigated.
- **結果(けっか)が発表されました。** — The results were announced.
- **この計画(けいかく)は承認(しょうにん)されました。** — This plan was approved.

## Conjugating the Passive

The passive form behaves like a Group 2 verb:

| Form | 読まれる |
|------|---------|
| Present polite | 読まれます |
| Negative | 読まれない / 読まれません |
| Past | 読まれた / 読まれました |
| Past negative | 読まれなかった / 読まれませんでした |
| て-form | 読まれて |

- **まだ結果が発表されていません。** — The results haven't been announced yet.
- **昨日(きのう)先生に叱られなかった。** — I wasn't scolded by the teacher yesterday.
- **この本は100年前に書かれて、今でも読まれています。** — This book was written 100 years ago and is still read today.

## Direct vs Indirect Passive Summary

| Type | The subject is... | Nuance | Example |
|------|------------------|--------|---------|
| Direct | Directly acted upon | Neutral | 先生に褒められた (praised by teacher) |
| Indirect | Negatively affected | Annoyed/troubled | 雨に降られた (got rained on) |

## Practice Tips

- In conversation, the suffering passive is very common — listen for it!
- In formal writing and news, direct passive is used heavily.
- Remember: the doer is marked with に, the affected person is the topic/subject.
- Pay attention to context to distinguish passive from potential in Group 2 verbs.
