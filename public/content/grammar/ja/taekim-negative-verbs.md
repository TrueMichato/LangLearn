# Negative Verb Conjugation — The ない Form

Now that you know how to classify verbs (from the previous lesson), it is time to learn your first conjugation: the **negative form**. This tells you how to say "do not" do something. Each verb type follows its own rule.

## る-Verbs: Drop る, Add ない

る-verb negation is straightforward. Remove the final **る** and attach **ない**.

| Dictionary form | Negative form | Meaning |
|----------------|---------------|---------|
| 食べる (taberu) | 食べ**ない** | do not eat |
| 見る (miru) | 見**ない** | do not see |
| 起きる (okiru) | 起き**ない** | do not wake up |
| 寝る (neru) | 寝**ない** | do not sleep |
| 出る (deru) | 出**ない** | do not go out |

- **朝ご飯を食べない。** (Asagohan o tabenai.) — (I) don't eat breakfast.
- **テレビを見ない。** (Terebi o minai.) — (I) don't watch TV.

## う-Verbs: Change to あ-Row + ない

For う-verbs, take the last kana and shift it to the corresponding **あ-row** sound in the same column of the kana chart, then add **ない**.

| Column | う-row → あ-row | Example |
|--------|----------------|---------|
| か-column | く → か | 書く → 書か**ない** |
| さ-column | す → さ | 話す → 話さ**ない** |
| た-column | つ → た | 待つ → 待た**ない** |
| な-column | ぬ → な | 死ぬ → 死な**ない** |
| ま-column | む → ま | 飲む → 飲ま**ない** |
| ば-column | ぶ → ば | 遊ぶ → 遊ば**ない** |
| ら-column | る → ら | 帰る → 帰ら**ない** |
| が-column | ぐ → が | 泳ぐ → 泳が**ない** |

- **コーヒーを飲まない。** (Kōhii o nomanai.) — (I) don't drink coffee.
- **手紙を書かない。** (Tegami o kakanai.) — (I) don't write letters.
- **今日は泳がない。** (Kyō wa oyoganai.) — (I) won't swim today.

## Special Case: う Ending → わない

Verbs ending in **う** are a special case. Instead of changing う to あ (which would produce the same vowel), the negative uses **わ** + ない.

| Dictionary form | Negative form | Meaning |
|----------------|---------------|---------|
| 買う (kau) | 買**わない** | do not buy |
| 会う (au) | 会**わない** | do not meet |
| 使う (tsukau) | 使**わない** | do not use |
| 言う (iu) | 言**わない** | do not say |

- **新しい服を買わない。** (Atarashii fuku o kawanai.) — (I) won't buy new clothes.
- **明日は友達に会わない。** (Ashita wa tomodachi ni awanai.) — (I) won't meet friends tomorrow.

> This is the one part of う-verb negation you need to memorize as an exception: う → わない, not う → あない.

## Irregular Verbs

The two irregular verbs have unique negative forms that must be memorized:

| Dictionary form | Negative form | Meaning |
|----------------|---------------|---------|
| する (suru) | **しない** | do not do |
| 来る (kuru) | **来ない** (こない) | do not come |

- **今日は勉強しない。** (Kyō wa benkyō shinai.) — (I) won't study today.
- **明日は来ない。** (Ashita wa konai.) — (He) won't come tomorrow.

Note that 来ない is read as **こない**, not くない. The kanji stays the same but the reading changes.

## Special: ある → ない

The verb **ある** (to exist, for inanimate objects) has a completely unique negative: it simply becomes **ない** — with no stem remaining.

- **時間がある。** (Jikan ga aru.) — There is time.
- **時間がない。** (Jikan ga nai.) — There is no time.

This is the only verb where the negative form has no visible connection to the original. But since ない itself is such a common word, you will learn it naturally.

## Complete Conjugation Chart

| Type | Rule | Example |
|------|------|---------|
| る-verb | Drop る → ない | 食べる → 食べない |
| う-verb | う-row → あ-row + ない | 書く → 書かない |
| う ending | う → わない | 買う → 買わない |
| する | → しない | する → しない |
| 来る | → こない | 来る → 来ない |
| ある | → ない | ある → ない |

Practice by taking verbs you already know and converting them to the negative. The pattern will become second nature quickly!
