# Giving & Receiving (あげる、もらう、くれる)

Japanese has three main verbs for giving and receiving. The correct choice depends on **who is giving**, **who is receiving**, and **your perspective** as the speaker. This system reflects the importance of social relationships in Japanese culture.

## Overview of the Three Verbs

| Verb | Meaning | Direction |
|------|---------|-----------|
| あげる | to give | I/someone → someone else |
| もらう | to receive | I/someone ← someone else |
| くれる | to give (to me) | Someone → me/my group |

Think of it this way:
- **あげる** = giving away (outward)
- **くれる** = giving toward me (inward)
- **もらう** = receiving

## あげる — To Give

Use **あげる** when you (or someone) give something to another person. The giver is the subject.

**Pattern:** [giver]は [receiver]に [thing]を あげる

- **私は友達(ともだち)にプレゼントをあげました。** — I gave my friend a present.
- **田中(たなか)さんは山田(やまだ)さんに本をあげました。** — Tanaka gave Yamada a book.
- **母(はは)は隣(となり)の人(ひと)にケーキをあげました。** — My mother gave the neighbor a cake.
- **弟(おとうと)に古(ふる)いゲームをあげました。** — I gave my little brother an old game.
- **彼女(かのじょ)に花(はな)をあげたいです。** — I want to give her flowers.

> ⚠️ Do not use あげる when someone gives something **to you**. That would sound unnatural.

## くれる — To Give (to Me / My Group)

Use **くれる** when someone gives something **to you** or someone in your in-group (family, close friends). The giver is the subject.

**Pattern:** [giver]は (私に) [thing]を くれる

- **友達は私に本をくれました。** — My friend gave me a book.
- **先生(せんせい)がアドバイスをくれました。** — The teacher gave me advice.
- **母が弟に新しい靴(くつ)をくれました。** — Mom gave my little brother new shoes.
- **隣の人がお菓子(かし)をくれました。** — The neighbor gave me sweets.
- **彼(かれ)が誕生日(たんじょうび)プレゼントをくれました。** — He gave me a birthday present.

> 💡 くれる implies gratitude — the speaker feels thankful for the giving.

## あげる vs くれる

The key difference is **perspective**:

- **私は田中さんにペンをあげました。** — I gave Tanaka a pen. (I → Tanaka)
- **田中さんは私にペンをくれました。** — Tanaka gave me a pen. (Tanaka → me)

Both describe giving, but:
- あげる = the giving goes **away** from the speaker
- くれる = the giving comes **toward** the speaker

- **山田さんは佐藤(さとう)さんにお土産(みやげ)をあげました。** — Yamada gave Sato a souvenir. (neutral third parties)
- **山田さんは妹(いもうと)にお土産をくれました。** — Yamada gave my sister a souvenir. (toward my group)

## もらう — To Receive

Use **もらう** when you (or someone) receive something from another person. The receiver is the subject.

**Pattern:** [receiver]は [giver]に/から [thing]を もらう

- **私は友達にプレゼントをもらいました。** — I received a present from my friend.
- **田中さんは先生に本をもらいました。** — Tanaka received a book from the teacher.
- **誕生日にたくさんのカードをもらいました。** — I received many cards on my birthday.
- **彼女から手紙(てがみ)をもらいました。** — I received a letter from her.
- **会社(かいしゃ)からボーナスをもらいました。** — I received a bonus from the company.

> 💡 You can use either に or から to mark the giver. から emphasizes the source.

## くれる vs もらう — Same Event, Different Focus

The same event can often be expressed with either くれる or もらう:

- **友達が本をくれた。** — My friend gave me a book. (focus on the giver's action)
- **友達に本をもらった。** — I got a book from my friend. (focus on my receiving)

- **母がお金(かね)をくれました。** — Mom gave me money.
- **母にお金をもらいました。** — I got money from Mom.

- **先生が推薦状(すいせんじょう)をくれました。** — The teacher gave me a recommendation letter.
- **先生に推薦状をもらいました。** — I received a recommendation letter from the teacher.

Both are correct — choose based on what you want to emphasize.

## て-Form Combinations (Giving & Receiving Favors)

These verbs combine with the **て-form** of other verbs to express doing favors.

### 〜てあげる — Do something for someone

- **友達に日本語を教(おし)えてあげました。** — I taught Japanese to my friend (as a favor).
- **荷物(にもつ)を持(も)ってあげましょうか。** — Shall I carry your luggage (for you)?
- **妹に料理を作(つく)ってあげました。** — I cooked for my little sister.
- **道(みち)を案内(あんない)してあげました。** — I showed them the way.
- **写真(しゃしん)を撮(と)ってあげますよ。** — I'll take a photo for you.

### 〜てくれる — Someone does something for me

- **友達が宿題(しゅくだい)を手伝(てつだ)ってくれました。** — My friend helped me with homework.
- **母が弁当(べんとう)を作ってくれました。** — Mom made me a bento.
- **先生が説明(せつめい)してくれました。** — The teacher explained it for me.
- **彼が駅(えき)まで送(おく)ってくれました。** — He drove me to the station.
- **隣の人が道を教えてくれました。** — The neighbor told me the way.

### 〜てもらう — Have someone do something for me

- **友達に手伝ってもらいました。** — I had my friend help me.
- **先生に推薦状を書(か)いてもらいました。** — I had the teacher write a recommendation.
- **美容師(びようし)に髪(かみ)を切(き)ってもらいました。** — I had the hairdresser cut my hair.
- **日本語をチェックしてもらえますか。** — Could you check my Japanese?
- **友達に空港(くうこう)まで送ってもらいました。** — I had my friend take me to the airport.

## Polite / Honorific Versions

| Casual | Polite/Honorific | Usage |
|--------|-----------------|-------|
| あげる | さしあげる | Humble — I give to a superior |
| くれる | くださる | Honorific — a superior gives to me |
| もらう | いただく | Humble — I receive from a superior |

- **先生がアドバイスをくださいました。** — The teacher kindly gave me advice.
- **社長(しゃちょう)にお時間(じかん)をいただきました。** — I received time from the company president.
- **先生にプレゼントをさしあげました。** — I gave the teacher a present. (humble)

## Summary

| Pattern | Who acts | Direction | Example |
|---------|---------|-----------|---------|
| あげる | Giver | Away from speaker | 私が友達にあげる |
| くれる | Giver | Toward speaker | 友達が私にくれる |
| もらう | Receiver | Receiving | 私が友達にもらう |
| 〜てあげる | I do for someone | Favor outward | 教えてあげる |
| 〜てくれる | Someone does for me | Favor toward me | 教えてくれる |
| 〜てもらう | I have someone do | Request/favor received | 教えてもらう |

Mastering these three verbs is essential for natural Japanese — they come up in almost every daily conversation!
