# Connecting Sentences (Compound Sentences)

In natural speech, you rarely say one short sentence at a time. Japanese has several tools for linking clauses together — each with a different nuance. This lesson covers the most essential connectors.

## て-Form as a Connector

The て-form links sequential actions: "I did X, and then Y." Events are understood in chronological order.

- **朝起きて、顔を洗った。** (Asa okite, kao wo aratta.) — I woke up in the morning and washed my face.
- **駅まで歩いて、電車に乗った。** (Eki made aruite, densha ni notta.) — I walked to the station and got on the train.
- **コーヒーを買って、公園で飲んだ。** (Koohii wo katte, kouen de nonda.) — I bought coffee and drank it in the park.

The て-form can also imply cause: 風邪をひいて、学校を休んだ (I caught a cold and stayed home from school).

## から and ので — "Because"

Both から and ので express a reason, but they differ in tone.

**から** is straightforward and casual. It states a reason directly.

- **暑いから、窓を開けた。** (Atsui kara, mado wo aketa.) — Because it's hot, I opened the window.
- **時間がないから、タクシーに乗ろう。** (Jikan ga nai kara, takushii ni norou.) — Since there's no time, let's take a taxi.

**ので** is softer and more polite. It presents the reason as a natural explanation rather than a blunt statement.

- **雨が降っているので、傘を持っていきます。** (Ame ga futteiru node, kasa wo motteikimasu.) — Since it's raining, I'll bring an umbrella.
- **明日テストがあるので、今日は勉強します。** (Ashita tesuto ga aru node, kyou wa benkyou shimasu.) — Because there's a test tomorrow, I'll study today.

> 💡 Use ので in polite or professional situations. Use から with friends or when being emphatic.

## けど / けれども — "But / However"

けど (casual) and けれども (formal) connect contrasting clauses, similar to English "but."

- **日本語は難しいけど、面白い。** (Nihongo wa muzukashii kedo, omoshiroi.) — Japanese is difficult, but interesting.
- **行きたいけれども、時間がありません。** (Ikitai keredomo, jikan ga arimasen.) — I'd like to go, but I don't have time.
- **高いけど、買った。** (Takai kedo, katta.) — It was expensive, but I bought it.

The formality spectrum: けど → けども → けれど → けれども (most formal).

## し — Listing Reasons

し lists multiple reasons or qualities. It feels like "for one thing… and also…"

- **この店は安いし、おいしいし、好きです。** (Kono mise wa yasui shi, oishii shi, suki desu.) — This restaurant is cheap, and delicious, and I like it.
- **疲れたし、お腹も空いたし、帰ろう。** (Tsukareta shi, onaka mo suita shi, kaerou.) — I'm tired, and hungry too, so let's go home.
- **彼は優しいし、頭もいい。** (Kare wa yasashii shi, atama mo ii.) — He's kind, and smart too.

## のに — "Despite / Even Though"

のに expresses disappointment or surprise that reality doesn't match expectations.

- **薬を飲んだのに、まだ頭が痛い。** (Kusuri wo nonda noni, mada atama ga itai.) — Even though I took medicine, my head still hurts.
- **約束したのに、来なかった。** (Yakusoku shita noni, konakatta.) — Despite promising, they didn't come.
- **一生懸命勉強したのに、テストに落ちた。** (Isshoukenmei benkyou shita noni, tesuto ni ochita.) — Even though I studied hard, I failed the test.

> 💡 Unlike けど, のに carries emotional weight — frustration, regret, or surprise.

## が — Formal "But"

が after a clause works as a formal, written connector meaning "but" or "however." This is different from the subject-marking particle が.

- **申し訳ございませんが、少々お待ちください。** (Moushiwake gozaimasen ga, shoushou omachi kudasai.) — I'm sorry, but please wait a moment.
- **お忙しいところ恐れ入りますが、お時間をいただけますか。** (Oisogashii tokoro osoreirimasu ga, ojikan wo itadakemasu ka.) — I'm sorry to bother you while you're busy, but could I have a moment of your time?

This が is common in polite requests and business Japanese.

## Summary

| Connector | Meaning | Tone |
|-----------|---------|------|
| て-form | and then / and so | Neutral, sequential |
| から | because | Direct, casual |
| ので | because | Soft, polite |
| けど | but | Casual |
| し | and also (reasons) | Listing |
| のに | despite / even though | Disappointed |
| が | but / however | Formal, written |
