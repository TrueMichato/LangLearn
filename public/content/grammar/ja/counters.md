# Counting & Counters (一つ、一人、一匹)

In Japanese, you cannot just use a number by itself to count things. You must pair numbers with special **counter words** (助数詞/じょすうし) that match the type of object you are counting. This lesson covers the most common counters you will need.

## Numbers Review

Before learning counters, make sure you know the basic numbers:

| Number | Japanese | Reading |
|--------|---------|---------|
| 1 | 一 | いち |
| 2 | 二 | に |
| 3 | 三 | さん |
| 4 | 四 | し / よん |
| 5 | 五 | ご |
| 6 | 六 | ろく |
| 7 | 七 | しち / なな |
| 8 | 八 | はち |
| 9 | 九 | きゅう / く |
| 10 | 十 | じゅう |

> 💡 Numbers 4, 7, and 9 have two readings each. The preferred reading often depends on the counter used.

## つ Counter — General Objects

The **つ** counter uses native Japanese numbers (ひとつ, ふたつ, etc.) and works for almost any object when you do not know the specific counter. It only goes up to 10.

| Count | Japanese | Reading |
|-------|---------|---------|
| 1 | 一つ | ひとつ |
| 2 | 二つ | ふたつ |
| 3 | 三つ | みっつ |
| 4 | 四つ | よっつ |
| 5 | 五つ | いつつ |
| 6 | 六つ | むっつ |
| 7 | 七つ | ななつ |
| 8 | 八つ | やっつ |
| 9 | 九つ | ここのつ |
| 10 | 十 | とお |

- **りんごを三つください。** — Three apples, please.
- **問題(もんだい)が二つあります。** — There are two problems.
- **理由(りゆう)は一つです。** — There is one reason.
- **このケーキを四つ買(か)いました。** — I bought four of these cakes.
- **質問(しつもん)が五つあります。** — There are five questions.

## 人(にん) Counter — People

The **人** counter is used for counting people. The first two have irregular readings.

| Count | Japanese | Reading |
|-------|---------|---------|
| 1 person | 一人 | ひとり |
| 2 people | 二人 | ふたり |
| 3 people | 三人 | さんにん |
| 4 people | 四人 | よにん |
| 5 people | 五人 | ごにん |
| 6 people | 六人 | ろくにん |
| 7 people | 七人 | しちにん / ななにん |
| 8 people | 八人 | はちにん |
| 9 people | 九人 | きゅうにん |
| 10 people | 十人 | じゅうにん |

- **学生(がくせい)が三人います。** — There are three students.
- **家族(かぞく)は四人です。** — My family has four people.
- **友達(ともだち)と二人で行(い)きました。** — I went with a friend, just the two of us.
- **パーティーに十人来(き)ました。** — Ten people came to the party.
- **一人で住(す)んでいます。** — I live alone.

## 匹(ひき) Counter — Small Animals

The **匹** counter is used for small to medium animals (cats, dogs, fish, insects). Watch out for sound changes!

| Count | Reading |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | い**っぴき** |
| 2 | に**ひき** |
| 3 | さん**びき** |
| 4 | よん**ひき** |
| 5 | ご**ひき** |
| 6 | ろ**っぴき** |
| 7 | なな**ひき** |
| 8 | は**っぴき** |
| 9 | きゅう**ひき** |
| 10 | じゅ**っぴき** |

- **猫(ねこ)を二匹飼(か)っています。** — I have two cats.
- **魚(さかな)が三匹泳(およ)いでいます。** — Three fish are swimming.
- **犬(いぬ)が一匹います。** — There is one dog.
- **虫(むし)を五匹見(み)ました。** — I saw five bugs.
- **うさぎが六匹いました。** — There were six rabbits.

> ⚠️ Notice the sound changes: 1, 6, 8, 10 use **っぴき**, and 3 uses **びき**.

## 枚(まい) Counter — Flat Objects

The **枚** counter is used for flat, thin objects: paper, shirts, plates, tickets, photos.

| Count | Reading |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | いちまい |
| 2 | にまい |
| 3 | さんまい |
| 4 | よんまい |
| 5 | ごまい |

- **紙(かみ)を三枚ください。** — Three sheets of paper, please.
- **写真(しゃしん)を五枚撮(と)りました。** — I took five photos.
- **シャツを二枚買いました。** — I bought two shirts.
- **切手(きって)が一枚必要(ひつよう)です。** — I need one stamp.
- **お皿(さら)を四枚出(だ)してください。** — Please take out four plates.

## 本(ほん) Counter — Long, Thin Objects

The **本** counter is for long, cylindrical objects: pens, bottles, trees, roads, rivers, umbrellas, movies (rolls of film).

| Count | Reading |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | い**っぽん** |
| 2 | に**ほん** |
| 3 | さん**ぼん** |
| 4 | よん**ほん** |
| 5 | ご**ほん** |
| 6 | ろ**っぽん** |
| 7 | なな**ほん** |
| 8 | は**っぽん** |
| 9 | きゅう**ほん** |
| 10 | じゅ**っぽん** |

- **ペンを二本持(も)っています。** — I have two pens.
- **ビールを三本飲(の)みました。** — I drank three bottles of beer.
- **傘(かさ)を一本買いました。** — I bought one umbrella.
- **映画(えいが)を五本見ました。** — I watched five movies.
- **木(き)が十本あります。** — There are ten trees.

> ⚠️ Like 匹, this counter has sound changes: 1, 6, 8, 10 → **っぽん**, 3 → **ぼん**.

## 台(だい) Counter — Machines & Vehicles

The **台** counter is used for machines, vehicles, and large equipment: cars, computers, TVs, bicycles.

- **車(くるま)が三台あります。** — There are three cars.
- **パソコンを一台買いました。** — I bought one computer.
- **自転車(じてんしゃ)が二台あります。** — There are two bicycles.
- **テレビが四台あります。** — There are four TVs.
- **エアコンを二台つけました。** — I turned on two air conditioners.

## 冊(さつ) Counter — Books & Bound Items

The **冊** counter is used for books, notebooks, magazines — anything bound together.

| Count | Reading |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | い**っさつ** |
| 2 | に**さつ** |
| 3 | さん**さつ** |
| 4 | よん**さつ** |
| 5 | ご**さつ** |

- **本(ほん)を三冊読(よ)みました。** — I read three books.
- **雑誌(ざっし)を一冊買いました。** — I bought one magazine.
- **ノートが五冊あります。** — There are five notebooks.
- **辞書(じしょ)が二冊必要です。** — I need two dictionaries.
- **漫画(まんが)を十冊持っています。** — I have ten manga volumes.

## Counter Placement in Sentences

Counters typically appear **after the noun + particle** or **before the verb**:

- **猫を二匹飼っています。** — I keep two cats. (counter before verb)
- **二匹の猫を飼っています。** — I keep two cats. (counter before noun with の)

Both patterns are natural, but the first is more common in everyday speech.

- **りんごを三つ食(た)べました。** — I ate three apples.
- **三つのりんごを食べました。** — I ate three apples.
- **学生が五人来ました。** — Five students came.
- **五人の学生が来ました。** — Five students came.

## Asking "How many?"

Use the question words:

- **いくつ** — how many? (general, using つ counter)
- **何人(なんにん)** — how many people?
- **何匹(なんびき)** — how many (animals)?
- **何枚(なんまい)** — how many (flat things)?
- **何本(なんぼん)** — how many (long things)?
- **何冊(なんさつ)** — how many (books)?

- **りんごはいくつありますか。** — How many apples are there?
- **何人家族ですか。** — How many people are in your family?
- **ペンは何本ありますか。** — How many pens are there?
- **猫は何匹いますか。** — How many cats are there?
- **本を何冊読みましたか。** — How many books did you read?

## Summary

| Counter | Used for | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| つ | General / anything | りんごを三つ |
| 人(にん) | People | 学生が三人 |
| 匹(ひき) | Small animals | 猫が二匹 |
| 枚(まい) | Flat objects | 紙を三枚 |
| 本(ほん) | Long objects | ペンを二本 |
| 台(だい) | Machines/vehicles | 車が三台 |
| 冊(さつ) | Books/bound items | 本を三冊 |

When in doubt, you can often use the つ counter — native speakers will understand you even if a more specific counter exists!
