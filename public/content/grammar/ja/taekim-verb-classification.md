# Understanding Verb Types — る, う, and Irregular

Before you can conjugate Japanese verbs, you need to know which **type** a verb belongs to. Japanese verbs fall into three categories: **る-verbs** (ichidan), **う-verbs** (godan), and **irregular verbs**. The type determines how every single conjugation is formed, so getting this right is essential.

## る-Verbs (Ichidan / One-Row Verbs)

る-verbs are the simplest to conjugate. They always end in **る**, and the sound immediately before る is either an **-i** or **-e** vowel sound.

To identify: look at the kana before る. If it sits in the い-row or え-row of the kana chart, the verb is likely a る-verb.

**Common る-verbs:**

| Verb | Reading | Meaning | Sound before る |
|------|---------|---------|-----------------|
| 食べる | たべる | to eat | べ (be — え-row) |
| 見る | みる | to see | み (mi — い-row) |
| 起きる | おきる | to wake up | き (ki — い-row) |
| 出る | でる | to go out | で (de — え-row) |
| 寝る | ねる | to sleep | ね (ne — え-row) |

Conjugation only involves one step: drop る and add the new ending. Simple!

## う-Verbs (Godan / Five-Row Verbs)

う-verbs end in a sound from the **う-row** of the kana chart: う, く, す, つ, ぬ, ぶ, む, る, ぐ. Some う-verbs also end in る, which is where confusion can arise.

**Common う-verbs:**

| Verb | Reading | Meaning | Ending |
|------|---------|---------|--------|
| 飲む | のむ | to drink | む |
| 書く | かく | to write | く |
| 話す | はなす | to speak | す |
| 待つ | まつ | to wait | つ |
| 買う | かう | to buy | う |
| 泳ぐ | およぐ | to swim | ぐ |
| 遊ぶ | あそぶ | to play | ぶ |

## How to Tell Them Apart

Here is a practical decision guide:

1. **Does the verb end in る?**
   - **No** → It is always an **う-verb**. (飲む, 書く, 話す, etc.)
   - **Yes** → Continue to step 2.

2. **What sound comes before る?**
   - **/a/, /u/, or /o/ sound** → It is an **う-verb**. (帰**る** = かえ**る**, the え here is part of かえ, and the vowel before る is actually from the あ-row of the か-column — but more practically, these are learned as exceptions.)
   - **/i/ or /e/ sound** → It is **probably** a る-verb, but check the exceptions list.

> **The honest truth:** The /i/ and /e/ rule works most of the time, but some common verbs break it. The safest approach is to memorize verb types when you learn new vocabulary.

## Watch Out: Trick Verbs

These verbs **look like** る-verbs (they end in る with an /i/ or /e/ sound before it) but are actually **う-verbs**:

| Verb | Reading | Meaning | Actual type |
|------|---------|---------|-------------|
| 帰る | かえる | to return home | う-verb |
| 走る | はしる | to run | う-verb |
| 切る | きる | to cut | う-verb |
| 知る | しる | to know | う-verb |
| 入る | はいる | to enter | う-verb |

These must be memorized, but the list of common exceptions is small and you will learn them through exposure.

## Irregular Verbs

Japanese has only **two** truly irregular verbs:

| Verb | Reading | Meaning |
|------|---------|---------|
| する | する | to do |
| 来る | くる | to come |

These two do not follow the rules of either る-verbs or う-verbs. Every conjugation must be learned individually. The good news: there are only two of them, and they are so common that you will internalize their forms quickly.

**する** is also the basis for many compound verbs:

- **勉強する** (benkyō suru) — to study
- **料理する** (ryōri suru) — to cook
- **運動する** (undō suru) — to exercise

All する-compound verbs conjugate the same way — only the する part changes.

## Quick Reference

| Type | Also called | Identify by | Examples |
|------|-------------|-------------|----------|
| る-verb | Ichidan | Ends in る with /i/ or /e/ before it | 食べる, 見る, 寝る |
| う-verb | Godan | Ends in any う-row kana (or る with exceptions) | 飲む, 書く, 帰る |
| Irregular | — | Memorize these two | する, 来る |

Knowing verb types is the foundation for every conjugation you will learn from here on — negative, past, て-form, potential, and more. In the next lesson, we will put this knowledge to use with the negative verb form.
