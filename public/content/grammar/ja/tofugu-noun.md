# Nouns — Overview

> **Source:** Content from [Tofugu](https://www.tofugu.com/japanese-grammar/noun/). For personal study.

Nouns (名詞/めいし) are words used to identify things — people, places, objects, and abstract concepts. Japanese nouns don't change form for plural, gender, or grammatical case.

## Types of Nouns

### Common Nouns
General words that label the world: 犬 (dog), 学校 (school), 石 (stone).

### Proper Nouns
Specific names of people, places, etc.: 東京 (Tokyo), 田中 (Tanaka). These do not change form.

### Verbal Nouns (する Verbs)
Nouns that become verbs when combined with する:

- 勉強 (study) → 勉強**する** (to study)
- 料理 (cooking) → 料理**する** (to cook)
- 運動 (exercise) → 運動**する** (to exercise)

### Personal Pronouns
Words like 私, 僕, 彼 function as nouns in Japanese. See the dedicated pronouns page.

### Demonstrative Pronouns (こそあど Words)
Words like これ (this), それ (that), あれ (that over there), どれ (which).

### Relational Nouns
Words that describe spatial or temporal relationships:
- 上 (うえ) — above
- 下 (した) — below
- 前 (まえ) — in front of
- 中 (なか) — inside

Used with の: 机**の上** (on top of the desk).

## Nouns and Particles

Particles give nouns their grammatical role:

| Particle | Role | Example |
|---|---|---|
| が | Subject | **猫が**いる (There is a cat) |
| を | Object | **寿司を**食べる (Eat sushi) |
| は | Topic | **今日は**暑い (Today is hot) |
| に | Location/time | **東京に**住む (Live in Tokyo) |
| で | Means/location | **電車で**行く (Go by train) |
| の | Possession | **私の**本 (My book) |

## Nouns as Predicates

Nouns can serve as the predicate (main info) of a sentence:

- これは**犬**だ。 — This is a **dog**.
- あの人は**先生**です。 — That person is a **teacher**.

## Politeness with Nouns

### Polite Prefixes: 御〜

- お〜 (native Japanese words): お水 (water), お寿司 (sushi)
- ご〜 (Sino-Japanese words): ご飯 (meal), ご意見 (opinion)

### Polite Suffixes (Name Enders)

| Suffix | Usage |
|---|---|
| 〜さん | Standard polite |
| 〜様 (さま) | Very formal/respectful |
| 〜君 (くん) | For boys/subordinates |
| 〜ちゃん | Affectionate, for children/close people |

## Modifying Nouns

### With い-adjectives
Placed directly before the noun:
- **大きい**犬 — a big dog
- **美味しい**寿司 — delicious sushi

### With な-adjectives
Use な to connect:
- **きれいな**花 — a beautiful flower
- **静かな**部屋 — a quiet room

### With Particle の
- **日本の**文化 — Japanese culture
- **木の**テーブル — a wooden table

### With Clauses
Entire clauses can modify nouns (always placed before):
- **昨日買った**本 — the book **I bought yesterday**
