# Questions & Interrogatives

Asking questions is essential for any conversation. Japanese forms questions in a clean, systematic way: add the particle **か** for yes/no questions, and use **question words** for information questions. This lesson also covers a powerful pattern that transforms question words into "some-," "no-," and "any-" words.

## Yes/No Questions with か

To turn any statement into a yes/no question, simply add **か** at the end:

- **学生です。** (Gakusei desu.) — I am a student.
- **学生ですか？** (Gakusei desu ka?) — Are you a student?

- **明日、来ますか？** (Ashita, kimasu ka?) — Will you come tomorrow?
- **これは日本語の本ですか？** (Kore wa nihongo no hon desu ka?) — Is this a Japanese book?

In casual speech, か is often dropped and the question is indicated by rising intonation alone:

- **もう食べた？** (Mou tabeta?) — Did you already eat?

Note: Using か with casual (plain) form can sound **blunt or masculine**. In polite speech, always pair か with ます/です forms.

## Key Question Words

Here are the essential question words you'll use every day:

| Japanese | Reading | Meaning |
|----------|---------|---------|
| 何 | なに / なん (nani / nan) | what |
| どこ | doko | where |
| いつ | itsu | when |
| 誰 | だれ (dare) | who |
| どう | dou | how (in what way) |
| なぜ / どうして | naze / doushite | why |
| いくら | ikura | how much (price) |
| いくつ | ikutsu | how many / how old |
| どれ | dore | which one |
| どの | dono | which (+ noun) |

- **これは何ですか？** (Kore wa nan desu ka?) — What is this?
- **トイレはどこですか？** (Toire wa doko desu ka?) — Where is the bathroom?
- **誰が来ましたか？** (Dare ga kimashita ka?) — Who came?
- **いつ日本に行きますか？** (Itsu Nihon ni ikimasu ka?) — When will you go to Japan?
- **この本はいくらですか？** (Kono hon wa ikura desu ka?) — How much is this book?

Note: 何 is read **なん** before です, で, and counters (何人, 何時). Otherwise, it's **なに**.

## Question Word + か = "Some-"

When you add **か** to a question word, it becomes an **indefinite** word — "some-" in English:

| Question Word | + か | Meaning |
|---------------|------|---------|
| 何 (what) | 何か (nanika) | something |
| 誰 (who) | 誰か (dareka) | someone |
| どこ (where) | どこか (dokoka) | somewhere |
| いつ (when) | いつか (itsuka) | sometime |

- **何か食べたい。** (Nanika tabetai.) — I want to eat something.
- **誰かが来た。** (Dareka ga kita.) — Someone came.
- **どこかに行きたい。** (Dokoka ni ikitai.) — I want to go somewhere.
- **いつか日本に行きたい。** (Itsuka Nihon ni ikitai.) — I want to go to Japan someday.

## Question Word + も + Negative = "No- / Not any"

Combine a question word with **も** and a **negative verb** to express "nothing," "no one," "nowhere," etc.:

| Pattern | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| 何も...ない | nothing / not anything |
| 誰も...ない | no one / not anyone |
| どこも...ない | nowhere |
| いつも...ない | never (not ever) |

- **何も食べなかった。** (Nanimo tabenakatta.) — I didn't eat anything.
- **誰も来なかった。** (Daremo konakatta.) — No one came.
- **どこにも行かなかった。** (Doko ni mo ikanakatta.) — I didn't go anywhere.

Note: When a particle like に is needed, it goes **between** the question word and も: どこ**にも**.

Also note: **いつも** without a negative means "always"!

- **いつも朝ご飯を食べる。** (Itsumo asagohan o taberu.) — I always eat breakfast.

## Question Word + でも = "Any- / Every-"

Add **でも** to a question word to mean "any-" or "every-" (with no restrictions):

| Pattern | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| 何でも (nandemo) | anything / everything |
| 誰でも (daredemo) | anyone / everyone |
| どこでも (dokodemo) | anywhere |
| いつでも (itsudemo) | anytime |

- **何でも食べる。** (Nandemo taberu.) — I eat anything.
- **誰でも参加できる。** (Daredemo sanka dekiru.) — Anyone can participate.
- **いつでも来てください。** (Itsudemo kite kudasai.) — Please come anytime.

## The Pattern at a Glance

| Question Word | + か (some-) | + も + neg (no-) | + でも (any-) |
|---------------|-------------|------------------|--------------|
| 何 (what) | 何か | 何も...ない | 何でも |
| 誰 (who) | 誰か | 誰も...ない | 誰でも |
| どこ (where) | どこか | どこも...ない | どこでも |
| いつ (when) | いつか | いつも...ない | いつでも |

This pattern is one of the most elegant and useful in Japanese — learn it once and you unlock dozens of new expressions.
