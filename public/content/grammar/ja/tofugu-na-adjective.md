# な-Adjectives — Overview

> **Source:** Content from [Tofugu](https://www.tofugu.com/japanese-grammar/na-adjective/). For personal study.

な-adjectives are one of two adjective types in Japanese (alongside い-adjectives). They describe nouns and share many traits with nouns — they cannot be conjugated for tense and instead use だ/です.

## Present Tense

Use だ (plain) or です (polite):

- 日本の電車はすごく便利**だ**。 — Trains in Japan are very convenient.
- 平日なら、車より電車の方が便利**です**よ。 — On weekdays, the train is more convenient than a car.

Casual speech can drop the copula entirely:
- これは便利。 — This is convenient.

## Past Tense

Use だった (plain) or でした (polite):

- この街は静か**だった**。 — This town was quiet.
- ディスコはすごく人気**でした**。 — Disco was super popular.

## Negative

Use じゃない or ではない:

- 字があまり綺麗**じゃない**ね。 — The handwriting isn't very neat.
- チェスは上手**ではない**。 — I'm not good at chess.

## Position in a Sentence

### At the End (Predicate)
Describes the subject:
- 最近、猫のビデオが人気だよね。 — Cat videos are popular lately.

### Before a Noun
Use な to connect:
- **安全な**場所 — a safe place
- **有名な**人 — a famous person

### As a Noun
Some な-adjectives can function as nouns:
- **安全**が第一だ。 — **Safety** comes first.
- **自由**を守りましょう。 — Let's protect our **freedom**.

### As an Adverb
Combine with に:
- **静かに**ドアを閉めた。 — I closed the door **quietly**.
- バラが**綺麗に**咲いていた。 — Roses were blooming **beautifully**.

## Origins of な-adjectives

Three sources:
1. **Native Japanese** — words with hiragana endings like 〜か, 〜やか: 静か, 賑やか
2. **Chinese origin** (≈ two-thirds) — written in kanji: 綺麗, 安全, 健康
3. **Foreign loanwords** — written in katakana: ユニーク, オリジナル, クレージー

## Common Mistakes

### 同じ (Same)
- As predicate: 同じ**だ** ✓ (normal な-adjective behavior)
- Before a noun: 同じ靴 ✓ (no な needed!)
- Before a conjunction: 同じ**な**のに ✓ (な reappears)

### Confusing な-adjectives with い-adjectives
Words like きれい **end** in い but are な-adjectives:
- ❌ きれ**かった** → ⭕ きれい**だった** (past)
- ❌ きれ**くない** → ⭕ きれい**じゃない** (negative)

**Tip:** Check the kanji — if い is part of the kanji (綺麗, not 綺麗い), it's a な-adjective.
