# Learning Resources: A Curated Study Guide

Learning Russian is a marathon, not a sprint. This guide compiles the best free resources recommended by the Russian-learning community — organized by skill level and type — so you can build an effective study routine without wasting time on low-quality materials.

---

## Free Online Courses (Beginner)

These structured courses are excellent starting points for self-study:

- **russianenthusiast.com** — Well-structured beginner course focusing on core grammar concepts with clear explanations
- **russianlessons.net** — Clearly laid out lessons with good grammatical detail and exercises
- **learnrussian.rt.com** — Class-style interactive lessons with native audio recordings
- **russianforfree.com** — Good audio content with a simple, easy-to-navigate layout
- **mezhdunami.org** *(Между нами)* — A free, comprehensive immersive textbook modeled after a US college Russian course — one of the best free resources available
- **learnrussianstepbystep.com** — 137 structured lessons on grammar and vocabulary with audio, organized progressively from alphabet to advanced grammar

> **Tip:** Don't try to use all of these at once. Pick one main course and stick with it. Use others as supplementary reference when you need a different explanation of a concept.

## Phrasebooks & Structured Courses

- **Book2 / 50languages.com** — 100 free phrasebook-style lessons for English-to-Russian learners, with MP3 audio by native speakers. Great for building practical conversational skills
- **RussianLessons.net Vocabulary** — Extensive categorized vocabulary lists (food, drinks, body parts, etc.) with Russian audio — excellent supplementary resource for building topic-specific vocabulary

## YouTube Channels by Level

YouTube is one of the richest free resources for Russian learners. Here are the best channels organized by level.

### Beginner Channels (A1–A2)

- **Real Russian Club (Daria)** — Structured lessons covering pronunciation, grammar, and vocabulary with clear explanations
- **Russian with Anastasia** — Beginner-friendly lessons with a patient teaching style
- **Be Fluent in Russian** — Great for getting started with practical everyday Russian
- **RussianThroughPropaganda** — A full structured course with downloadable exercises (also at russianthroughpropaganda.com)

### Intermediate Channels (A2–B2)

- **Russian with Dasha** — Excellent from A2 level and up, with natural conversational content
- **Russian with Max** — Immersive style videos suitable from A2+, helps develop listening skills
- **Russian Grammar** — Detailed grammar explanations for those ready to go deeper
- **Lina's Lessons** — Clear, well-organized grammar and vocabulary explanations
- **Amazing Russian** — High-quality content covering grammar, culture, and real-world usage

### Advanced Channels (Fully in Russian)

Once you reach B2+, switch to content made *for* Russian speakers:

- **О русском по-русски** *(About Russian, in Russian)* — Excellent linguistic content for advanced learners
- **вДудь** *(vDud')* — Celebrity interviews and cultural discussions (some episodes have English subtitles)
- **Орел и Решка** *(Oryol i Reshka)* — A popular travel show — great for natural conversational Russian
- **Антон Птушкин** *(Anton Ptushkin)* — High-quality travel vlogs with beautiful cinematography and clear narration

## Dictionaries & Lookup Tools

Having the right dictionary makes all the difference. Bookmark these:

- **Wiktionary** — Comprehensive entries with full declension/conjugation tables, etymology, and audio examples
- **ReversoContext** — Shows words in real example sentences from translated texts — invaluable for understanding usage
- **Yandex Translate** — Higher-quality Russian translations than most alternatives; handles context well
- **MasterRussian Verb Conjugations** — The best free reference for Russian verb forms across all tenses and aspects
- **Forvo** — Native speaker pronunciations of virtually any Russian word — hear how real people say it
- **RussianForFree.com Dialogues** — Graded dialogues (beginner to advanced) with audio and translations, plus grammar exercises organized by level

> **Tip:** Use ReversoContext alongside a standard dictionary. Seeing a word in 10 different sentences teaches you more than memorizing one definition.

## Reading Resources

Reading is one of the most effective ways to build vocabulary naturally.

- **ReadLang** — Import any article or text, then click words for instant translation; automatically builds vocabulary lists
- **Lingua.com** — Graded reading texts (A1–B2) with audio recordings and comprehension exercises
- **Dual-language books** — Parallel Russian/English texts of classics like *Анна Каренина* (Anna Karenina) and *Алиса в стране чудес* (Alice in Wonderland) are available free online

### Reading Strategy

1. Start with texts at your level — it's okay to not understand every word
2. Read first for general meaning, then re-read for details
3. Look up only the words that appear repeatedly or block comprehension
4. Save new words to your flashcard deck (that's what LangLearn is for!)

## Listening Resources

Training your ear is essential — Russian sounds very different from how it looks on paper.

- **News in Slow Russian** — Current events spoken at a learner-friendly pace — great for intermediate learners
- **russianpodcast.eu** — A collection of Russian podcast episodes with transcripts
- **Эхо Москвы** *(Ekho Moskvy)* — Radio interviews WITH full transcripts — invaluable for comprehension practice because you can listen first, then check your understanding
- **GLOSS (gloss.dliflc.edu)** — Grammar notes and graded listening materials from the Defense Language Institute, organized by level. Includes Russian content with audio and transcripts. (Note: site may not work on all browsers)

### Listening Strategy

- **Послушайте сначала без текста, потом проверьте.** *(Poslushajte snachala bez teksta, potom prover'te.)* — Listen first without the text, then check.
- Start with slow, clear content and gradually increase speed and complexity
- Re-listen to the same content multiple times — each pass reveals new details

## Study Methodology Tips

These tips come from experienced learners in the Russian-learning community. They've been validated by thousands of learners over years of discussion.

### Daily Practice

- **Занимайтесь минимум двадцать минут каждый день.** *(Zanimajtes' minimum dvadtsat' minut kazhdyj den'.)* — Practice at least twenty minutes every day.
- Small sessions throughout the day beat one long block — three 15-minute sessions are more effective than one 45-minute session
- Consistency matters more than intensity — 20 minutes daily beats 3 hours on weekends

### Vocabulary Building

- Don't try to memorize words by just reading them — use spaced repetition (that's exactly what LangLearn does!)
- **Don't learn similar words at the same time** — studying all days of the week together, or words that sound alike, causes interference and confusion
- **Учите слова в контексте.** *(Uchite slova v kontekste.)* — Learn words in context. Sentences are more effective than isolated word lists.

### Grammar Approach

- Grammar through osmosis + reference is better than pure memorization
- Read the grammar explanation, see it in examples, then move on — it will click over time with exposure
- Don't try to memorize every declension table before you start reading and listening

### Managing Expectations

- **Будьте терпеливы с собой!** *(Bud'te terpelivy s soboy!)* — Be patient with yourself!
- Expect roughly 2,000 hours of study for reasonable conversational fluency
- Progress feels slow at first but accelerates as your foundation grows
- Plateaus are normal — they mean your brain is consolidating what you've learned

## Building Your Study Routine

Here's a suggested daily routine that combines these resources effectively:

| Time | Activity | Resource |
|------|----------|----------|
| 10 min | Review flashcards | LangLearn SRS Review |
| 10 min | Grammar lesson | LangLearn Grammar or russianenthusiast.com |
| 10 min | Listening practice | News in Slow Russian or Эхо Москвы |
| 10 min | Reading practice | Lingua.com or ReadLang |
| 5 min | Add new words | LangLearn Words |

> You don't need to do all of this every day. Even just the flashcard review keeps your momentum going.

### Key Principles

1. **Spaced repetition is non-negotiable** — review your cards every day, even if you skip everything else
2. **Input before output** — spend more time reading and listening than trying to speak, especially at the beginning
3. **Use content you enjoy** — if you hate news podcasts, watch Орел и Решка instead
4. **Track your progress** — LangLearn's analytics help you see how far you've come
