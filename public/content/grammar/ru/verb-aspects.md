# Perfective vs Imperfective Verbs

One of the most important concepts in Russian grammar is **verbal aspect** (вид глагола). Almost every Russian verb belongs to one of two aspects: **imperfective** (несовершенный вид) or **perfective** (совершенный вид). Choosing the right aspect is essential for expressing meaning accurately.

## What Is Aspect?

Aspect describes **how** an action relates to time — not *when* it happens, but whether it is viewed as a process or a completed event.

- **Imperfective** (НСВ) — focuses on the **process**, duration, repetition, or ongoing nature of an action.
- **Perfective** (СВ) — focuses on the **result**, completion, or a single completed event.

## Aspect Pairs

Most verbs come in pairs — one imperfective and one perfective:

| Imperfective | Perfective | Meaning |
|-------------|-----------|---------|
| читать | прочитать | to read |
| писать | написать | to write |
| делать | сделать | to do/make |
| учить | выучить | to learn/study |
| покупать | купить | to buy |

Common ways to form perfective verbs:
- Add a **prefix**: читать → **про**читать, писать → **на**писать
- Change the **suffix**: покупать → купить, решать → решить

## When to Use Each Aspect

### Use Imperfective When:

1. **Describing a process or duration:**
   - Я **читал** книгу два часа. (I was reading a book for two hours.)

2. **Talking about habitual or repeated actions:**
   - Каждый день я **учу** новые слова. (Every day I study new words.)

3. **Stating a general fact (without focus on completion):**
   - Ты **читал** эту книгу? (Have you read this book? — asking about the experience)

### Use Perfective When:

1. **An action was completed with a result:**
   - Я **прочитал** книгу. (I finished reading the book.)

2. **A single, one-time event:**
   - Он **написал** письмо. (He wrote a letter — and it's done.)

3. **Sequential actions (one after another):**
   - Я **встал**, **позавтракал** и **ушёл**. (I got up, had breakfast, and left.)

## Aspect and Tense

| | Past | Present | Future |
|---|------|---------|--------|
| Imperfective | читал/читала | читаю | буду читать |
| Perfective | прочитал/прочитала | ❌ (no present!) | прочитаю |

**Key rule:** Perfective verbs have **no present tense**. Their conjugated form is always future.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Using perfective for repeated actions:** ❌ Каждый день я *прочитал* книгу. → ✅ Каждый день я **читал** книгу.
2. **Using imperfective when result matters:** ❌ Я вчера *писал* письмо (и отправил). → ✅ Я вчера **написал** письмо.

## Practice Strategy

- When you learn a new verb, always learn **both aspects** together.
- Ask yourself: "Am I talking about the **process** or the **result**?"
- Process/habit/duration → imperfective. Result/completion/single event → perfective.

With practice, choosing the right aspect will become intuitive!
