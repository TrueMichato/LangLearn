# Choosing Verb Aspect: A Practical Guide (Выбор вида глагола)

Earlier lessons introduced perfective and imperfective aspects and explored aspect pairs in detail. This lesson takes a **practical, decision-based approach** — when you're composing a sentence, how do you choose? We'll work through real situations with clear rules of thumb, common patterns, and the tricky edge cases that trip up learners.

---

## 1. When to Use Imperfective

The imperfective aspect focuses on the **process**, **duration**, **repetition**, or **general experience** — not on the result.

### Ongoing actions (process in progress)

**Я читал, когда ты позвонил.** *(Ya chitál, kogdá ty pozvoníl.)* — I was reading when you called.

**Она готовила ужин.** *(Oná gotóvila úzhin.)* — She was cooking dinner.

**Дети играли в парке.** *(Déti igráli v párke.)* — The kids were playing in the park.

### Repeated or habitual actions

**Он каждый день бегал.** *(On kázhdyy den bégal.)* — He ran every day.

**Мы часто ходили в кино.** *(My chásto khodíli v kinó.)* — We often went to the movies.

**Она всегда опаздывала.** *(Oná vsegdá opázdyvala.)* — She was always late.

### General experience ("have you ever...?")

**Ты читал «Войну и мир»?** *(Ty chitál «Voynu i mir»?)* — Have you (ever) read "War and Peace"?

**Вы когда-нибудь летали на самолёте?** *(Vy kogdá-nibud letáli na samolyóte?)* — Have you ever flown on a plane?

### Naming the action (without focus on result)

**Что ты делал вчера? — Я работал.** *(Chtó ty délal vchera? — Ya rabótal.)* — What did you do yesterday? — I worked.

**Чем вы занимались?** *(Chem vy zanimális?)* — What were you busy with?

## 2. When to Use Perfective

The perfective aspect focuses on the **result**, **completion**, **single event**, or **boundary** (beginning or end) of an action.

### Completed action with a result

**Я прочитал эту книгу.** *(Ya prochitál étu knígu.)* — I read (finished) this book.

**Она написала письмо.** *(Oná napisála pismó.)* — She wrote (completed) a letter.

**Мы купили машину.** *(My kupíli mashínu.)* — We bought a car.

### Single, one-time event

**Он открыл дверь.** *(On otkrýl dver.)* — He opened the door (once).

**Она уронила чашку.** *(Oná uroníla cháshku.)* — She dropped the cup.

**Вдруг он засмеялся.** *(Vdrug on zasmeyálsya.)* — Suddenly he laughed.

### Sequence of completed events

**Я пришёл домой, поужинал и лёг спать.** *(Ya prishyól domóy, pouzhínal i lyóg spat.)* — I came home, had dinner, and went to bed.

**Она встала, оделась и вышла.** *(Oná vstála, odélas i výshla.)* — She got up, got dressed, and went out.

### Achieving a result in the future

**Я прочитаю эту книгу завтра.** *(Ya prochitáyu étu knígu závtra.)* — I'll read (finish) this book tomorrow.

**Мы решим эту проблему.** *(My reshím étu problémy.)* — We'll solve this problem.

---

## 3. The Decision Flowchart

When choosing aspect, ask yourself these questions in order:

**1. Is the action repeated or habitual?** → Imperfective
- Signal words: всегда, часто, каждый день, обычно, иногда

**2. Is the action in progress / being described as a process?** → Imperfective
- Signal words: когда, пока, в это время, долго

**3. Is the action completed with a clear result?** → Perfective
- Signal words: уже, наконец, вдруг, сразу

**4. Is it a sequence of completed events?** → Perfective
- Signal: multiple actions listed one after another

**5. Is it a general experience question?** → Imperfective
- Signal: когда-нибудь, "have you ever"

**6. Is it a specific request to complete something?** → Perfective
- Signal: imperative for a concrete action

## 4. Aspect in Negation

Negation interacts with aspect in important ways:

### Past tense negation: "didn't do" vs. "wasn't doing"

**Imperfective — the action didn't take place at all:**

**Я не читал эту книгу.** *(Ya ne chitál étu knígu.)* — I haven't read this book (never).

**Она не звонила.** *(Oná ne zvoníla.)* — She didn't call (at all, no attempt).

**Perfective — the expected result wasn't achieved:**

**Я не прочитал эту книгу.** *(Ya ne prochitál étu knígu.)* — I didn't finish reading this book.

**Она не позвонила.** *(Oná ne pozvoníla.)* — She didn't call (she was expected to, but didn't).

### Negative commands: almost always imperfective

**Не открывай окно!** *(Ne otkryvay oknó!)* — Don't open the window!

**Не трогай!** *(Ne trógay!)* — Don't touch!

**Не говори ему.** *(Ne govorí yemú.)* — Don't tell him.

> Perfective in negative commands is rare and implies warning against an accidental action: **Не урони!** *(Ne uroní!)* — Don't drop it! (be careful)

## 5. Aspect in Requests and Commands

### Imperfective imperative — general invitation, ongoing instruction

**Читайте, пожалуйста.** *(Chitáyte, pozháluysta.)* — Please read. (general invitation)

**Входите!** *(Vkhodíte!)* — Come in! (welcoming)

**Садитесь.** *(Sadítes.)* — Have a seat. (polite invitation)

### Perfective imperative — specific task, concrete result expected

**Прочитайте эту статью к завтра.** *(Prochitáyte étu statyu k závtra.)* — Read this article by tomorrow.

**Откройте учебник на странице пять.** *(Otkróyte uchébnik na straníitse pyat.)* — Open the textbook to page five.

**Дайте мне, пожалуйста, чек.** *(Dáyte mne, pozháluysta, chek.)* — Give me the receipt, please.

### The давай/давайте distinction

**Давай пойдём в кино!** *(Daváy poydyóm v kinó!)* — Let's go to the movies! (perfective — specific plan)

**Давай будем заниматься каждый день.** *(Daváy búdem zanimátsya kázhdyy den.)* — Let's study every day. (imperfective — ongoing habit)

## 6. Common Aspect Pairs: Quick Reference

| Imperfective | Perfective | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| читать *(chitát)* | прочитать *(prochitát)* | to read |
| писать *(pisát)* | написать *(napisát)* | to write |
| делать *(délat)* | сделать *(sdélat)* | to do/make |
| говорить *(govorít)* | сказать *(skazát)* | to say |
| брать *(brat)* | взять *(vzyat)* | to take |
| покупать *(pokupát)* | купить *(kupít)* | to buy |
| открывать *(otkryvát)* | открыть *(otkrýt)* | to open |
| закрывать *(zakryvát)* | закрыть *(zakrýt)* | to close |
| решать *(reshát)* | решить *(reshít)* | to decide/solve |
| объяснять *(obyasnyát)* | объяснить *(obyasnít)* | to explain |

## 7. Tricky Cases and Edge Situations

### "Trying" vs. "succeeding"

**Imperfective can imply attempt without guaranteed success:**

**Я открывал банку, но не смог.** *(Ya otkryvál bánku, no ne smog.)* — I was trying to open the jar, but couldn't.

**Perfective implies success:**

**Я открыл банку.** *(Ya otkrýl bánku.)* — I opened the jar (successfully).

### Annulled result (action happened but was reversed)

**Imperfective — the result no longer stands:**

**Кто открывал окно? Тут холодно!** *(Kto otkryvál oknó? Tut khólodno!)* — Who opened the window? It's cold! (someone opened and maybe closed it, but the effect remains)

**Perfective — the result still stands:**

**Кто открыл окно?** *(Kto otkrýl oknó?)* — Who opened the window? (it's still open)

### Simultaneous vs. sequential actions

**Imperfective — simultaneous:**

**Пока я готовил, она читала.** *(Poká ya gotóvil, oná chitála.)* — While I was cooking, she was reading.

**Perfective — sequential:**

**Когда я приготовил ужин, мы сели есть.** *(Kogdá ya prigotóvil úzhin, my séli yest.)* — When I finished cooking dinner, we sat down to eat.

## 8. Common Mistakes

❌ **Я каждый день прочитал газету.** — Repeated action needs imperfective.
✅ **Я каждый день читал газету.** — I read the newspaper every day.

❌ **Не прочитай эту книгу!** — Negative commands use imperfective.
✅ **Не читай эту книгу!** — Don't read this book!

❌ **Я начал прочитать.** — After начать, use imperfective.
✅ **Я начал читать.** — I started reading.

❌ **Я буду прочитать.** — Don't combine буду with perfective infinitive.
✅ **Я прочитаю.** (perfective future) / **Я буду читать.** (imperfective future)

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Imperfective** = process, repetition, experience, ongoing — "what was happening."
2. **Perfective** = result, completion, single event, sequence — "what happened."
3. Signal words guide your choice: всегда/часто → imperfective; уже/вдруг → perfective.
4. Negative commands are almost always **imperfective**.
5. In negation, imperfective = "didn't do at all"; perfective = "didn't achieve the result."
6. After начать/продолжать → imperfective. After забыть/успеть → perfective.
7. When in doubt, ask: "Am I focusing on the process or the result?"
