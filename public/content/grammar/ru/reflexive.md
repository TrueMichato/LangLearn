# Reflexive Verbs (-ся/-сь)

Reflexive verbs are extremely common in Russian. They are formed by adding the particle **-ся** (after consonants and the soft sign ь) or **-сь** (after vowels) to the end of a verb. This particle originally comes from the pronoun **себя** (oneself), but reflexive verbs have developed many meanings beyond the literal "oneself."

---

## 1. What Are Reflexive Verbs?

A reflexive verb is any verb ending in **-ся** or **-сь**. The suffix follows the personal ending of the verb:

| After consonant/ь → **-ся** | After vowel → **-сь** |
|---|---|
| он моет**ся** | она мое**тся** → (same: -тся) |
| мыть**ся** (infinitive) | я мою**сь** |
| мой**ся**! (imperative) | мы моем**ся** |

The rule is simple:
- If the ending before the particle is a **consonant** → add **-ся**
- If the ending before the particle is a **vowel** → add **-сь**

**Я умываюсь каждое утро.** *(Ya umyvayus kazhdoye utro.)* — I wash my face every morning.

**Он одевается быстро.** *(On odevayetsya bystro.)* — He gets dressed quickly.

**Дети купаются в море.** *(Deti kupayutsya v more.)* — The children are bathing in the sea.

**Мы готовимся к экзамену.** *(My gotovimsa k ekzamenu.)* — We are preparing for the exam.

**Она улыбается.** *(Ona ulybayetsya.)* — She is smiling.

---

## 2. True Reflexive Meaning

In the most literal sense, reflexive verbs describe an action directed **back at the subject** — the person does something **to themselves**.

### Non-reflexive → Reflexive pairs

| Non-reflexive | Meaning | Reflexive | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| мыть | to wash (something) | мыться | to wash oneself |
| одевать | to dress (someone) | одеваться | to dress oneself |
| причёсывать | to comb (someone) | причёсываться | to comb one's own hair |
| брить | to shave (someone) | бриться | to shave oneself |
| купать | to bathe (someone) | купаться | to bathe oneself |

**Мама моет ребёнка.** *(Mama moyet rebyonka.)* — Mom is washing the child.

**Ребёнок моется сам.** *(Rebyonok moyetsya sam.)* — The child washes himself.

**Она одевает дочку.** *(Ona odevayet dochku.)* — She is dressing her daughter.

**Она одевается перед зеркалом.** *(Ona odevayetsya pered zerkalom.)* — She is getting dressed in front of the mirror.

**Парикмахер причёсывает клиента.** *(Parikmakher prichyosyvayet kliyenta.)* — The barber is combing the client's hair.

**Я причёсываюсь перед выходом.** *(Ya prichyosyvayus pered vykhodom.)* — I comb my hair before going out.

**Он бреет бороду.** *(On breyet borodu.)* — He is shaving his beard. (shaving something)

**Он бреется каждое утро.** *(On breyetsya kazhdoye utro.)* — He shaves every morning. (shaves himself)

## 3. Reciprocal Meaning

Reflexive verbs can express a **reciprocal** action — something people do **to each other**.

**Мы встречаемся каждую пятницу.** *(My vstrechayemsya kazhduyu pyatnitsu.)* — We meet (each other) every Friday.

**Они целуются.** *(Oni tseluyutsya.)* — They are kissing (each other).

**Друзья обнимаются при встрече.** *(Druzya obnimayutsya pri vstreche.)* — Friends hug (each other) when they meet.

**Мы переписываемся каждый день.** *(My perepisyvayemsya kazhdyy den.)* — We write to each other every day.

**Они ссорятся из-за пустяков.** *(Oni ssoryatsya iz-za pustyakov.)* — They quarrel (with each other) over trifles.

**Мы давно не виделись!** *(My davno ne videlis!)* — We haven't seen each other in a long time!

**Они познакомились на вечеринке.** *(Oni poznakomilis na vecherinke.)* — They met (got acquainted with each other) at a party.

## 4. Verbs That Only Exist as Reflexive

Some Russian verbs **only** appear in the reflexive form. They have no non-reflexive counterpart. You must simply memorize these.

| Verb | Transliteration | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| бояться | boyatsya | to be afraid |
| смеяться | smeyatsya | to laugh |
| нравиться | nravitsya | to be pleasing (to like) |
| стараться | staratsya | to try, to make an effort |
| надеяться | nadeyatsya | to hope |
| гордиться | gorditsya | to be proud |
| улыбаться | ulybatsya | to smile |
| появляться | poyavlyatsya | to appear |

**Я боюсь темноты.** *(Ya boyus temnoty.)* — I am afraid of the dark.

**Дети смеются над шуткой.** *(Deti smeyutsya nad shutkoy.)* — The children are laughing at the joke.

**Мне нравится эта книга.** *(Mne nravitsya eta kniga.)* — I like this book. (lit. This book is pleasing to me.)

**Он старается учиться хорошо.** *(On starayetsya uchitsya khorosho.)* — He tries to study well.

**Мы надеемся на лучшее.** *(My nadeyemsya na luchsheye.)* — We hope for the best.

**Она гордится своим сыном.** *(Ona gorditsya svoim synom.)* — She is proud of her son.

**Почему ты улыбаешься?** *(Pochemu ty ulybayeshsya?)* — Why are you smiling?

## 5. Reflexive vs Non-Reflexive Pairs (Change in Meaning)

Many verbs change their meaning significantly when **-ся** is added. The reflexive form is not simply "doing the action to oneself."

| Non-reflexive | Meaning | Reflexive | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| учить | to teach / to study (memorize) | учиться | to study, to be a student |
| находить | to find | находиться | to be located |
| заниматься | — | заниматься | to study, to be engaged in |
| готовить | to cook, to prepare (something) | готовиться | to prepare oneself (for) |
| возвращать | to return (something) | возвращаться | to return, come back |
| начинать | to begin (something) | начинаться | to begin (intransitive) |
| кончать | to finish (something) | кончаться | to end, to run out |
| открывать | to open (something) | открываться | to open (by itself) |

**Она учит английский.** *(Ona uchit angliyskiy.)* — She is studying (memorizing) English.

**Она учится в университете.** *(Ona uchitsya v universitete.)* — She studies at the university (is a student).

**Я нашёл ключи.** *(Ya nashyol klyuchi.)* — I found the keys.

**Музей находится на главной улице.** *(Muzey nakhoditsya na glavnoy ulitse.)* — The museum is located on the main street.

**Мама готовит обед.** *(Mama gotovit obed.)* — Mom is cooking lunch.

**Студенты готовятся к экзамену.** *(Studenty gotovyatsya k ekzamenu.)* — The students are preparing for the exam.

**Он возвращает книгу в библиотеку.** *(On vozvrashchayet knigu v biblioteku.)* — He is returning the book to the library.

**Она возвращается домой поздно.** *(Ona vozvrashchayetsya domoy pozdno.)* — She returns home late.

**Урок начинается в девять часов.** *(Urok nachinayetsya v devyat chasov.)* — The lesson begins at nine o'clock.

**Фильм кончается через час.** *(Film konchayetsya cherez chas.)* — The movie ends in an hour.

## 6. Conjugation of Reflexive Verbs

Reflexive verbs conjugate just like their non-reflexive counterparts — you simply add **-ся** or **-сь** after the personal ending.

### Present Tense: мыться (to wash oneself) — 1st conjugation

| Pronoun | Conjugation | Transliteration |
|---|---|---|
| я | мою**сь** | moyus |
| ты | моешь**ся** | moyeshsya |
| он/она | моет**ся** | moyetsya |
| мы | моем**ся** | moyemsya |
| вы | моете**сь** | moyetes |
| они | моют**ся** | moyutsya |

### Present Tense: учиться (to study) — 2nd conjugation

| Pronoun | Conjugation | Transliteration |
|---|---|---|
| я | учу**сь** | uchus |
| ты | учишь**ся** | uchishsya |
| он/она | учит**ся** | uchitsya |
| мы | учим**ся** | uchimsya |
| вы | учите**сь** | uchites |
| они | учат**ся** | uchatsya |

**Я учусь в медицинском институте.** *(Ya uchus v meditsinskom institute.)* — I study at the medical institute.

**Ты моешься слишком долго!** *(Ty moyeshsya slishkom dolgo!)* — You take too long washing!

**Мы учимся говорить по-русски.** *(My uchimsya govorit po-russki.)* — We are learning to speak Russian.

**Они всегда смеются.** *(Oni vsegda smeyutsya.)* — They are always laughing.

**Вы готовитесь к поездке?** *(Vy gotovites k poyezdke?)* — Are you preparing for the trip?

### Past Tense

In the past tense, **-ся** is added after consonants and **-сь** after vowels — the same rule applies:

| Form | мыться | учиться |
|---|---|---|
| он (masc.) | мыл**ся** | учил**ся** |
| она (fem.) | мыла**сь** | учила**сь** |
| оно (neut.) | мыло**сь** | училo**сь** |
| они (pl.) | мыли**сь** | учили**сь** |

**Он мылся в душе.** *(On mylsya v dushe.)* — He was washing in the shower.

**Она училась в Москве.** *(Ona uchilas v Moskve.)* — She studied in Moscow.

**Дети купались в реке.** *(Deti kupalis v reke.)* — The children were swimming in the river.

**Мы встретились у метро.** *(My vstretilis u metro.)* — We met at the metro station.

**Всё случилось очень быстро.** *(Vsyo sluchilos ochen bystro.)* — Everything happened very fast.

## 7. The Passive and Impersonal Uses

Reflexive verbs are also used to form **passive** constructions and **impersonal** expressions.

### Passive meaning

**Дом строится рабочими.** *(Dom stroitsya rabochimi.)* — The house is being built by workers.

**Эта книга читается легко.** *(Eta kniga chitayetsya legko.)* — This book reads easily (is easy to read).

**Магазин открывается в девять.** *(Magazin otkryvayetsya v devyat.)* — The store opens at nine.

**Задача решается быстро.** *(Zadacha reshayetsya bystro.)* — The problem is solved quickly.

**Письмо пишется от руки.** *(Pismo pishetsya ot ruki.)* — The letter is written by hand.

### Impersonal expressions

**Мне не спится.** *(Mne ne spitsya.)* — I can't sleep. (lit. It doesn't sleep itself to me.)

**Ему не работается сегодня.** *(Yemu ne rabotayetsya segodnya.)* — He can't get any work done today.

**Как вам живётся в Москве?** *(Kak vam zhivyotsya v Moskve?)* — How is life treating you in Moscow?

**Мне хорошо здесь дышится.** *(Mne khorosho zdes dyshitsya.)* — I breathe well here. (The air is good for me here.)

**Нам не верится!** *(Nam ne veritsya!)* — We can't believe it!

## 8. Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Я боюся.** — Incorrect: after vowel у, use -сь.
✅ **Я боюсь.** *(Ya boyus.)* — I am afraid.

❌ **Она учитьсь.** — Incorrect spelling.
✅ **Она учится.** *(Ona uchitsya.)* — She studies.

❌ **Мне нравится книги.** — Subject-verb agreement error.
✅ **Мне нравятся книги.** *(Mne nravyatsya knigi.)* — I like books. (Books are pleasing to me — plural subject.)

❌ **Он моет себя.** — Technically correct but unnatural.
✅ **Он моется.** *(On moyetsya.)* — He washes himself. (Use the reflexive verb.)

❌ **Дверь открывает.** — Missing agent or reflexive marker.
✅ **Дверь открывается.** *(Dver otkryvayetsya.)* — The door opens (by itself).

## Key Takeaways

1. Reflexive verbs end in **-ся** (after consonants) or **-сь** (after vowels).
2. They can express: action on **oneself**, **reciprocal** action, **passive** voice, or **impersonal** meaning.
3. Some verbs **only exist** as reflexive (бояться, смеяться, нравиться).
4. Many verbs **change meaning** when -ся is added (находить → находиться).
5. Conjugation follows the same pattern as non-reflexive verbs — just append the reflexive suffix.
6. In **нравиться** constructions, the thing that is liked is the **subject** (and must agree with the verb).
