# Noun Cases Overview

Russian uses **six grammatical cases** to show the role of a noun in a sentence. Instead of relying on word order (like English), Russian changes the endings of nouns, adjectives, and pronouns. Understanding cases is the key to reading and speaking Russian correctly.

## The Six Cases

### 1. Именительный (Nominative) — Who? What?

The **nominative** case is the dictionary form. It marks the **subject** of a sentence.

- **Кошка** спит. (The cat sleeps.)
- **Студент** читает. (The student reads.)

### 2. Родительный (Genitive) — Of whom? Of what?

The **genitive** case shows **possession**, **absence**, or is used after certain prepositions (из, от, без, у).

- Книга **студента**. (The student's book.)
- У меня нет **кошки**. (I don't have a cat.)

| Gender | Nominative | Genitive |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| Masc. | студент | студент**а** |
| Fem. | кошка | кошк**и** |
| Neut. | окно | окн**а** |

### 3. Дательный (Dative) — To whom? To what?

The **dative** case indicates the **indirect object** — the recipient of an action.

- Я дал книгу **студенту**. (I gave the book to the student.)
- Позвони **маме**. (Call mom.)

### 4. Винительный (Accusative) — Whom? What?

The **accusative** case marks the **direct object** of a verb.

- Я вижу **кошку**. (I see the cat.)
- Он читает **книгу**. (He reads the/a book.)

For masculine animate nouns, accusative = genitive. For inanimate, accusative = nominative.

### 5. Творительный (Instrumental) — By whom? With what?

The **instrumental** case shows the **means** or **instrument** of an action, or accompaniment (with).

- Я пишу **ручкой**. (I write with a pen.)
- Она гуляет с **другом**. (She walks with a friend.)

### 6. Предложный (Prepositional) — About whom? Where?

The **prepositional** case is always used with a preposition (в, на, о/об). It often indicates **location** or **topic**.

- Я живу в **Москве**. (I live in Moscow.)
- Мы говорим о **музыке**. (We talk about music.)

## Tips for Learning Cases

1. **Learn cases in context** — memorize example phrases, not just endings.
2. **Start with accusative and genitive** — they are the most common after nominative.
3. **Practice with real sentences** from your reading material.
4. Cases may seem overwhelming at first, but patterns emerge quickly with practice!
