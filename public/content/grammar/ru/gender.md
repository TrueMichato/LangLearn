# Gender & Agreement

Russian nouns belong to one of **three grammatical genders**: masculine (мужской род), feminine (женский род), or neuter (средний род). Gender affects how adjectives, pronouns, and past-tense verbs agree with the noun. Learning to identify gender quickly is one of the most important early skills in Russian.

---

## 1. The Three Genders

Every Russian noun has a fixed gender. Unlike some languages, gender in Russian is mostly predictable from the noun's ending.

| Gender | Typical Endings | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Masculine (м) | consonant, -й, -ь | стол, музей, словарь |
| Feminine (ж) | -а, -я, -ь | книга, неделя, тетрадь |
| Neuter (ср) | -о, -е, -мя | окно, море, время |

**Стол стоит в углу.** *(Stol stoit v uglu.)* — The table stands in the corner.

**Книга лежит на столе.** *(Kniga lezhit na stole.)* — The book is lying on the table.

**Окно открыто.** *(Okno otkryto.)* — The window is open.

**Музей работает каждый день.** *(Muzey rabotayet kazhdyy den'.)* — The museum works every day.

**Неделя была длинной.** *(Nedelya byla dlinnoy.)* — The week was long.

**Море очень красивое.** *(More ochen' krasivoye.)* — The sea is very beautiful.

---

## 2. Masculine Nouns

Most masculine nouns end in a **consonant**. Some end in **-й** or **-ь**.

### Consonant Endings

**Дом очень большой.** *(Dom ochen' bol'shoy.)* — The house is very big.

**Брат работает в банке.** *(Brat rabotayet v banke.)* — The brother works at a bank.

**Город красивый и старый.** *(Gorod krasivyy i staryy.)* — The city is beautiful and old.

**Студент сдал экзамен.** *(Student sdal ekzamen.)* — The student passed the exam.

**Врач принимает пациентов.** *(Vrach prinimayet patsiyentov.)* — The doctor sees patients.

### Nouns Ending in -й

**Музей закрыт сегодня.** *(Muzey zakryt segodnya.)* — The museum is closed today.

**Герой спас деревню.** *(Geroy spas derevnyu.)* — The hero saved the village.

**Трамвай приехал вовремя.** *(Tramvay priyekhal vovremya.)* — The tram arrived on time.

**Чай уже готов.** *(Chay uzhe gotov.)* — The tea is already ready.

**Край земли далеко.** *(Kray zemli daleko.)* — The edge of the earth is far away.

### Masculine Nouns Ending in -ь (Soft Sign)

Some nouns ending in **-ь** are masculine. These must be memorized.

**Словарь лежит на полке.** *(Slovar' lezhit na polke.)* — The dictionary is on the shelf.

**Учитель объясняет правило.** *(Uchitel' ob"yasnyayet pravilo.)* — The teacher explains the rule.

**Автомобиль сломался.** *(Avtomobil' slomalsya.)* — The car broke down.

**Гость пришёл рано.** *(Gost' prishyol rano.)* — The guest came early.

**Дождь идёт весь день.** *(Dozhd' idyot ves' den'.)* — It has been raining all day.

---

## 3. Feminine Nouns

Most feminine nouns end in **-а** or **-я**. Some end in **-ь**.

### Nouns Ending in -а

**Мама готовит ужин.** *(Mama gotovit uzhin.)* — Mom is cooking dinner.

**Школа находится рядом.** *(Shkola nakhoditsya ryadom.)* — The school is nearby.

**Работа начинается в девять.** *(Rabota nachinayetsya v devyat'.)* — Work starts at nine.

**Собака бежит по парку.** *(Sobaka bezhit po parku.)* — The dog is running through the park.

**Москва — столица России.** *(Moskva — stolitsa Rossii.)* — Moscow is the capital of Russia.

### Nouns Ending in -я

**Неделя пролетела быстро.** *(Nedelya proletela bystro.)* — The week flew by quickly.

**Кухня маленькая, но уютная.** *(Kukhnya malen'kaya, no uyutnaya.)* — The kitchen is small but cozy.

**Земля покрыта снегом.** *(Zemlya pokryta snegom.)* — The ground is covered with snow.

**Песня очень красивая.** *(Pesnya ochen' krasivaya.)* — The song is very beautiful.

**Станция метро далеко отсюда.** *(Stantsiya metro daleko otsyuda.)* — The metro station is far from here.

### Feminine Nouns Ending in -ь (Soft Sign)

Some **-ь** nouns are feminine. These are the tricky ones — you must learn them individually.

**Тетрадь лежит в сумке.** *(Tetrad' lezhit v sumke.)* — The notebook is in the bag.

**Ночь была тихой.** *(Noch' byla tikhoy.)* — The night was quiet.

**Дверь закрыта.** *(Dver' zakryta.)* — The door is closed.

**Жизнь прекрасна.** *(Zhizn' prekrasna.)* — Life is beautiful.

**Мышь убежала.** *(Mysh' ubezhala.)* — The mouse ran away.

---

## 4. Neuter Nouns

Neuter nouns end in **-о**, **-е**, or **-мя**.

### Nouns Ending in -о

**Молоко стоит в холодильнике.** *(Moloko stoit v kholodil'nike.)* — The milk is in the fridge.

**Письмо пришло вчера.** *(Pis'mo prishlo vchera.)* — The letter came yesterday.

**Окно выходит на улицу.** *(Okno vykhodit na ulitsu.)* — The window faces the street.

**Слово имеет два значения.** *(Slovo imeyet dva znacheniya.)* — The word has two meanings.

**Небо сегодня голубое.** *(Nebo segodnya goluboye.)* — The sky is blue today.

### Nouns Ending in -е

**Море было спокойным.** *(More bylo spokoynym.)* — The sea was calm.

**Поле покрыто цветами.** *(Pole pokryto tsvetami.)* — The field is covered with flowers.

**Здание очень высокое.** *(Zdaniye ochen' vysokoye.)* — The building is very tall.

**Солнце встаёт рано летом.** *(Solntse vstayot rano letom.)* — The sun rises early in summer.

**Сердце бьётся быстро.** *(Serdtse b'yotsya bystro.)* — The heart beats fast.

### Nouns Ending in -мя

These are a small but important group of neuter nouns:

**Время летит быстро.** *(Vremya letit bystro.)* — Time flies quickly.

**Имя мальчика — Иван.** *(Imya mal'chika — Ivan.)* — The boy's name is Ivan.

**Пламя горело ярко.** *(Plamya gorelo yarko.)* — The flame burned brightly.

**Знамя развевалось на ветру.** *(Znamya razvevalos' na vetru.)* — The banner fluttered in the wind.

**Племя жило в лесу.** *(Plemya zhilo v lesu.)* — The tribe lived in the forest.

---

## 5. The Soft-Sign Problem: -ь Nouns

The soft sign **-ь** does not tell you the gender. You must memorize whether each **-ь** noun is masculine or feminine.

| Masculine -ь nouns | Feminine -ь nouns |
|---|---|
| словарь (dictionary) | тетрадь (notebook) |
| учитель (teacher) | ночь (night) |
| автомобиль (car) | дверь (door) |
| дождь (rain) | жизнь (life) |
| гость (guest) | мышь (mouse) |
| рубль (ruble) | кровать (bed) |
| огонь (fire) | площадь (square) |
| день (day) | осень (autumn) |

> **Tip:** Many **-ь** nouns referring to male people or male animals are masculine (учитель, царь). Many abstract or nature-related **-ь** nouns are feminine (ночь, осень, жизнь).

**День был солнечным.** *(Den' byl solnechnym.)* — The day was sunny.

**Осень — моё любимое время года.** *(Osen' — moyo lyubimoye vremya goda.)* — Autumn is my favorite season.

**Огонь горит в камине.** *(Ogon' gorit v kamine.)* — The fire burns in the fireplace.

**Площадь города очень большая.** *(Ploshchad' goroda ochen' bol'shaya.)* — The city's square is very large.

**Кровать стоит у стены.** *(Krovat' stoit u steny.)* — The bed stands by the wall.

## 6. Adjective Agreement with Gender

Adjectives **must agree** with the noun they modify in gender (and also number and case). In the nominative case, adjective endings follow a clear pattern.

### Adjective Ending Table (Nominative Case)

| Gender | Hard Stem | Soft Stem | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Masculine | -ый / -ой | -ий | новый, большой, синий |
| Feminine | -ая | -яя | новая, большая, синяя |
| Neuter | -ое | -ее | новое, большое, синее |

> **Note:** The **-ой** ending is used when the stress falls on the ending: большо́й, молодо́й.

**Новый дом построен.** *(Novyy dom postroyen.)* — The new house is built.

**Новая машина стоит дорого.** *(Novaya mashina stoit dorogo.)* — The new car is expensive.

**Новое платье очень красивое.** *(Novoye plat'ye ochen' krasivoye.)* — The new dress is very beautiful.

**Синий костюм висит в шкафу.** *(Siniy kostyum visit v shkafu.)* — The blue suit hangs in the closet.

**Синяя ручка лежит на столе.** *(Sinyaya ruchka lezhit na stole.)* — The blue pen is on the table.

**Синее небо над городом.** *(Sineye nebo nad gorodom.)* — The blue sky is above the city.

## 7. Agreement in Full Sentences

When you build a sentence, the adjective must match the gender of the noun it describes.

**Старый город очень красивый.** *(Staryy gorod ochen' krasivyy.)* — The old city is very beautiful.

**Старая церковь стоит на холме.** *(Staraya tserkov' stoit na kholme.)* — The old church stands on a hill.

**Старое дерево растёт в саду.** *(Staroye derevo rastyot v sadu.)* — The old tree grows in the garden.

**Русский язык интересный.** *(Russkiy yazyk interesnyy.)* — The Russian language is interesting.

**Русская литература богатая.** *(Russkaya literatura bogataya.)* — Russian literature is rich.

**Русское искусство известно во всём мире.** *(Russkoye iskusstvo izvestno vo vsyom mire.)* — Russian art is known all over the world.

### Past Tense Verb Agreement

Past tense verbs also agree with the subject's gender:

| Gender | Ending | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Masculine | -л | он читал (he read) |
| Feminine | -ла | она читала (she read) |
| Neuter | -ло | оно читало (it read) |

**Мальчик прочитал книгу.** *(Mal'chik prochital knigu.)* — The boy read the book.

**Девочка прочитала книгу.** *(Devochka prochitala knigu.)* — The girl read the book.

**Письмо пришло утром.** *(Pis'mo prishlo utrom.)* — The letter arrived in the morning.

**Кошка убежала из дома.** *(Koshka ubezhala iz doma.)* — The cat ran away from home.

**Солнце взошло над горизонтом.** *(Solntse vzoshlo nad gorizontom.)* — The sun rose above the horizon.

## 8. Common Exceptions and Special Cases

### Masculine Nouns Ending in -а/-я

A few nouns ending in **-а/-я** are actually masculine because they refer to male people:

**Папа пришёл с работы.** *(Papa prishyol s raboty.)* — Dad came from work.

**Дедушка рассказал историю.** *(Dedushka rasskazal istoriyu.)* — Grandfather told a story.

**Дядя живёт в Петербурге.** *(Dyadya zhivyot v Peterburge.)* — Uncle lives in Petersburg.

**Мужчина читал газету.** *(Muzhchina chital gazetu.)* — The man was reading a newspaper.

**Юноша учился в университете.** *(Yunosha uchilsya v universitete.)* — The young man studied at university.

> **Key rule:** These nouns decline like feminine nouns (-а/-я endings) but take masculine adjectives and past tense verbs: **молодой мужчина** (not молодая).

### Indeclinable Foreign Nouns

Some borrowed words do not change form. Their gender must be memorized:

- **кофе** — masculine (hot debate! some accept neuter)
- **метро** — neuter
- **такси** — neuter
- **пальто** — neuter
- **кафе** — neuter

**Кофе был горячим.** *(Kofe byl goryachim.)* — The coffee was hot.

**Метро было закрыто.** *(Metro bylo zakryto.)* — The metro was closed.

**Такси приехало быстро.** *(Taksi priyekhalo bystro.)* — The taxi arrived quickly.

**Пальто висело на вешалке.** *(Pal'to viselo na veshalke.)* — The coat was hanging on the hanger.

**Кафе открылось недавно.** *(Kafe otkrylos' nedavno.)* — The café opened recently.

|---|---|---|

## Key Takeaways

1. **Masculine** nouns typically end in a consonant, **-й**, or sometimes **-ь**.
2. **Feminine** nouns typically end in **-а**, **-я**, or sometimes **-ь**.
3. **Neuter** nouns end in **-о**, **-е**, or **-мя**.
4. Soft-sign (**-ь**) nouns can be either masculine or feminine — memorize them.
5. Adjectives change their ending to match the noun's gender: **-ый/-ой/-ий** (m), **-ая/-яя** (f), **-ое/-ее** (n).
6. Past tense verbs also agree with gender: **-л** (m), **-ла** (f), **-ло** (n).
7. A few nouns ending in **-а/-я** that refer to males are grammatically masculine (папа, дядя).
