# The Russian Alphabet & Sounds

The Russian alphabet, called the **Cyrillic alphabet** (кириллица), has **33 letters**. While some letters look familiar from Latin, many have completely different sounds — these "false friends" are the biggest trap for beginners. This lesson covers every letter, its sound, and the key distinctions you need to start reading Russian.

---

## 2. False Friend Letters

The most confusing aspect for English speakers is that several Russian letters **look like Latin letters but have completely different sounds**. Pay special attention to these:

| Russian Letter | Looks Like | Actually Sounds Like | Example |
|----------------|-----------|----------------------|---------|
| Р р | P | **R** (rolled/trilled) | **река** *(reka)* — river |
| Н н | H | **N** | **нет** *(nyet)* — no |
| С с | C | **S** | **суп** *(sup)* — soup |
| В в | B | **V** | **вечер** *(vecher)* — evening |
| У у | Y | **OO** (as in boot) | **ухо** *(ukho)* — ear |
| Х х | X | **KH** (guttural) | **хорошо** *(khorosho)* — good |
| Е е | E | **YE** | **если** *(yesli)* — if |

Remembering these "false friends" is critical. When you see **РЕСТОРАН**, don't read it as "PECTOPAH" — it's *restoran* (restaurant)!

## 3. Vowels

Russian has **10 vowel letters**, divided into two groups based on how they affect the preceding consonant:

### Hard Vowels

These vowels leave the preceding consonant **hard** (non-palatalized):

| Letter | Sound | Example |
|--------|-------|---------|
| А а | "ah" | **мама** *(mama)* — mom |
| О о | "oh" (when stressed) | **дом** *(dom)* — house |
| У у | "oo" | **стул** *(stul)* — chair |
| Э э | "eh" | **это** *(eto)* — this |
| Ы ы | "ih" (no English equivalent) | **мы** *(my)* — we |

### Soft Vowels

These vowels make the preceding consonant **soft** (palatalized) and add a "y" sound when at the start of a word or after another vowel:

| Letter | Sound | Example |
|--------|-------|---------|
| Я я | "ya" | **яблоко** *(yabloko)* — apple |
| Ё ё | "yo" | **ёж** *(yozh)* — hedgehog |
| Ю ю | "yu" | **юбка** *(yubka)* — skirt |
| Е е | "ye" | **ель** *(yel')* — spruce |
| И и | "ee" | **игра** *(igra)* — game |

Each hard vowel has a soft counterpart: А↔Я, О↔Ё, У↔Ю, Э↔Е, Ы↔И. This pairing is fundamental to Russian phonology and spelling.

## 4. Consonants

Russian has **21 consonant letters**. Most consonants come in **voiced/voiceless pairs**:

| Voiced | Voiceless |
|--------|-----------|
| Б (b) | П (p) |
| В (v) | Ф (f) |
| Г (g) | К (k) |
| Д (d) | Т (t) |
| Ж (zh) | Ш (sh) |
| З (z) | С (s) |

Some consonants have **no pair**:
- **Voiced only**: Л, М, Н, Р, Й — these are called *sonants* and are always voiced
- **Voiceless only**: Х, Ц, Ч, Щ — these are always voiceless

**Мой брат читает книгу.** *(Moy brat chitayet knigu.)* — My brother is reading a book.

**Жук сидит на цветке.** *(Zhuk sidit na tsvetke.)* — The beetle is sitting on a flower.

## 5. The Two Signs: Ъ and Ь

Russian has two unique letters that produce **no sound** on their own but modify the pronunciation of surrounding letters.

### Мягкий знак (Ь) — Soft Sign

The soft sign makes the preceding consonant **soft** (palatalized). Compare:

- **мат** *(mat)* — checkmate → **мать** *(mat')* — mother
- **брат** *(brat)* — brother → **кровать** *(krovat')* — bed
- **угол** *(ugol)* — corner → **уголь** *(ugol')* — coal

The soft sign is extremely common and can appear in the middle or end of a word.

### Твёрдый знак (Ъ) — Hard Sign

The hard sign is rare. It appears between a prefix and a root that starts with Е, Ё, Ю, or Я. It prevents the consonant from merging with the vowel:

- **объект** *(ob-yekt)* — object (the Б stays hard, and Е is pronounced "ye")
- **подъезд** *(pod-yezd)* — entrance/entryway
- **съесть** *(s-yest')* — to eat up

Without the hard sign, the prefix consonant would merge with the vowel and be palatalized.

## 6. Putting It Together

Now that you know all 33 letters, here's a summary of the alphabet's structure:

- **10 vowels**: А, Е, Ё, И, О, У, Ы, Э, Ю, Я
- **21 consonants**: Б, В, Г, Д, Ж, З, Й, К, Л, М, Н, П, Р, С, Т, Ф, Х, Ц, Ч, Ш, Щ
- **2 signs**: Ъ (hard sign), Ь (soft sign)

Key things to remember:
1. Watch out for false friends — Р is R, Н is N, С is S, В is V
2. Hard and soft vowels come in pairs: А↔Я, О↔Ё, У↔Ю, Э↔Е, Ы↔И
3. The soft sign (Ь) softens the preceding consonant; the hard sign (Ъ) keeps it hard before a soft vowel

**Русский язык очень красивый.** *(Russkiy yazyk ochen' krasivyy.)* — The Russian language is very beautiful.
