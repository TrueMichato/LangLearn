# Register & Formality (ты/Вы, formal vs informal)

Russian has a rich system of **register** — the way language changes depending on the social context, relationship between speakers, and level of formality. The most visible marker is the **ты/Вы** distinction, but formality in Russian extends to vocabulary, grammar, greetings, and even names. Understanding register is essential for communicating appropriately.

---

## 1. Ты vs. Вы — The Core Distinction

Russian uses two pronouns for "you":

| Pronoun | Usage | Verb form |
|---|---|---|
| **ты** *(ty)* | informal, singular | 2nd person singular |
| **Вы** *(Vy)* | formal singular / any plural | 2nd person plural |

### When to use ты (informal)

- Friends and peers of similar age
- Family members
- Children (adults always address children as ты)
- Close colleagues (after mutual agreement)
- Animals and pets
- In prayer (addressing God)
- Online/social media among peers

**Ты хочешь кофе?** *(Ty khóchesh kófe?)* — Do you want coffee?

**Как ты себя чувствуешь?** *(Kak ty sebyá chúvstvuyesh?)* — How are you feeling?

**Ты мне очень помог.** *(Ty mne óchen pomóg.)* — You helped me a lot.

### When to use Вы (formal)

- Strangers and acquaintances
- Older people (unless they invite ты)
- Professional settings (boss, client, teacher)
- Official correspondence
- Service situations (shops, offices, doctors)
- When meeting someone for the first time

**Вы говорите по-русски?** *(Vy govoríte po-rússki?)* — Do you speak Russian?

**Как Вас зовут?** *(Kak Vas zovút?)* — What is your name?

**Не могли бы Вы мне помочь?** *(Ne moglí by Vy mne pomóch?)* — Could you help me?

> **Written convention:** When Вы refers to one person formally, it is often capitalized (**Вы, Вас, Вам, Вами**) in letters and official documents. In plural usage ("you all"), it is lowercase.

## 2. Переход на «ты» — Switching to Informal

The transition from Вы to ты is a significant social moment in Russian culture. It signals growing closeness and trust.

### How it happens

The **older** or **higher-status** person typically initiates:

**Давайте на «ты»!** *(Daváyte na «ty»!)* — Let's use «ты»!

**Можно на «ты»?** *(Mózhno na «ty»?)* — Can we switch to «ты»?

**Давай перейдём на «ты».** *(Davay pereydyóm na «ty».)* — Let's switch to «ты».

### Cultural notes

- It's considered **rude** for a younger person to initiate ты with an older person
- In some workplaces, everyone uses ты (especially tech companies, creative industries)
- In traditional workplaces, Вы is maintained even between long-time colleagues
- Switching back from ты to Вы can signal anger, distance, or a breakup

**Мы с тобой уже давно знакомы. Давай на «ты»!** *(My s toboy uzhé davnó znakómy. Davay na «ty»!)* — We've known each other for a long time. Let's use «ты»!

## 3. Names and Forms of Address

Russian has three levels of name formality:

### Full name + patronymic (most formal)

**Иван Петрович** *(Iván Petróvich)* — Ivan, son of Pyotr (formal address for men)

**Мария Ивановна** *(Maríya Ivánovna)* — Maria, daughter of Ivan (formal address for women)

This is used with **Вы** in professional, educational, and formal settings:

**Иван Петрович, можно Вас спросить?** *(Iván Petróvich, mózhno Vas sprosít?)* — Ivan Petrovich, may I ask you?

**Мария Ивановна, спасибо за урок.** *(Maríya Ivánovna, spasíbo za urók.)* — Maria Ivanovna, thank you for the lesson.

### First name only (informal)

**Иван, ты идёшь?** *(Iván, ty idyósh?)* — Ivan, are you coming?

### Diminutive names (intimate/casual)

| Full name | Diminutive | Very casual/affectionate |
|---|---|---|
| Александр | Саша *(Sasha)* | Сашенька, Шура |
| Екатерина | Катя *(Katya)* | Катенька, Катюша |
| Дмитрий | Дима *(Dima)* | Димочка |
| Мария | Маша *(Masha)* | Машенька |
| Иван | Ваня *(Vanya)* | Ванечка |
| Николай | Коля *(Kolya)* | Коленька |

**Саш, ты дома?** *(Sash, ty dóma?)* — Sasha, are you home? (very casual, clipped form)

**Катюша, иди сюда!** *(Katyúsha, idí syudá!)* — Katyusha, come here! (affectionate)

## 4. Formal vs. Informal Greetings

| Formal | Informal | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Здравствуйте! *(Zdrávstvuyte!)* | Привет! *(Privét!)* | Hello! |
| Доброе утро *(Dóbroye útro)* | Доброе утро / Утро! | Good morning |
| До свидания *(Do svidániya)* | Пока! *(Poká!)* | Goodbye |
| Всего доброго *(Vsevó dóbrogo)* | Давай! / Ну, пока! | All the best / Bye! |
| Спокойной ночи *(Spokóynoy nóchi)* | Спокойной! / Ночи! | Good night |
| Простите *(Prostíte)* | Прости *(Prostí)* | Excuse me / Sorry |
| Пожалуйста *(Pozháluysta)* | Пожалуйста | Please / You're welcome |
| Извините *(Izviníte)* | Извини *(Izviní)* | Excuse me |
| Будьте добры *(Búdte dobry)* | — | Be so kind (very polite) |

**Здравствуйте, Анна Сергеевна!** *(Zdrávstvuyte, Ánna Sergéyevna!)* — Hello, Anna Sergeevna! (formal)

**Привет, Аня!** *(Privét, Ánya!)* — Hi, Anya! (informal)

**Извините, Вы не подскажете, где метро?** *(Izviníte, Vy ne podskazhéte, gde metró?)* — Excuse me, could you tell me where the metro is? (polite to stranger)

## 5. Formal vs. Informal Requests

### Formal requests (with Вы)

**Не могли бы Вы мне помочь?** *(Ne moglí by Vy mne pomóch?)* — Could you help me?

**Будьте добры, передайте соль.** *(Búdte dóbry, peredáyte sol.)* — Be so kind, pass the salt.

**Вы не подскажете, который час?** *(Vy ne podskazhéte, kotóryy chas?)* — Could you tell me what time it is?

**Разрешите представиться.** *(Razreshíte predstávitsya.)* — Allow me to introduce myself.

### Informal requests (with ты)

**Помоги мне, пожалуйста.** *(Pomogí mne, pozháluysta.)* — Help me, please.

**Передай соль.** *(Peredáy sol.)* — Pass the salt.

**Скажи, сколько времени?** *(Skazhí, skólko vrémeni?)* — Tell me, what time is it?

**Слушай, я хотел спросить...** *(Slúshay, ya khotél sprosít...)* — Listen, I wanted to ask...

## 6. Register in Vocabulary

Russian has many word pairs where one is formal/literary and the other is colloquial:

| Formal / Neutral | Colloquial / Slang | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| потому что *(potomú chto)* | потому что / из-за того что | because |
| сейчас *(seychás)* | щас *(shchas)* | now |
| хорошо *(khoroshó)* | ладно *(ládno)* | okay/good |
| понимать *(ponimát)* | врубаться *(vrubátsya)* | to understand |
| говорить *(govorít)* | болтать *(boltát)* | to talk |
| есть *(yest)* | жрать *(zhrat)* | to eat (vulgar) |
| деньги *(déngi)* | бабки *(bábki)* | money (slang) |
| лицо *(litsó)* | физиономия *(fizionómiya)* | face (jocular) |
| отлично *(otlíchno)* | класс! / круто! *(klass / krúto)* | excellent / cool |
| этот человек *(étot chelovék)* | этот чувак *(étot chuváK)* | this person / this dude |

**Formal letter:** Уважаемый Иван Петрович, сообщаю Вам, что... *(Uvazhàyemyy Iván Petróvich, soobshcháyu Vam, chto...)* — Dear Ivan Petrovich, I inform you that...

**Text to friend:** Вань, слушай, щас расскажу... *(Van, slúshay, shchas rasskazhú...)* — Vanya, listen, I'll tell you now...

## 7. Register in Written Communication

### Formal letters and emails

**Уважаемый/ая...** *(Uvazhàyemyy/aya...)* — Dear... (formal opening)

**С уважением, ...** *(S uvazhéniyem, ...)* — Sincerely, ... (formal closing)

**Прошу Вас рассмотреть...** *(Proshú Vas rassmotret...)* — I request that you consider...

**В ответ на Ваше письмо...** *(V otvét na Váshe pismó...)* — In response to your letter...

### Informal messages

**Привет!** / **Здарова!** *(Privét! / Zdaróva!)* — Hi! / Yo!

**Ну что, как дела?** *(Nu chto, kak delá?)* — So, how's it going?

**Ладно, давай, пока!** *(Ládno, davay, poká!)* — Okay, see ya!

**Целую!** *(Tselúyu!)* — Kisses! (common casual closing, especially among women)

## 8. Common Situations and Register Choice

| Situation | Register | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Doctor's office | Formal (Вы) | Доктор, у меня болит голова. |
| Ordering in a café | Polite (Вы) | Мне, пожалуйста, кофе. |
| With close friend | Informal (ты) | Слушай, пойдём в кино? |
| Job interview | Very formal (Вы) | Я бы хотел узнать о вакансии. |
| Online forum | Varies | Usually ты among peers |
| Speaking to a child | Informal (ты) | Как тебя зовут, малыш? |
| Police officer | Formal (Вы) | Здравствуйте. Ваши документы? |
| University lecture | Formal (Вы) | Откройте учебники на странице 50. |

## 9. Common Mistakes and Cultural Pitfalls

❌ **Using ты with an older stranger** — This is disrespectful in Russian culture.
✅ Always start with **Вы** unless invited to use ты.

❌ **Using first name only with a teacher or boss** — In traditional settings, use name + patronymic.
✅ **Мария Ивановна, можно вопрос?** — Maria Ivanovna, may I ask a question?

❌ **Mixing ты verb forms with Вы** — Don't say "Вы хочешь" (Вы + ты-form).
✅ **Вы хотите** — Do you (formal) want?

❌ **Using «Привет» in a formal email** — Too casual.
✅ **Здравствуйте** or **Уважаемый/ая** for formal correspondence.

❌ **Saying «Давай на ты» to your boss** — The higher-status person should initiate.
✅ Wait for your boss to suggest it.

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Ты** = informal singular; **Вы** = formal singular or any plural.
2. The transition to **ты** is initiated by the **older or higher-status** person.
3. **Name + patronymic** is the standard formal address (Иван Петрович).
4. Greetings, requests, and vocabulary all shift between formal and informal registers.
5. In formal writing, **Вы/Вас/Вам** is capitalized when addressing one person.
6. When in doubt, use **Вы** — it's always safer to be too formal than too informal.
