# Negation Patterns (не, ни, нет, никогда)

Russian negation is richer and more layered than English. Where English uses one negative word ("I don't know anything"), Russian uses **double** (or even triple) negation: «Я ничего не знаю» — literally "I nothing don't know." Understanding **не**, **ни**, **нет**, and the many **ни-** words is essential to sounding natural in Russian.

---

## 1. Не — The Basic Negation Particle

**Не** *(ne)* is the primary way to negate in Russian. It is placed **directly before** the word it negates:

### Negating the verb

**Я не знаю.** *(Ya ne znayu.)* — I don't know.

**Она не работает сегодня.** *(Ona ne rabotayet segodnya.)* — She isn't working today.

**Мы не ходили в кино.** *(My ne khodili v kino.)* — We didn't go to the movies.

### Negating other words

**Не я это сделал.** *(Ne ya eto sdelal.)* — It wasn't me who did it. (negating subject)

**Я читал не эту книгу.** *(Ya chital ne etu knigu.)* — I read not this book (but another). (negating object)

**Он пришёл не вчера.** *(On prishyol ne vchera.)* — He came not yesterday. (negating adverb)

**Не каждый это поймёт.** *(Ne kazhdyy eto poymyot.)* — Not everyone will understand this.

> The position of **не** determines **what** is being negated — it always negates the word immediately following it.

---

## 2. Нет — "No" and "There Is Not"

**Нет** *(net)* serves two main functions:

### As "no" (opposite of "да")

**— Ты хочешь кофе? — Нет, спасибо.** *(— Ty khochesh kofe? — Net, spasibo.)* — Do you want coffee? — No, thanks.

### As "there is not" / "does not exist" (with genitive)

**Нет** replaces the verb **есть** (there is) in negative existential sentences. The subject goes into the **genitive case**:

**У меня нет времени.** *(U menya net vremeni.)* — I don't have time. (lit. "At me there is not of time.")

**В городе нет метро.** *(V gorode net metro.)* — There is no metro in the city.

**Здесь нет магазина.** *(Zdes net magazina.)* — There is no store here.

**У неё нет брата.** *(U neyó net brata.)* — She doesn't have a brother.

### Past and future of нет

| Tense | Form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Present | нет + gen. | Его нет дома. *(Yegó net dóma.)* — He isn't home. |
| Past | не было + gen. | Его не было дома. *(Yegó ne bylo dóma.)* — He wasn't home. |
| Future | не будет + gen. | Его не будет дома. *(Yegó ne budet dóma.)* — He won't be home. |

**Вчера не было дождя.** *(Vchera ne bylo dozhdya.)* — There was no rain yesterday.

**Завтра не будет занятий.** *(Zavtra ne budet zanyatiy.)* — There will be no classes tomorrow.

## 3. Double Negation with Ни- Words

Russian requires **double negation**: a negative pronoun/adverb (ни-word) **plus** не before the verb. This is grammatically required — it is not "bad grammar" as it would be in standard English.

### The ни- words

| Ни- word | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| никто *(niktó)* | nobody | Никто не пришёл. — Nobody came. |
| ничто / ничего *(nichegó)* | nothing | Я ничего не знаю. — I don't know anything. |
| никогда *(nikogdá)* | never | Она никогда не опаздывает. — She never is late. |
| нигде *(nigdé)* | nowhere | Я нигде не могу найти ключи. — I can't find the keys anywhere. |
| никуда *(nikudá)* | (to) nowhere | Он никуда не ходит. — He doesn't go anywhere. |
| ниоткуда *(niotkúda)* | from nowhere | Помощь ниоткуда не пришла. — Help came from nowhere. |
| никак *(nikák)* | in no way | Никак не могу понять. — I just can't understand. |
| никакой *(nikakóy)* | no (kind of) | Никакой разницы нет. — There's no difference. |
| ничей *(nichéy)* | nobody's | Это ничья кошка. — It's nobody's cat. |

### Examples of double negation

**Никто не знает.** *(Niktó ne znáyet.)* — Nobody knows.

**Я ничего не понимаю.** *(Ya nichevó ne ponimáyu.)* — I don't understand anything.

**Мы никогда не были в Японии.** *(My nikogdá ne byli v Yapónii.)* — We have never been to Japan.

**Он никуда не поехал.** *(On nikudá ne poyékhal.)* — He didn't go anywhere.

**Ничего не случилось.** *(Nichevó ne sluchílos.)* — Nothing happened.

> **Triple negation** is also possible: **Никто никогда ничего не говорил.** — Nobody ever said anything.

## 4. Ни- Words with Prepositions

When a **ни-** pronoun is used with a preposition, the preposition goes **between** ни and the pronoun:

| Without preposition | With preposition | Example |
|---|---|---|
| никто | ни у кого | Ни у кого нет ручки. — Nobody has a pen. |
| ничего | ни о чём | Мы ни о чём не говорили. — We didn't talk about anything. |
| никого (gen/acc) | ни с кем | Она ни с кем не разговаривает. — She doesn't talk with anyone. |

**Я ни к кому не ходил.** *(Ya ni k komú ne khodíl.)* — I didn't visit anyone.

**Он ни на что не жалуется.** *(On ni na chtó ne zháluyetsya.)* — He doesn't complain about anything.

**Мы ни о ком не забыли.** *(My ni o kóm ne zabýli.)* — We didn't forget about anyone.

## 5. Не vs. Ни — The Key Difference

This distinction confuses many learners:

### Не — factual negation ("not")

**Он не был на уроке.** — He was not at the lesson (a simple fact).

**Я не знаю этого человека.** — I don't know this person.

### Ни — emphatic negation / "not a single" / concessive ("no matter")

**Он не сказал ни слова.** *(On ne skazál ni slóva.)* — He didn't say a single word. (emphatic)

**Ни один студент не ответил.** *(Ni odín studént ne otvétil.)* — Not a single student answered.

**Кто бы ни пришёл, мы рады.** *(Kto by ni prishyól, my rády.)* — Whoever comes, we are glad. (concessive)

### Ни... ни... — "neither... nor..."

**Я не люблю ни кофе, ни чай.** *(Ya ne lyublyú ni kófe, ni chay.)* — I like neither coffee nor tea.

**Он не говорит ни по-русски, ни по-английски.** *(On ne govorít ni po-rússki, ni po-anglíyski.)* — He speaks neither Russian nor English.

**Ни мне, ни тебе.** *(Ni mne, ni tebé.)* — Neither for me nor for you.

## 6. Нечего / Некого — "There Is Nothing/Nobody to..."

These special forms express the **absence of possibility**. They are used in impersonal constructions with the **dative** case for the person:

| Form | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| нечего *(néchego)* | there's nothing to | Мне нечего делать. — I have nothing to do. |
| некого *(nékogo)* | there's nobody to | Мне некого спросить. — I have nobody to ask. |
| негде *(négde)* | there's nowhere (location) | Нам негде жить. — We have nowhere to live. |
| некуда *(nékuda)* | there's nowhere (direction) | Ему некуда идти. — He has nowhere to go. |
| некогда *(nékogda)* | there's no time to | Мне некогда. — I have no time. |

> **Stress matters!** Не́когда (stressed on не) = "there's no time." Никогда́ (stressed on -да) = "never."

**Нам не о чем говорить.** *(Nam ne o chem govorít.)* — We have nothing to talk about.

**Ей не с кем поговорить.** *(Yey ne s kem pogovorít.)* — She has nobody to talk to.

**Здесь некуда сесть.** *(Zdes nékuda sest.)* — There's nowhere to sit here.

## 7. Genitive Case After Negation

In negative sentences, the **accusative** object often shifts to the **genitive** case, especially with:

### Abstract or uncountable nouns

**Я не пью молока.** *(Ya ne pyú moloká.)* — I don't drink milk. (genitive)

**Она не чувствует боли.** *(Oná ne chúvstvuyet bóli.)* — She doesn't feel pain.

### Complete negation of existence

**Я не видел этого фильма.** *(Ya ne vídel étogo fílma.)* — I haven't seen this movie. (genitive = never)

Compare: **Я не видел этот фильм.** *(Ya ne vídel étot film.)* — I didn't see this movie (this time). (accusative = specific occasion)

> The genitive after negation emphasizes **complete absence**, while the accusative emphasizes a **specific instance**.

## 8. Common Mistakes

❌ **Никто пришёл.** — Missing не before the verb.
✅ **Никто не пришёл.** — Nobody came.

❌ **Я не видел ничего.** — Word order: ничего should come before не.
✅ **Я ничего не видел.** — I didn't see anything.

❌ **У меня не есть время.** — Don't use не есть; use нет.
✅ **У меня нет времени.** — I don't have time.

❌ **Он не говорит не русский, не английский.** — Wrong: use ни...ни for "neither...nor."
✅ **Он не говорит ни по-русски, ни по-английски.** — He speaks neither Russian nor English.

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Не** is the basic negation particle, placed directly before the negated word.
2. **Нет** means "no" and "there is not" (with genitive case).
3. Russian requires **double negation**: ни-word + не + verb.
4. **Ни** adds emphasis ("not a single") or means "neither...nor."
5. **Нечего/некого** express absence of possibility ("nothing/nobody to...").
6. After negation, objects often shift from accusative to **genitive** case.
7. Prepositions split ни- words: ни у кого, ни о чём, ни с кем.
