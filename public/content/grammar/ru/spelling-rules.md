# Russian Spelling Rules

Russian spelling follows a few strict rules that override the "expected" endings you might predict from grammar tables. These rules exist because of how certain consonants interact with certain vowels — some combinations are simply impossible in Russian. Understanding these rules is essential because they affect **noun plurals**, **adjective endings**, **verb conjugations**, and **case endings** throughout the language.

---

## 1. The 7-Letter Rule (Правило семи букв)

This is the most important spelling rule in Russian:

> **After К, Г, Х, Ж, Ш, Щ, Ч — never write Ы, always write И.**

The letters **к, г, х** are called *velars* (back-of-mouth consonants), and **ж, ш, щ, ч** are called *hushing consonants* (шипящие). None of them can be followed by Ы — you must use И instead.

### Why It Matters: Noun Plurals

The normal nominative plural ending for feminine nouns is **-ы**. But when the stem ends in one of the 7 letters, you write **-и** instead:

| Singular | Expected Plural | Actual Plural | Rule Applied |
|----------|----------------|---------------|--------------|
| **кни́га** *(kníga)* — book | *книгы | **кни́ги** *(knígi)* | Г + Ы → Г + И |
| **ко́шка** *(kóshka)* — cat | *кошкы | **ко́шки** *(kóshki)* | К + Ы → К + И |
| **руба́шка** *(rubáshka)* — shirt | *рубашкы | **руба́шки** *(rubáshki)* | К + Ы → К + И |
| **но́жка** *(nózhka)* — little leg | *ножкы | **но́жки** *(nózhki)* | К + Ы → К + И |

Compare with a noun that does NOT end in one of the 7 letters:

- **ла́мпа** *(lámpa)* — lamp → **ла́мпы** *(lámpy)* — lamps (П is not one of the 7, so Ы is fine)

### Why It Matters: Adjective Endings

The 7-letter rule also affects adjective endings. The masculine nominative ending **-ый** becomes **-ий** after the 7 letters:

- **но́вый** *(nóvyy)* — new (В is not one of the 7, so -ый)
- **ру́сский** *(rússkiy)* — Russian (К is one of the 7, so -ий)
- **хоро́ший** *(khoróshiy)* — good (Ш is one of the 7, so -ий)
- **ти́хий** *(tíkhiy)* — quiet (Х is one of the 7, so -ий)
- **стро́гий** *(strógiy)* — strict (Г is one of the 7, so -ий)

**Ру́сский язы́к — краси́вый язы́к.** *(Rússkiy yazýk — krasívyy yazýk.)* — Russian is a beautiful language.

**Хоро́шие кни́ги лежа́т на по́лке.** *(Khoróshiye knígi lezhát na pólke.)* — Good books are on the shelf.

## 2. The 5-Letter Rule (Правило пяти букв)

The second major spelling rule:

> **After Ж, Ш, Ч, Щ, Ц — never write unstressed О, write Е instead.**

This rule applies only to the *hushing consonants* (ж, ш, ч, щ) plus **ц**. When the О in an ending would be **unstressed**, you must replace it with **Е**.

If the О is **stressed**, it stays as О.

### Stressed О Is Kept

In the instrumental case, the ending **-ом/-ем** can be stressed. When it is stressed after one of the 5 letters, you write О:

- **нож** *(nozh)* — knife → **ножо́м** *(nazhóm)* — with a knife (stressed О ✓)
- **карандаш** *(karandash)* — pencil → **карандашо́м** *(karandashóm)* — with a pencil (stressed О ✓)
- **отцо́м** *(ottsóm)* — with a father (stressed О after Ц ✓)

### Unstressed О → Е

But when the same ending is **unstressed**, you write Е instead:

- **муж** *(muzh)* — husband → **му́жем** *(múzhem)* — with a husband (unstressed → Е)
- **то́вари́щем** *(továrischem)* — with a comrade (unstressed → Е)
- **поле́нцем** *(palyéntsem)* — with a log (unstressed → Е after Ц)

### Practical Comparison

| Stressed → О | Unstressed → Е |
|-------------|---------------|
| **ножо́м** *(nazhóm)* — with a knife | **му́жем** *(múzhem)* — with a husband |
| **мячо́м** *(myachóm)* — with a ball | **за́йцем** *(záytsem)* — with a hare |
| **плечо́м** *(plechóm)* — with a shoulder | **се́рдцем** *(sértsem)* — with a heart |

The key is to check: **Is the ending stressed?** If yes → О is fine. If no → write Е.

## 3. The Ц Rule

The letter **Ц** has its own additional restriction:

> **After Ц — never write Ю or Я, write У and А instead.**

This means:
- Ц + ~~Ю~~ → **ЦУ**
- Ц + ~~Я~~ → **ЦА**

### Examples

- **у́лица** *(úlitsa)* — street (ЦА, not *ЦЯ)
- **цари́ца** *(tsaritsa)* — tsarina (ЦА, not *ЦЯ)
- **огурцы́** *(ogurtsý)* — cucumbers (ЦЫ — note: Ц is also an exception to the 7-letter rule in some forms!)
- **отцу́** *(ottsú)* — to the father (dative: ЦУ, not *ЦЮ)
- **ку́рицу** *(kúritsu)* — chicken (accusative: ЦУ, not *ЦЮ)

### Exceptions

A small number of borrowed words and proper names may break this rule:

- **Цюрих** *(Tsyúrikh)* — Zurich
- **Цявловский** *(Tsyavlóvskiy)* — a surname

These exceptions are rare and mostly limited to foreign words and proper names.

## 4. Why These Rules Matter

These spelling rules are not just arbitrary conventions — they explain many of the "exceptions" you'll encounter when learning Russian grammar.

### They Simplify Declension

When you learn that the nominative plural ending is **-ы/-и**, you now know the rule: use -и after к, г, х, ж, ш, щ, ч. There's no need to memorize individual "exceptions" — they all follow the same spelling rule.

### They Simplify Conjugation

Verb endings follow the same rules:

- **писа́ть** *(pisát')* — to write → **я пишу́** *(ya pishú)* (Ш + У, not *ШЮ)
- **учи́ть** *(uchít')* — to teach → **я учу́** *(ya uchú)* (Ч + У, not *ЧЮ)
- **слы́шать** *(slýshat')* — to hear → **я слы́шу** *(ya slýshu)* (Ш + У, not *ШЮ)

### They Explain Adjective Endings

Adjective declension tables often seem to have different endings after different stems. But the differences all follow from these same rules:

- **но́вого** *(nóvovo)* — of new (standard genitive ending)
- **хоро́шего** *(khoróshevo)* — of good (after Ш: unstressed О → Е)
- **большо́го** *(bol'shóvo)* — of big (after Ш: stressed О stays)

## 5. Summary of All Three Rules

| Rule | After These Letters | Never Write | Write Instead |
|------|-------------------|-------------|---------------|
| **7-letter rule** | К, Г, Х, Ж, Ш, Щ, Ч | Ы | И |
| **5-letter rule** | Ж, Ш, Ч, Щ, Ц | unstressed О | Е |
| **Ц rule** | Ц | Ю, Я | У, А |

A helpful mnemonic for the 7 letters: **Г**де **Ж**е **Ш**ёл **Ч**ижик, **Щ**ипая **Х**леб с **К**олбасой? — Where did the siskin walk, nibbling bread with sausage? (Each word starts with one of the 7 letters.)

**Э́ти пра́вила ну́жно за́помнить.** *(Éti právila núzhno zápomnit'.)* — These rules must be memorized.
