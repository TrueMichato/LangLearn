# Genitive Case (Родительный падеж)

The **genitive case** (родительный падеж) is one of the most frequently used cases in Russian. It answers the questions **Кого?** (Of whom?) and **Чего?** (Of what?). The genitive expresses possession, absence, quantity, and is required after many prepositions. Once you master the genitive, a huge portion of Russian grammar falls into place.

---

## 1. Genitive Endings

### Singular Endings

| Gender | Nominative Ending | Genitive Ending | Example: Nom → Gen |
|---|---|---|---|
| Masc. (consonant) | — | -а | стол → стол**а** |
| Masc. (-й) | -й | -я | музей → музе**я** |
| Masc. (-ь) | -ь | -я | словарь → словар**я** |
| Fem. (-а) | -а | -ы | книга → книг**и** ¹ |
| Fem. (-я) | -я | -и | песня → песн**и** |
| Fem. (-ь) | -ь | -и | дверь → двер**и** |
| Neuter (-о) | -о | -а | окно → окн**а** |
| Neuter (-е) | -е | -я | море → мор**я** |
| Neuter (-мя) | -мя | -мени | время → врем**ени** |

> ¹ After **г, к, х, ж, ш, щ, ч** the ending is **-и** (not **-ы**): книга → книг**и**

### Plural Endings

| Gender | Nominative Sg. | Genitive Pl. | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Masc. (consonant) | стол | стол**ов** | пять столов |
| Masc. (-й) | музей | музе**ев** | пять музеев |
| Masc. (-ь) | словарь | словар**ей** | пять словарей |
| Fem. (-а) | книга | книг (zero) | пять книг |
| Fem. (-я) | песня | пес**ен** | пять песен |
| Fem. (-ь) | дверь | двер**ей** | пять дверей |
| Neuter (-о) | окно | ок**он** (zero) | пять окон |
| Neuter (-е) | море | мор**ей** | пять морей |

> The genitive plural is one of the trickiest forms in Russian — there are many patterns and exceptions. Focus on the most common words first.

## 2. Possession (Whose?)

The genitive case expresses **possession**, similar to English "'s" or "of."

**Книга студента лежит на столе.** *(Kniga studenta lezhit na stole.)* — The student's book is on the table.

**Дом брата очень большой.** *(Dom brata ochen' bol'shoy.)* — The brother's house is very big.

**Машина отца стоит во дворе.** *(Mashina ottsa stoit vo dvore.)* — Father's car is in the yard.

**Телефон сестры зазвонил.** *(Telefon sestry zazvonil.)* — The sister's phone rang.

**Имя учителя — Иван Петрович.** *(Imya uchitelya — Ivan Petrovich.)* — The teacher's name is Ivan Petrovich.

**Цвет неба изменился.** *(Tsvet neba izmenilsya.)* — The color of the sky changed.

> **Word order:** In Russian, the possessor (genitive noun) usually comes **after** the thing possessed: книга студента (the book of the student).

## 3. Absence with Нет

The genitive is used with **нет** (there is no / there isn't) to express absence. The absent thing goes into the genitive case.

**Structure:** Нет + genitive noun

**У меня нет машины.** *(U menya net mashiny.)* — I don't have a car.

**В комнате нет стола.** *(V komnate net stola.)* — There is no table in the room.

**Нет времени на отдых.** *(Net vremeni na otdykh.)* — There is no time for rest.

**У неё нет брата.** *(U neyo net brata.)* — She doesn't have a brother.

**Здесь нет воды.** *(Zdes' net vody.)* — There is no water here.

**В городе нет метро.** *(V gorode net metro.)* — There is no metro in the city.

### Past and Future Tense of Absence

- **Past:** не было + genitive
- **Future:** не будет + genitive

**Вчера не было дождя.** *(Vchera ne bylo dozhdya.)* — There was no rain yesterday.

**Завтра не будет занятий.** *(Zavtra ne budet zanyatiy.)* — There will be no classes tomorrow.

**Раньше здесь не было магазина.** *(Ran'she zdes' ne bylo magazina.)* — There used to be no store here.

**На вечеринке не было музыки.** *(Na vecherinke ne bylo muzyki.)* — There was no music at the party.

**В следующем году не будет экзамена.** *(V sleduyushchem godu ne budet ekzamena.)* — There will be no exam next year.

## 4. Numbers and the Genitive

Russian numbers interact with the genitive case in a specific pattern:

| Number | Case Required | Example |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Nominative singular | одна книга |
| 2, 3, 4 | Genitive **singular** | две книги, три стола |
| 5–20 | Genitive **plural** | пять книг, десять столов |
| 21 | Nominative singular | двадцать одна книга |
| 22–24 | Genitive singular | двадцать два стола |
| 25–30 | Genitive plural | двадцать пять столов |

**Два студента сидят в классе.** *(Dva studenta sidyat v klasse.)* — Two students are sitting in class.

**У меня три книги.** *(U menya tri knigi.)* — I have three books.

**В комнате пять окон.** *(V komnate pyat' okon.)* — There are five windows in the room.

**Она купила четыре яблока.** *(Ona kupila chetyre yabloka.)* — She bought four apples.

**Он прочитал десять страниц.** *(On prochital desyat' stranits.)* — He read ten pages.

**В городе двадцать один музей.** *(V gorode dvadtsat' odin muzey.)* — There are twenty-one museums in the city.

> **Key insight:** After 2, 3, 4 — use genitive **singular**. After 5 and above — use genitive **plural**. This is one of the most common mistakes for learners.

## 5. Prepositions with the Genitive

Many common prepositions require the genitive case.

### Из (from, out of)

**Он из России.** *(On iz Rossii.)* — He is from Russia.

**Она вышла из дома.** *(Ona vyshla iz doma.)* — She went out of the house.

**Мы приехали из Москвы.** *(My priyekhali iz Moskvy.)* — We came from Moscow.

**Сок из апельсинов очень вкусный.** *(Sok iz apel'sinov ochen' vkusnyy.)* — Orange juice is very tasty.

**Он достал книгу из сумки.** *(On dostal knigu iz sumki.)* — He took the book out of the bag.

### От (from a person/source)

**Я получил письмо от друга.** *(Ya poluchil pis'mo ot druga.)* — I received a letter from a friend.

**Аптека недалеко от дома.** *(Apteka nedaleko ot doma.)* — The pharmacy is not far from the house.

**От мамы пришло сообщение.** *(Ot mamy prishlo soobshcheniye.)* — A message came from mom.

**Мы живём далеко от центра.** *(My zhivyom daleko ot tsentra.)* — We live far from the center.

**Он отошёл от окна.** *(On otoshyol ot okna.)* — He stepped away from the window.

### До (until, up to, before)

**Я работаю до пяти часов.** *(Ya rabotayu do pyati chasov.)* — I work until five o'clock.

**От дома до школы десять минут.** *(Ot doma do shkoly desyat' minut.)* — From home to school is ten minutes.

**До встречи!** *(Do vstrechi!)* — Until we meet! / See you!

**Мы доехали до вокзала.** *(My doyekhali do vokzala.)* — We drove to the train station.

**До конца урока пять минут.** *(Do kontsa uroka pyat' minut.)* — There are five minutes until the end of the lesson.

### Без (without)

**Я пью кофе без сахара.** *(Ya p'yu kofe bez sakhara.)* — I drink coffee without sugar.

**Он пришёл без зонта.** *(On prishyol bez zonta.)* — He came without an umbrella.

**Без воды нельзя жить.** *(Bez vody nel'zya zhit'.)* — One can't live without water.

**Она вышла без куртки.** *(Ona vyshla bez kurtki.)* — She went out without a jacket.

**Мы справились без помощи.** *(My spravilis' bez pomoshchi.)* — We managed without help.

### У (at, by / possession)

**У** + genitive is the standard way to express **having** something in Russian.

**У меня есть собака.** *(U menya yest' sobaka.)* — I have a dog.

**У студента много книг.** *(U studenta mnogo knig.)* — The student has many books.

**У брата новая машина.** *(U brata novaya mashina.)* — My brother has a new car.

**У бабушки болит голова.** *(U babushki bolit golova.)* — Grandmother has a headache.

**У окна стоит стул.** *(U okna stoit stul.)* — A chair stands by the window.

## 6. Partitive Genitive

The genitive is used for **a part of** or **some of** a substance (partitive meaning).

**Дайте мне стакан воды.** *(Dayte mne stakan vody.)* — Give me a glass of water.

**Он выпил чашку чая.** *(On vypil chashku chaya.)* — He drank a cup of tea.

**Купи килограмм сахара.** *(Kupi kilogramm sakhara.)* — Buy a kilogram of sugar.

**Я хочу кусок хлеба.** *(Ya khochu kusok khleba.)* — I want a piece of bread.

**Она налила тарелку супа.** *(Ona nalila tarelku supa.)* — She poured a plate of soup.

**Добавь немного соли.** *(Dobav' nemnogo soli.)* — Add a little salt.

> Some masculine nouns have a special **partitive genitive** ending in **-у/-ю**: чаю (some tea), сахару (some sugar), сыру (some cheese). This is informal and optional — the standard genitive is always acceptable.

## 7. Genitive with Quantity Words

Besides numbers, several quantity words also require the genitive:

| Word | Meaning | Case Required |
|---|---|---|
| много | many, much | Genitive plural |
| мало | few, little | Genitive plural |
| несколько | several | Genitive plural |
| сколько | how many | Genitive plural |
| немного | a little | Genitive singular/plural |

**У него много друзей.** *(U nego mnogo druzey.)* — He has many friends.

**В городе мало парков.** *(V gorode malo parkov.)* — There are few parks in the city.

**Несколько студентов опоздали.** *(Neskol'ko studentov opozdali.)* — Several students were late.

**Сколько у тебя братьев?** *(Skol'ko u tebya brat'yev?)* — How many brothers do you have?

**Здесь немного воды.** *(Zdes' nemnogo vody.)* — There is a little water here.

**На столе много книг.** *(Na stole mnogo knig.)* — There are many books on the table.

## 8. Genitive of Adjectives

When a noun is in the genitive, its adjective must also be in the genitive.

### Adjective Genitive Endings (Singular)

| Gender | Nominative | Genitive |
|---|---|---|
| Masculine | новый | нов**ого** |
| Feminine | новая | нов**ой** |
| Neuter | новое | нов**ого** |

> **Pronunciation note:** The ending **-ого/-его** is pronounced as **-ovo/-yevo** (г sounds like в).

**Книга нового студента на столе.** *(Kniga novogo studenta na stole.)* — The new student's book is on the table.

**Дом старого друга далеко.** *(Dom starogo druga daleko.)* — The old friend's house is far away.

**Без горячей воды трудно.** *(Bez goryachey vody trudno.)* — It's hard without hot water.

**Из большого города трудно уехать.** *(Iz bol'shogo goroda trudno uyekhat'.)* — It's hard to leave a big city.

**У молодой учительницы красивый голос.** *(U molodoy uchitel'nitsy krasivyy golos.)* — The young teacher has a beautiful voice.

**Цена нового телефона высокая.** *(Tsena novogo telefona vysokaya.)* — The price of the new phone is high.

## 9. Common Expressions with the Genitive

Many everyday phrases use the genitive case:

**Который час? — Сейчас два часа.** *(Kotoryy chas? — Seychas dva chasa.)* — What time is it? — It's two o'clock.

**Сегодня первое января.** *(Segodnya pervoye yanvarya.)* — Today is January 1st.

**Он боится темноты.** *(On boitsya temnoty.)* — He is afraid of the dark.

**Она старше брата.** *(Ona starshe brata.)* — She is older than her brother.

**Мне не хватает времени.** *(Mne ne khvatayet vremeni.)* — I don't have enough time.

**Чашка кофе стоит на столе.** *(Chashka kofe stoit na stole.)* — A cup of coffee is on the table.

## Key Takeaways

1. The genitive answers **Кого? Чего?** (Of whom? Of what?).
2. Main endings: masculine **-а/-я**, feminine **-ы/-и**, neuter **-а/-я**.
3. Use genitive for **possession**: книга студент**а** (the student's book).
4. Use genitive for **absence**: нет вод**ы** (there is no water).
5. After **2–4** use genitive singular; after **5+** use genitive plural.
6. Common prepositions: **из, от, до, без, у** all require genitive.
7. Quantity words (**много, мало, сколько**) take genitive plural.
8. Adjective genitive endings: masculine/neuter **-ого/-его**, feminine **-ой/-ей**.
