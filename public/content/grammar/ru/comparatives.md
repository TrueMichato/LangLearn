# Comparatives & Superlatives

When you want to say something is bigger, better, more interesting, or the most beautiful, you need comparatives and superlatives. Russian has both **simple** (synthetic) and **compound** (analytic) forms for each. This lesson covers all the patterns, irregular forms, and practical usage.

---

## Simple Comparative with -ее / -ей

The most common way to form the comparative is by adding **-ее** (or the colloquial **-ей**) to the adjective stem. This form does not change for gender, number, or case — it is **indeclinable**.

### Formation

Take the adjective stem and add **-ее**:

- красивый → красив **ее** *(krasivyy → krasiveye)* — more beautiful
- интересный → интересн **ее** *(interesnyy → interesneye)* — more interesting
- сильный → сильн **ее** *(sil'nyy → sil'neye)* — stronger
- тёплый → тепл **ее** *(tyoplyy → tepleye)* — warmer
- умный → умн **ее** *(umnyy → umneye)* — smarter

### Examples

- **Эта книга интереснее, чем та.** *(Eta kniga interesneye, chem ta.)* — This book is more interesting than that one.
- **Сегодня теплее, чем вчера.** *(Segodnya tepleye, chem vchera.)* — Today is warmer than yesterday.
- **Мой брат сильнее меня.** *(Moy brat sil'neye menya.)* — My brother is stronger than me.
- **Русский язык труднее английского.** *(Russkiy yazyk trudneye angliyskogo.)* — Russian is harder than English.
- **Она говорит быстрее всех.** *(Ona govorit bystreye vsekh.)* — She speaks faster than everyone.
- **Жизнь в деревне спокойнее.** *(Zhizn' v derevne spokoyneye.)* — Life in the countryside is calmer.

> **Colloquial note:** In everyday speech, Russians often use **-ей** instead of **-ее**: интересней, сильней, быстрей. Both forms are correct, but **-ее** is more formal.

## Consonant-Stem Changes

Many common adjectives have **consonant changes** in the comparative form. Instead of -ее, they use **-е** with a stem consonant mutation.

### Common Patterns

| Change | Adjective | Comparative | Translation |
|--------|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| г → ж | дорогой | дороже | more expensive |
| к → ч | лёгкий | легче | easier/lighter |
| х → ш | тихий | тише | quieter |
| ст → щ | простой | проще | simpler |
| т → ч | богатый | богаче | richer |
| д → ж | молодой | моложе | younger |
| з → ж | близкий | ближе | closer |
| с → ш | высокий | выше | taller/higher |

### Examples

- **Этот ресторан дороже того.** *(Etot restoran dorozhe togo.)* — This restaurant is more expensive than that one.
- **Математика легче физики.** *(Matematika legche fiziki.)* — Math is easier than physics.
- **Говори тише, ребёнок спит.** *(Govori tishe, rebyonok spit.)* — Speak more quietly, the child is sleeping.
- **Это задание проще предыдущего.** *(Eto zadaniye proshche predydushchego.)* — This task is simpler than the previous one.
- **Он моложе своей жены на два года.** *(On molozhe svoyey zheny na dva goda.)* — He is two years younger than his wife.
- **Наш дом ближе к центру.** *(Nash dom blizhe k tsentru.)* — Our house is closer to the center.

## Irregular Comparatives

Some of the most frequently used adjectives have **completely irregular** comparative forms. These must simply be memorized.

| Adjective | Meaning | Comparative | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|-------------|---------|
| хороший | good | лучше | better |
| плохой | bad | хуже | worse |
| большой | big | больше | bigger/more |
| маленький | small | меньше | smaller/less |
| старый | old | старше | older (people) |
| высокий | tall | выше | taller |
| низкий | low/short | ниже | lower/shorter |
| широкий | wide | шире | wider |
| узкий | narrow | уже | narrower |
| далёкий | far | дальше | farther |

### Examples

- **Этот фильм лучше того.** *(Etot fil'm luchshe togo.)* — This film is better than that one.
- **Погода сегодня хуже, чем вчера.** *(Pogoda segodnya khuzhe, chem vchera.)* — The weather today is worse than yesterday.
- **Москва больше Петербурга.** *(Moskva bol'she Peterburga.)* — Moscow is bigger than St. Petersburg.
- **Моя комната меньше твоей.** *(Moya komnata men'she tvoyey.)* — My room is smaller than yours.
- **Мой дедушка старше бабушки.** *(Moy dedushka starshe babushki.)* — My grandfather is older than my grandmother.
- **Эта река шире той.** *(Eta reka shire toy.)* — This river is wider than that one.

> **Note:** **больше** and **меньше** also function as adverbs meaning "more" and "less/fewer": Я хочу **больше** спать. *(I want to sleep more.)*

## Compound Comparative with более / менее

The compound comparative uses **более** (more) or **менее** (less) + the full adjective. This form is **declinable** — the adjective agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun.

### When to Use It

- When you need the comparative in an **oblique case** (not nominative/accusative)
- In formal or written Russian
- When the simple form sounds awkward

### Examples

- **Она более ответственная студентка.** *(Ona boleye otvetstvennaya studentka.)* — She is a more responsible student.
- **Мы живём в более спокойном районе.** *(My zhivyom v boleye spokoynom rayone.)* — We live in a calmer neighborhood.
- **Это менее важный вопрос.** *(Eto meneye vazhnyy vopros.)* — This is a less important question.
- **Он работает в более крупной компании.** *(On rabotayet v boleye krupnoy kompanii.)* — He works at a larger company.
- **Я ищу менее дорогой вариант.** *(Ya ishchu meneye dorogoy variant.)* — I am looking for a less expensive option.
- **Они переехали в более тёплый город.** *(Oni pereyekhali v boleye tyoplyy gorod.)* — They moved to a warmer city.

> **Common mistake:** Do not combine более/менее with the simple comparative. Say **более красивый** or **красивее**, but never ~~более красивее~~.

## Simple Superlative with -ейший / -айший

The simple (synthetic) superlative is formed by adding **-ейший** to the adjective stem (or **-айший** after г, к, х with consonant mutation). It conveys an **emphatic, absolute** degree — "extremely, most" — and is common in literary and formal Russian.

### Formation

- красивый → красив**ейший** *(krasiveyshiy)* — most beautiful
- умный → умн**ейший** *(umneyshiy)* — most intelligent
- сильный → сильн**ейший** *(sil'neyshiy)* — strongest
- высокий → выс**очайший** *(vysochayshiy)* — highest (к → ч + -айший)
- строгий → строж**айший** *(strozhayshiy)* — strictest (г → ж + -айший)
- близкий → ближ**айший** *(blizhayshiy)* — nearest (з → ж + -айший)

### Examples

- **Это красивейший город в мире.** *(Eto krasiveyshiy gorod v mire.)* — This is the most beautiful city in the world.
- **Он умнейший человек, которого я знаю.** *(On umneyshiy chelovek, kotorogo ya znayu.)* — He is the smartest person I know.
- **Это важнейший вопрос нашего времени.** *(Eto vazhneyshiy vopros nashego vremeni.)* — This is the most important question of our time.
- **Где ближайшая аптека?** *(Gde blizhayshaya apteka?)* — Where is the nearest pharmacy?
- **Она добрейший человек.** *(Ona dobreyshiy chelovek.)* — She is the kindest person.

> **Usage note:** The -ейший/-айший superlative often expresses an **emphatic/absolute** meaning ("extremely") rather than a strict comparison. For clear comparison ("the biggest of all"), use the compound superlative.

## Compound Superlative with самый

The compound superlative is formed with **самый** + adjective. This is the most common superlative in everyday Russian. Both **самый** and the adjective **decline** together.

### Formation

- самый большой *(samyy bol'shoy)* — the biggest
- самая красивая *(samaya krasivaya)* — the most beautiful
- самое интересное *(samoye interesnoye)* — the most interesting
- самые важные *(samyye vazhnyye)* — the most important (plural)

### Examples

- **Байкал — самое глубокое озеро в мире.** *(Baykal — samoye glubokoye ozero v mire.)* — Baikal is the deepest lake in the world.
- **Москва — самый большой город в России.** *(Moskva — samyy bol'shoy gorod v Rossii.)* — Moscow is the biggest city in Russia.
- **Это самая вкусная пицца, которую я ел.** *(Eto samaya vkusnaya pitstsa, kotoruyu ya yel.)* — This is the most delicious pizza I've ever eaten.
- **Он живёт в самом красивом доме на улице.** *(On zhivyot v samom krasivom dome na ulitse.)* — He lives in the most beautiful house on the street.
- **Это были самые счастливые дни моей жизни.** *(Eto byli samyye schastlivyye dni moyey zhizni.)* — Those were the happiest days of my life.

> **Alternative:** You can also form the superlative with **наиболее** (most) and **наименее** (least) — these are bookish/formal: наиболее эффективный метод *(the most effective method)*.

## Comparing with чем and the Genitive

There are **two ways** to express "than" in Russian comparisons:

### 1. Using чем (than)

The nouns being compared stay in the **same case**:

- **Москва больше, чем Париж.** *(Moskva bol'she, chem Parizh.)* — Moscow is bigger than Paris.
- **Зима холоднее, чем осень.** *(Zima kholodneye, chem osen'.)* — Winter is colder than autumn.
- **Он бегает быстрее, чем я.** *(On begayet bystreye, chem ya.)* — He runs faster than me.

### 2. Using the Genitive (without чем)

The second noun goes into the **genitive case**:

- **Москва больше Парижа.** *(Moskva bol'she Parizha.)* — Moscow is bigger than Paris.
- **Зима холоднее осени.** *(Zima kholodneye oseni.)* — Winter is colder than autumn.
- **Он бегает быстрее меня.** *(On begayet bystreye menya.)* — He runs faster than me.

### More Examples

- **Эта задача сложнее, чем предыдущая.** *(Eta zadacha slozhneye, chem predydushchaya.)* — This problem is harder than the previous one.
- **Кофе дороже чая.** *(Kofe dorozhe chaya.)* — Coffee is more expensive than tea.
- **Мой дом дальше от центра, чем твой.** *(Moy dom dal'she ot tsentra, chem tvoy.)* — My house is farther from the center than yours.
- **Она моложе брата на три года.** *(Ona molozhe brata na tri goda.)* — She is three years younger than her brother.
- **Летом дни длиннее ночей.** *(Letom dni dlinneye nochey.)* — In summer, days are longer than nights.

> **Tip:** The genitive construction is shorter and more natural in speech. The чем construction is clearer when the sentence is complex or when the compared items are in oblique cases.

## Summary Table

| Type | Formation | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Simple comparative | stem + -ее/-е | красивее, дороже |
| Compound comparative | более/менее + adj | более красивый |
| Simple superlative | stem + -ейший/-айший | красивейший |
| Compound superlative | самый + adj | самый красивый |
| "Than" (option 1) | comparative + чем + nom | лучше, чем... |
| "Than" (option 2) | comparative + genitive | лучше кого/чего |

### Key Points to Remember

1. Simple comparatives are **indeclinable** — they never change form.
2. Compound comparatives with более/менее **decline** with the noun.
3. Never mix simple and compound forms (~~более лучше~~).
4. The -ейший/-айший superlative is emphatic/literary; **самый** is everyday.
5. Irregular comparatives (лучше, хуже, больше, меньше) are extremely common — learn them by heart!
