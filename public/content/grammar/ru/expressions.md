# Common Expressions & Idioms

Knowing grammar rules is one thing — sounding natural is another. This lesson covers the expressions, phrases, and idioms that Russians use every day. From polite greetings to colorful idioms, these will help you communicate naturally and understand native speakers.

---

## Greetings and Farewells

### Formal Greetings

- **Здравствуйте!** *(Zdravstvuyte!)* — Hello! (formal, to multiple people or someone you address as "вы")
- **Доброе утро!** *(Dobroye utro!)* — Good morning!
- **Добрый день!** *(Dobryy den'!)* — Good afternoon!
- **Добрый вечер!** *(Dobryy vecher!)* — Good evening!
- **Рад вас видеть.** *(Rad vas videt'.)* — Glad to see you. (said by a male speaker)

### Informal Greetings

- **Привет!** *(Privet!)* — Hi!
- **Здорово!** *(Zdorovo!)* — Hey! (very casual, among close friends)
- **Как дела?** *(Kak dela?)* — How are you?
- **Что нового?** *(Chto novogo?)* — What's new?
- **Давно не виделись!** *(Davno ne videlis'!)* — Long time no see!

### Formal Farewells

- **До свидания!** *(Do svidaniya!)* — Goodbye!
- **Всего доброго!** *(Vsego dobrogo!)* — All the best!
- **До встречи!** *(Do vstrechi!)* — See you! (until we meet)
- **Спокойной ночи!** *(Spokoynoy nochi!)* — Good night!
- **Приятно было познакомиться.** *(Priyatno bylo poznakomit'sya.)* — It was nice to meet you.

### Informal Farewells

- **Пока!** *(Poka!)* — Bye!
- **Увидимся!** *(Uvidimsya!)* — See you!
- **Удачи!** *(Udachi!)* — Good luck!
- **Бывай!** *(Byvay!)* — Take care! (casual)
- **До скорого!** *(Do skorogo!)* — See you soon!

## Polite Expressions

Politeness is highly valued in Russian culture. These expressions are essential for daily interactions.

### Saying Please and Thank You

- **Пожалуйста.** *(Pozhaluysta.)* — Please / You're welcome.
- **Спасибо!** *(Spasibo!)* — Thank you!
- **Большое спасибо!** *(Bol'shoye spasibo!)* — Thank you very much!
- **Благодарю вас.** *(Blagodaryu vas.)* — I thank you. (very formal)
- **Не за что.** *(Ne za chto.)* — Don't mention it. / You're welcome.

### Apologizing and Excusing

- **Извините.** *(Izvinite.)* — Excuse me / I'm sorry. (formal)
- **Простите.** *(Prostite.)* — Forgive me / I'm sorry. (formal, slightly stronger)
- **Прости.** *(Prosti.)* — Sorry. (informal)
- **Извини, я опоздал.** *(Izvini, ya opozdal.)* — Sorry, I'm late. (informal)
- **Мне очень жаль.** *(Mne ochen' zhal'.)* — I'm very sorry. (expressing sympathy)

### Making Polite Requests

- **Будьте добры, помогите мне.** *(Bud'te dobry, pomogite mne.)* — Be so kind, help me.
- **Не могли бы вы повторить?** *(Ne mogli by vy povtorit'?)* — Could you repeat that?
- **Можно вас попросить?** *(Mozhno vas poprosit'?)* — May I ask you (a favor)?
- **Подскажите, пожалуйста.** *(Podskazhite, pozhaluysta.)* — Could you tell me, please.
- **Будьте так любезны.** *(Bud'te tak lyubezny.)* — Be so kind. (very formal)

## Useful Daily Phrases

### Shopping and Prices

- **Сколько стоит?** *(Skol'ko stoit?)* — How much does it cost?
- **Сколько с меня?** *(Skol'ko s menya?)* — How much do I owe?
- **Можно посмотреть?** *(Mozhno posmotret'?)* — May I have a look?
- **У вас есть...?** *(U vas yest'...?)* — Do you have...?
- **Дайте, пожалуйста, два килограмма яблок.** *(Dayte, pozhaluysta, dva kilogramma yablok.)* — Give me two kilograms of apples, please.

### Asking for Directions

- **Где находится...?** *(Gde nakhoditsya...?)* — Where is ... located?
- **Как пройти до метро?** *(Kak proyti do metro?)* — How do I get to the metro?
- **Это далеко отсюда?** *(Eto daleko otsyuda?)* — Is it far from here?
- **Идите прямо, потом направо.** *(Idite pryamo, potom napravo.)* — Go straight, then turn right.
- **Подскажите, где ближайшая аптека?** *(Podskazhite, gde blizhayshaya apteka?)* — Could you tell me where the nearest pharmacy is?

### At a Restaurant

- **Можно меню, пожалуйста?** *(Mozhno menyu, pozhaluysta?)* — May I have the menu, please?
- **Что вы порекомендуете?** *(Chto vy porekomenduyete?)* — What do you recommend?
- **Счёт, пожалуйста.** *(Schyot, pozhaluysta.)* — The check, please.
- **Очень вкусно!** *(Ochen' vkusno!)* — Very delicious!
- **Мне, пожалуйста, кофе с молоком.** *(Mne, pozhaluysta, kofe s molokom.)* — I'd like coffee with milk, please.

## Common Idioms

Russian is rich in idiomatic expressions. Here are some of the most widely used ones.

### Ни пуха ни пера! (Break a leg!)

Literally: "Neither down nor feather!" This is said to wish someone good luck, especially before an exam. The required response is **К чёрту!** *(K chyortu!)* — "To the devil!" (accepting the "reverse" wish).

- **— Завтра у меня экзамен. — Ни пуха ни пера! — К чёрту!** *(— Zavtra u menya ekzamen. — Ni pukha ni pera! — K chyortu!)* — "I have an exam tomorrow." — "Break a leg!" — "Thanks!" (lit. "To the devil!")

### Как рыба в воде (Like a fish in water)

Means feeling completely comfortable and at ease in a situation.

- **Он чувствует себя в Москве как рыба в воде.** *(On chuvstvuyet sebya v Moskve kak ryba v vode.)* — He feels completely at home in Moscow.
- **На сцене она как рыба в воде.** *(Na stsene ona kak ryba v vode.)* — She is in her element on stage.

### Бить баклуши (To beat wooden blocks)

Means to idle, to waste time doing nothing productive.

- **Хватит бить баклуши, иди делать уроки!** *(Khvatit bit' baklushi, idi delat' uroki!)* — Stop wasting time, go do your homework!
- **Он целый день бьёт баклуши.** *(On tselyy den' b'yot baklushi.)* — He wastes the whole day doing nothing.

### Вешать лапшу на уши (To hang noodles on one's ears)

Means to deceive someone, to tell lies.

- **Не вешай мне лапшу на уши!** *(Ne veshay mne lapshu na ushi!)* — Don't try to fool me!
- **Он вечно вешает лапшу на уши начальнику.** *(On vechno veshayet lapshu na ushi nachal'niku.)* — He's always pulling the wool over the boss's eyes.

### Делать из мухи слона (To make an elephant out of a fly)

Means to make a mountain out of a molehill — to exaggerate a problem.

- **Не делай из мухи слона, всё будет хорошо.** *(Ne delay iz mukhi slona, vsyo budet khorosho.)* — Don't make a mountain out of a molehill, everything will be fine.
- **Она всегда делает из мухи слона.** *(Ona vsegda delayet iz mukhi slona.)* — She always blows things out of proportion.

## Idiomatic Expressions with Cases

Many common Russian expressions rely on specific case constructions. Understanding these patterns helps you sound more natural.

### Dative Case Expressions

- **Мне всё равно.** *(Mne vsyo ravno.)* — I don't care. / It's all the same to me.
- **Мне нравится этот город.** *(Mne nravitsya etot gorod.)* — I like this city. (lit. This city is pleasing to me.)
- **Ему повезло.** *(Yemu povezlo.)* — He was lucky.
- **Ей холодно.** *(Yey kholodno.)* — She is cold. (impersonal construction)
- **Нам пора идти.** *(Nam pora idti.)* — It's time for us to go.
- **Тебе не кажется, что...?** *(Tebe ne kazhetsya, chto...?)* — Don't you think that...?

### Genitive Case Expressions

- **У меня нет слов.** *(U menya net slov.)* — I have no words. (I'm speechless.)
- **У него нет времени.** *(U nego net vremeni.)* — He has no time.
- **Без проблем!** *(Bez problem!)* — No problem!
- **С Новым годом!** *(S Novym godom!)* — Happy New Year! (lit. With the New Year!)
- **Ни слова больше!** *(Ni slova bol'she!)* — Not another word!

### Instrumental Case Expressions

- **Между прочим...** *(Mezhdu prochim...)* — By the way...
- **С удовольствием!** *(S udovol'stviyem!)* — With pleasure!
- **Она стала врачом.** *(Ona stala vrachom.)* — She became a doctor.
- **Что с тобой?** *(Chto s toboy?)* — What's wrong with you?
- **С днём рождения!** *(S dnyom rozhdeniya!)* — Happy birthday! (lit. With the day of birth!)

## Colloquial and Slang Expressions

These expressions are used in everyday informal speech. They'll help you understand native speakers in casual settings.

### Common Colloquialisms

- **Ладно.** *(Ladno.)* — Okay / Alright.
- **Ничего себе!** *(Nichego sebe!)* — Wow! / No way!
- **Да ладно!** *(Da ladno!)* — No way! / Come on! (disbelief)
- **Блин!** *(Blin!)* — Darn! (mild expletive, lit. "pancake")
- **Короче...** *(Koroche...)* — In short... / Basically... (filler word)

### Everyday Reactions

- **Здорово!** *(Zdorovo!)* — Cool! / Great!
- **Ужас!** *(Uzhas!)* — Terrible! / How awful!
- **Точно!** *(Tochno!)* — Exactly! / That's right!
- **Серьёзно?** *(Ser'yozno?)* — Seriously?
- **Не может быть!** *(Ne mozhet byt'!)* — That can't be! / Impossible!

### Conversational Fillers

- **Ну...** *(Nu...)* — Well...
- **Значит...** *(Znachit...)* — So... / That means...
- **Как бы...** *(Kak by...)* — Like... / Sort of... (hedging)
- **В общем...** *(V obshchem...)* — In general... / Basically...
- **Типа...** *(Tipa...)* — Like... / Kind of... (very colloquial)

> **Note:** Using too many fillers (especially **типа** and **как бы**) is considered a sign of uneducated speech. Use them sparingly!

## Cultural Context for Expressions

### The Russian "You" — Ты vs. Вы

Understanding when to use **ты** (informal) vs. **вы** (formal) is crucial:

- **Вы** — with strangers, elders, in professional settings, showing respect
- **Ты** — with friends, family, children, peers (after mutual agreement)
- **Можно на "ты"?** *(Mozhno na "ty"?)* — Can we use "ты"? (asking permission to switch to informal)
- **Давай на "ты"!** *(Davay na "ty"!)* — Let's use "ты"!
- **Вы очень добры.** *(Vy ochen' dobry.)* — You are very kind. (formal)

### Toasting Traditions

Russians have rich toasting traditions. Simply saying "cheers" is considered lazy.

- **За здоровье!** *(Za zdorov'ye!)* — To health!
- **За дружбу!** *(Za druzhbu!)* — To friendship!
- **За любовь!** *(Za lyubov'!)* — To love!
- **За встречу!** *(Za vstrechu!)* — To our meeting!
- **Давайте выпьем за...** *(Davayte vyp'yem za...)* — Let's drink to...

### Superstitions in Language

Russian has many expressions rooted in superstition:

- **Тьфу-тьфу-тьфу!** *(T'fu-t'fu-t'fu!)* — Knock on wood! (accompanied by spitting over left shoulder three times)
- **Не сглазь!** *(Ne sglaz'!)* — Don't jinx it!
- **Присядем на дорожку.** *(Prisyadem na dorozhku.)* — Let's sit down before the journey. (tradition before leaving on a trip)
- **Ни пуха ни пера!** — К чёрту! (as covered above — the "reverse wish" tradition)
- **Последний — плохая примета. Крайний!** *(Posledniy — plokhaya primeta. Krayniy!)* — "Last" is bad luck. Say "extreme" instead! (some Russians avoid the word "последний" in certain contexts)

## Useful Expression Patterns

Here are versatile patterns you can use in many situations:

### Мне кажется, что... (It seems to me that...)

- **Мне кажется, что он прав.** *(Mne kazhetsya, chto on prav.)* — It seems to me that he's right.
- **Мне кажется, что завтра будет дождь.** *(Mne kazhetsya, chto zavtra budet dozhd'.)* — I think it will rain tomorrow.

### Дело в том, что... (The thing is that...)

- **Дело в том, что я не могу прийти.** *(Delo v tom, chto ya ne mogu priyti.)* — The thing is, I can't come.
- **Дело в том, что это очень сложно.** *(Delo v tom, chto eto ochen' slozhno.)* — The thing is, it's very complicated.

### Не стоит + infinitive (It's not worth...)

- **Не стоит беспокоиться.** *(Ne stoit bespokoit'sya.)* — It's not worth worrying.
- **Не стоит туда ходить.** *(Ne stoit tuda khodit'.)* — It's not worth going there.

### Стоит + infinitive (It's worth...)

- **Стоит попробовать!** *(Stoit poprobovat'!)* — It's worth trying!
- **Стоит посмотреть этот фильм.** *(Stoit posmotret' etot fil'm.)* — It's worth watching this film.

### Как насчёт...? (How about...?)

- **Как насчёт кофе?** *(Kak naschyot kofe?)* — How about coffee?
- **Как насчёт завтра?** *(Kak naschyot zavtra?)* — How about tomorrow?

### Key Takeaways

1. Always match your register — **ты** vs. **вы** matters enormously in Russian culture.
2. Learn responses together with expressions: **Ни пуха ни пера! — К чёрту!**
3. Idioms make you sound natural — start with the most common ones.
4. Many everyday expressions use **impersonal constructions** with the dative case.
5. Russian colloquialisms add color but use them only in appropriate informal settings!
