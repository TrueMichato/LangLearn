# Conditional Mood (бы)

The Russian conditional mood is formed using the particle **бы** combined with the **past tense** of a verb. It expresses hypothetical situations, wishes, polite requests, and unreal conditions. Unlike English, Russian does not distinguish between present and past unreal conditions — the same construction with **бы** covers both.

---

## 1. Basic Formation

The conditional is formed with the **past tense** of any verb + the particle **бы** (or **б** after vowels in informal speech):

| Subject | Past tense | + бы | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| я | читал(а) | бы | I would read |
| ты | написал(а) | бы | you would write |
| он | пошёл | бы | he would go |
| она | сделала | бы | she would do |
| мы | поехали | бы | we would go (by transport) |
| они | купили | бы | they would buy |

The particle **бы** is **not fixed** in position — it can come right after the verb, after the subject, or elsewhere in the clause. It most commonly appears right after the verb or after the first stressed word.

**Я бы прочитал эту книгу.** *(Ya by prochital etu knigu.)* — I would read this book.

**Он купил бы машину.** *(On kupil by mashynu.)* — He would buy a car.

**Мы бы поехали в Италию.** *(My by poyekhali v Italiyu.)* — We would go to Italy.

**Она сделала бы это иначе.** *(Ona sdelala by eto inache.)* — She would do it differently.

---

## 2. Conditional Sentences with Если бы

The most common use of **бы** is in conditional sentences with **если бы** (if). These express **unreal** or **hypothetical** conditions.

### Structure

**Если бы** + past tense, ... past tense + **бы**

| Condition (если бы...) | Result (...бы) |
|---|---|
| Если бы я знал... | ...я бы сказал. |
| If I knew (had known)... | ...I would say (would have said). |

### Present unreal conditions

**Если бы я знал русский, я бы поехал в Россию.** *(Yesli by ya znal russkiy, ya by poyekhal v Rossiyu.)* — If I knew Russian, I would go to Russia.

**Если бы у меня были деньги, я бы купил новый компьютер.** *(Yesli by u menya byli dengi, ya by kupil novyy kompyuter.)* — If I had money, I would buy a new computer.

**Если бы она жила ближе, мы бы встречались чаще.** *(Yesli by ona zhila blizhe, my by vstrechalis chashche.)* — If she lived closer, we would meet more often.

### Past unreal conditions

**Если бы я встал раньше, я бы не опоздал.** *(Yesli by ya vstal ranshe, ya by ne opozdal.)* — If I had gotten up earlier, I wouldn't have been late.

**Если бы он учился лучше, он бы поступил в университет.** *(Yesli by on uchilsya luchshe, on by postupil v universitet.)* — If he had studied better, he would have entered the university.

**Если бы мы знали об этом, мы бы пришли.** *(Yesli by my znali ob etom, my by prishli.)* — If we had known about it, we would have come.

> **Note:** Russian uses the same construction for both present and past hypotheticals. Context determines the time reference.

## 3. Expressing Wishes with Бы

The particle **бы** is used with various constructions to express wishes and desires.

### Хотел(а) бы — "would like"

**Я хотел бы поговорить с вами.** *(Ya khotel by pogovorit s vami.)* — I would like to talk with you.

**Она хотела бы жить у моря.** *(Ona khotela by zhit u morya.)* — She would like to live by the sea.

**Мы хотели бы заказать столик.** *(My khoteli by zakazat stolik.)* — We would like to reserve a table.

### Мне хотелось бы — "I would like" (softer)

**Мне хотелось бы узнать больше.** *(Mne khotelos by uznat bolshe.)* — I would like to learn more.

**Нам хотелось бы побывать в Санкт-Петербурге.** *(Nam khotelos by pobyvat v Sankt-Peterburge.)* — We would like to visit Saint Petersburg.

### Если бы + exclamation (wishes about the past/present)

**Если бы я только знал!** *(Yesli by ya tolko znal!)* — If only I knew! (If only I had known!)

**Если бы ты был здесь!** *(Yesli by ty byl zdes!)* — If only you were here!

### Вот бы — "if only / I wish"

**Вот бы сейчас на море!** *(Vot by seychas na more!)* — If only I could be at the sea right now!

**Вот бы у меня была такая машина!** *(Vot by u menya byla takaya mashyna!)* — I wish I had a car like that!

## 4. Polite Requests with Бы

Adding **бы** makes requests softer and more polite. This works with several constructions:

### Не могли бы вы... — "Could you..."

**Не могли бы вы открыть окно?** *(Ne mogli by vy otkryt okno?)* — Could you open the window?

**Не могли бы вы говорить медленнее?** *(Ne mogli by vy govorit medlenneye?)* — Could you speak more slowly?

**Не мог бы ты помочь мне?** *(Ne mog by ty pomoch mne?)* — Could you help me?

### Вы бы... — gentle suggestion

**Вы бы отдохнули.** *(Vy by otdokhnuli.)* — You should rest. (gentle)

**Ты бы поел.** *(Ty by poyel.)* — You should eat something. (caring suggestion)

**Вы бы позвонили врачу.** *(Vy by pozvonili vrachu.)* — You should call the doctor. (gentle advice)

### Мне бы... — "I'd like to" (casual)

**Мне бы кофе.** *(Mne by kofe.)* — I'd like a coffee. (casual)

**Мне бы поспать.** *(Mne by pospat.)* — I'd like to get some sleep.

## 5. Бы with Чтобы (Purpose and Desire)

The conjunction **чтобы** (что + бы) is used to express **purpose** ("in order to") and **desire** ("that someone do something").

### Purpose: чтобы + infinitive (same subject)

**Я учу русский, чтобы читать Достоевского.** *(Ya uchu russkiy, chtoby chitat Dostoyevskogo.)* — I study Russian in order to read Dostoevsky.

**Она встала рано, чтобы успеть на поезд.** *(Ona vstala rano, chtoby uspet na poyezd.)* — She got up early in order to catch the train.

### Desire: чтобы + past tense (different subjects)

**Я хочу, чтобы ты пришёл.** *(Ya khochu, chtoby ty prishyol.)* — I want you to come. (lit. I want that you came.)

**Мама хочет, чтобы я учился хорошо.** *(Mama khochet, chtoby ya uchilsya khorosho.)* — Mom wants me to study well.

**Учитель попросил, чтобы мы написали сочинение.** *(Uchitel poprosil, chtoby my napisali sochineniye.)* — The teacher asked that we write an essay.

**Я хотел бы, чтобы вы объяснили это.** *(Ya khotel by, chtoby vy obyasnili eto.)* — I would like you to explain this.

## 6. Other Uses of Бы

### Кто бы ни / что бы ни — "whoever / whatever"

**Кто бы ни пришёл, мы рады.** *(Kto by ni prishyol, my rady.)* — Whoever comes, we are glad.

**Что бы он ни делал, всё получается.** *(Chto by on ni delal, vsyo poluchayetsya.)* — Whatever he does, everything works out.

**Где бы ты ни был, звони мне.** *(Gde by ty ni byl, zvoni mne.)* — Wherever you are, call me.

**Как бы то ни было...** *(Kak by to ni bylo...)* — Be that as it may... / In any case...

### Как бы — "as if" / hedging

**Он как бы не заметил.** *(On kak by ne zametil.)* — He sort of didn't notice. (hedging)

**Она как бы улыбнулась.** *(Ona kak by ulybnulas.)* — She sort of smiled.

> In modern colloquial Russian, **как бы** is overused as a filler word, similar to English "like."

## 7. Common Mistakes

❌ **Если бы я знаю...** — Incorrect: don't use present tense with если бы.
✅ **Если бы я знал...** — If I knew...

❌ **Я бы хочу кофе.** — Incorrect: бы requires past tense.
✅ **Я бы хотел кофе.** — I would like a coffee.

❌ **Я хочу чтобы ты приходишь.** — Incorrect: чтобы + different subject needs past tense.
✅ **Я хочу, чтобы ты пришёл.** — I want you to come.

❌ **Если бы я буду знать...** — Incorrect: no future tense with если бы.
✅ **Если бы я знал...** — If I knew (would know)...

---

## Key Takeaways

1. The conditional is formed with **past tense + бы**.
2. **Если бы... бы** expresses both present and past unreal conditions.
3. **Хотел(а) бы** means "would like" — a polite way to express desires.
4. **Чтобы + infinitive** = purpose (same subject); **чтобы + past tense** = desire (different subjects).
5. **Бы** can appear in various positions in the sentence — it is not fixed.
6. **Кто бы ни / что бы ни** means "whoever / whatever."
