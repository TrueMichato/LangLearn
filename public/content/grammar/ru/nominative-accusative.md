# Nominative & Accusative Cases

The **nominative** (именительный) and **accusative** (винительный) cases are the two most frequently used cases in Russian. The nominative marks the subject — who does the action. The accusative marks the direct object — who or what receives the action. Mastering these two cases is essential before moving to other cases.

---

## 1. Review: The Nominative Case (Именительный падеж)

The nominative case is the **dictionary form** of a noun. It answers the questions **Кто?** (Who?) and **Что?** (What?). It is used for the **subject** of a sentence.

**Студент читает книгу.** *(Student chitayet knigu.)* — The student reads a book.

**Кошка спит на диване.** *(Koshka spit na divane.)* — The cat sleeps on the sofa.

**Молоко стоит на столе.** *(Moloko stoit na stole.)* — The milk is on the table.

**Мама готовит обед.** *(Mama gotovit obed.)* — Mom is cooking lunch.

**Дети играют во дворе.** *(Deti igrayut vo dvore.)* — The children play in the yard.

**Учитель объясняет урок.** *(Uchitel' ob"yasnyayet urok.)* — The teacher explains the lesson.

> The nominative is also used after **это** (this is): **Это мой брат.** *(Eto moy brat.)* — This is my brother.

---

## 2. Introduction to the Accusative Case (Винительный падеж)

The accusative case marks the **direct object** — the person or thing that receives the action of the verb. It answers **Кого?** (Whom?) and **Что?** (What?).

**Я вижу дом.** *(Ya vizhu dom.)* — I see a house.

**Она читает газету.** *(Ona chitayet gazetu.)* — She reads a newspaper.

**Мы любим музыку.** *(My lyubim muzyku.)* — We love music.

**Он пишет письмо.** *(On pishet pis'mo.)* — He writes a letter.

**Они знают этого человека.** *(Oni znayut etogo cheloveka.)* — They know this person.

**Ты слышишь песню?** *(Ty slyshish' pesnyu?)* — Do you hear the song?

> Compare: **Газета лежит на столе** (nominative — the newspaper is the subject) vs. **Она читает газету** (accusative — the newspaper is the object).

---

## 3. Accusative Endings

The key to the accusative case is that its form depends on both **gender** and whether the noun is **animate** (living) or **inanimate** (non-living).

### Endings Table: Nominative → Accusative (Singular)

| Gender | Animate/Inanimate | Nominative | Accusative | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Masc. | Inanimate | стол (table) | стол | Я вижу **стол**. |
| Masc. | Animate | студент (student) | студент**а** | Я вижу **студента**. |
| Masc. -ь | Inanimate | словарь (dictionary) | словарь | Я вижу **словарь**. |
| Masc. -ь | Animate | учитель (teacher) | учител**я** | Я вижу **учителя**. |
| Fem. -а | — | книг**а** (book) | книг**у** | Я читаю **книгу**. |
| Fem. -я | — | песн**я** (song) | песн**ю** | Я слышу **песню**. |
| Fem. -ь | — | дверь (door) | дверь | Я вижу **дверь**. |
| Neuter | — | окн**о** (window) | окн**о** | Я вижу **окно**. |
| Neuter | — | мор**е** (sea) | мор**е** | Я вижу **море**. |

### Summary Rules

- **Masculine inanimate** → accusative = nominative (no change)
- **Masculine animate** → accusative = genitive (-а/-я)
- **Feminine -а** → **-у**; feminine **-я** → **-ю**; feminine **-ь** → no change
- **Neuter** → accusative = nominative (no change)

## 4. The Animate vs. Inanimate Distinction

This is one of the most important concepts in Russian grammar. For **masculine singular** nouns (and all **plural** nouns), the accusative form depends on whether the noun is animate or inanimate.

### Animate = Living Beings
People, animals, and personified characters are animate.

**Я вижу студента.** *(Ya vizhu studenta.)* — I see the student.

**Она знает врача.** *(Ona znayet vracha.)* — She knows the doctor.

**Мы любим кота.** *(My lyubim kota.)* — We love the cat.

**Ребёнок видит собаку.** *(Rebyonok vidit sobaku.)* — The child sees the dog.

**Он встретил друга.** *(On vstretil druga.)* — He met a friend.

### Inanimate = Non-Living Things
Objects, places, abstract concepts are inanimate.

**Я вижу стол.** *(Ya vizhu stol.)* — I see the table.

**Она читает журнал.** *(Ona chitayet zhurnal.)* — She reads a magazine.

**Мы строим дом.** *(My stroim dom.)* — We are building a house.

**Он открыл словарь.** *(On otkryl slovar'.)* — He opened the dictionary.

**Ты видишь парк?** *(Ty vidish' park?)* — Do you see the park?

> **Remember:** For masculine nouns — animate accusative looks like genitive; inanimate accusative looks like nominative.

## 5. Feminine Nouns in the Accusative

Feminine nouns change predictably in the accusative. The animate/inanimate distinction does **not** matter for feminine singular.

### Feminine -а → -у

**Студент читает книгу.** *(Student chitayet knigu.)* — The student reads a book.

**Я люблю маму.** *(Ya lyublyu mamu.)* — I love mom.

**Он видит школу.** *(On vidit shkolu.)* — He sees the school.

**Мы знаем Москву.** *(My znayem Moskvu.)* — We know Moscow.

**Она купила сумку.** *(Ona kupila sumku.)* — She bought a bag.

### Feminine -я → -ю

**Он слушает песню.** *(On slushayet pesnyu.)* — He listens to a song.

**Я вижу деревню.** *(Ya vizhu derevnyu.)* — I see the village.

**Мы изучаем неделю.** *(My izuchayem nedelyu.)* — We are studying for a week.

**Она нашла станцию.** *(Ona nashla stantsiyu.)* — She found the station.

**Ты знаешь эту историю?** *(Ty znayesh' etu istoriyu?)* — Do you know this story?

### Feminine -ь → No Change

**Я открыл дверь.** *(Ya otkryl dver'.)* — I opened the door.

**Он потерял тетрадь.** *(On poteryal tetrad'.)* — He lost the notebook.

**Мы видим площадь.** *(My vidim ploshchad'.)* — We see the square.

**Она любит осень.** *(Ona lyubit osen'.)* — She loves autumn.

**Ты чувствуешь боль?** *(Ty chuvstvuyesh' bol'?)* — Do you feel pain?

---

## 6. Neuter Nouns in the Accusative

Neuter nouns do **not change** in the accusative (for singular inanimate, which covers nearly all neuter nouns).

**Я вижу окно.** *(Ya vizhu okno.)* — I see the window.

**Он пишет письмо.** *(On pishet pis'mo.)* — He writes a letter.

**Мы пьём молоко.** *(My p'yom moloko.)* — We drink milk.

**Она открыла окно.** *(Ona otkryla okno.)* — She opened the window.

**Ребёнок ест яблоко.** *(Rebyonok yest yabloko.)* — The child eats an apple.

**Я люблю море.** *(Ya lyublyu more.)* — I love the sea.

> **Exception:** The rare neuter animate nouns (like **животное** — animal, when used as a noun) follow animate rules in the accusative plural.

## 7. Verbs That Take the Accusative

Many common Russian verbs require their object in the accusative case:

| Verb | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| видеть | to see | Я вижу **дом**. |
| читать | to read | Она читает **книгу**. |
| писать | to write | Он пишет **письмо**. |
| любить | to love | Мы любим **музыку**. |
| знать | to know | Ты знаешь **ответ**? |
| слушать | to listen to | Я слушаю **радио**. |
| смотреть | to watch | Мы смотрим **фильм**. |
| покупать | to buy | Она покупает **хлеб**. |
| есть | to eat | Дети едят **кашу**. |
| пить | to drink | Он пьёт **воду**. |

**Я слушаю музыку каждый день.** *(Ya slushayu muzyku kazhdyy den'.)* — I listen to music every day.

**Мы смотрим новый фильм.** *(My smotrim novyy fil'm.)* — We are watching a new movie.

**Он покупает свежий хлеб.** *(On pokupayet svezhiy khleb.)* — He buys fresh bread.

**Дети едят горячую кашу.** *(Deti yedyat goryachuyu kashu.)* — The children eat hot porridge.

**Она пьёт холодную воду.** *(Ona p'yot kholodnuyu vodu.)* — She drinks cold water.

## 8. Accusative of Direction and Motion

The accusative is also used to express **direction** (movement toward a place) with the prepositions **в** (into) and **на** (onto).

### В + Accusative (into, to)

**Я иду в школу.** *(Ya idu v shkolu.)* — I am going to school.

**Он едет в Москву.** *(On yedyet v Moskvu.)* — He is going to Moscow.

**Мы идём в магазин.** *(My idyom v magazin.)* — We are going to the store.

**Она пошла в библиотеку.** *(Ona poshla v biblioteku.)* — She went to the library.

**Дети бегут в парк.** *(Deti begut v park.)* — The children are running to the park.

### На + Accusative (onto, to)

**Я иду на работу.** *(Ya idu na rabotu.)* — I am going to work.

**Он идёт на урок.** *(On idyot na urok.)* — He is going to the lesson.

**Мы едем на море.** *(My yedyem na more.)* — We are going to the sea.

**Она пошла на почту.** *(Ona poshla na pochtu.)* — She went to the post office.

**Они поехали на стадион.** *(Oni poyekhali na stadion.)* — They went to the stadium.

> **Contrast:** В/на + **accusative** = direction (going TO). В/на + **prepositional** = location (being AT).
> Compare: **Я иду в школу** (I'm going to school) vs. **Я в школе** (I'm at school).

**Мальчик бежит на площадку.** *(Mal'chik bezhit na ploshchadku.)* — The boy is running to the playground.

**Мы летим в Петербург.** *(My letim v Peterburg.)* — We are flying to Petersburg.

**Положи книгу на полку.** *(Polozhi knigu na polku.)* — Put the book on the shelf.

**Студенты идут на лекцию.** *(Studenty idut na lektsiyu.)* — The students are going to the lecture.

**Я положил телефон в карман.** *(Ya polozhil telefon v karman.)* — I put the phone in my pocket.

## 9. Adjective Agreement in the Accusative

Adjectives also change form in the accusative. The rules mirror the noun rules:

### Adjective Accusative Endings (Singular)

| Gender | Inanimate | Animate |
|---|---|---|
| Masculine | -ый/-ий/-ой (= nom.) | -ого/-его (= gen.) |
| Feminine | -ую/-юю | -ую/-юю |
| Neuter | -ое/-ее (= nom.) | -ое/-ее (= nom.) |

**Я читаю интересную книгу.** *(Ya chitayu interesnuyu knigu.)* — I am reading an interesting book.

**Она видит красивый дом.** *(Ona vidit krasivyy dom.)* — She sees a beautiful house.

**Мы знаем нового студента.** *(My znayem novogo studenta.)* — We know the new student.

**Он купил большое окно.** *(On kupil bol'shoye okno.)* — He bought a large window.

**Я встретил старого друга.** *(Ya vstretil starogo druga.)* — I met an old friend.

**Она пьёт горячую воду.** *(Ona p'yot goryachuyu vodu.)* — She drinks hot water.

## 10. Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Forgetting animate accusative

- ❌ Я вижу студент. *(wrong — animate masculine must change)*
- ✅ **Я вижу студента.** *(Ya vizhu studenta.)* — I see the student.

### Mistake 2: Changing inanimate masculine nouns

- ❌ Я вижу стола. *(wrong — inanimate masculine stays the same)*
- ✅ **Я вижу стол.** *(Ya vizhu stol.)* — I see the table.

### Mistake 3: Confusing direction and location

- **Я иду в школу.** *(direction — accusative)* — I'm going to school.
- **Я в школе.** *(location — prepositional)* — I'm at school.

### Mistake 4: Forgetting to change feminine -а/-я endings

- ❌ Он читает книга. *(wrong — must change to accusative)*
- ✅ **Он читает книгу.** *(On chitayet knigu.)* — He reads a book.

**Она слушает новую песню.** *(Ona slushayet novuyu pesnyu.)* — She listens to a new song.

**Мы изучаем русскую грамматику.** *(My izuchayem russkuyu grammatiku.)* — We study Russian grammar.

**Я нашёл старую тетрадь.** *(Ya nashyol staruyu tetrad'.)* — I found an old notebook.

**Он пригласил хорошего друга.** *(On priglasil khoroshego druga.)* — He invited a good friend.

**Ребёнок ест вкусное яблоко.** *(Rebyonok yest vkusnoye yabloko.)* — The child eats a tasty apple.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Nominative** = subject (who/what does the action).
2. **Accusative** = direct object (who/what receives the action).
3. Masculine inanimate nouns do **not change** in the accusative.
4. Masculine animate nouns take **genitive-like** endings in the accusative (-а/-я).
5. Feminine **-а → -у**, **-я → -ю**, **-ь → no change**.
6. Neuter nouns do **not change** in the accusative.
7. Use **в/на + accusative** for direction of motion.
8. Adjectives follow the same animate/inanimate logic as nouns.
