# Passive Constructions & Impersonal Sentences

Russian has several ways to express passive meaning and impersonal (subjectless) sentences. Unlike English, which relies heavily on the passive voice ("The book was written by..."), Russian often prefers **active constructions**, **reflexive verbs**, or **impersonal sentences**. Understanding when and how to use each pattern is key to natural Russian.

---

## 1. Passive with Short-Form Participles (Most Common Written Passive)

The most standard passive construction in Russian uses a **short-form passive participle** (past tense, perfective):

### Formation

Take the perfective past passive participle and use its **short form**:

| Verb | Full participle | Short form (m/f/n/pl) |
|---|---|---|
| написать (to write) | написанный | написан / написана / написано / написаны |
| построить (to build) | построенный | построен / построена / построено / построены |
| открыть (to open) | открытый | открыт / открыта / открыто / открыты |
| прочитать (to read) | прочитанный | прочитан / прочитана / прочитано / прочитаны |
| решить (to solve) | решённый | решён / решена / решено / решены |

### Examples

**Книга написана Толстым.** *(Kníga napísana Tolstým.)* — The book was written by Tolstoy.

**Дом построен в прошлом году.** *(Dom postróyen v próshlom godú.)* — The house was built last year.

**Проблема решена.** *(Probléma reshená.)* — The problem is solved.

**Двери открыты.** *(Dvéri otkrýty.)* — The doors are open(ed).

**Письмо отправлено.** *(Pismó otprávleno.)* — The letter has been sent.

**Эта картина нарисована моей дочерью.** *(Éta kartína narisóvana moyéy dócheryú.)* — This painting was drawn by my daughter.

> The **agent** (who performed the action) is expressed with the **instrumental case**: написана Толстым, нарисована дочерью.

## 2. Passive with Reflexive Verbs (-ся)

The reflexive suffix **-ся/-сь** can give an imperfective verb a passive meaning. This is common for **processes** and **habitual/general** statements:

**Этот журнал издаётся в Москве.** *(Étot zhurnál izdayótsya v Moskvé.)* — This magazine is published in Moscow.

**Хлеб продаётся в этом магазине.** *(Khleb prodayótsya v étom magazíne.)* — Bread is sold in this store.

**Эта книга читается легко.** *(Éta kníga chitáyetsya legkó.)* — This book reads easily (is easy to read).

**Здесь строится новая школа.** *(Zdes stróitsya nóvaya shkóla.)* — A new school is being built here.

**Собрание проводится каждый понедельник.** *(Sobrániye provóditsya kázhdyy ponedélnik.)* — The meeting is held every Monday.

> The **-ся passive** is used mostly with **imperfective** verbs for ongoing or repeated actions. The agent is usually **not mentioned**.

## 3. Impersonal Sentences — Overview

Impersonal sentences have **no grammatical subject** in the nominative case. They are extremely common in Russian and express states, natural phenomena, necessity, possibility, and more.

### Types of impersonal sentences

| Type | Example | Translation |
|---|---|---|
| Natural phenomena | Светает. *(Svetáyet.)* | It's getting light. |
| Physical states | Мне холодно. *(Mne khólodno.)* | I'm cold. |
| Emotional states | Мне грустно. *(Mne grústno.)* | I'm sad. |
| Necessity | Нужно работать. *(Núzhno rabótat.)* | It's necessary to work. |
| Possibility | Можно войти? *(Mózhno voytí?)* | May I come in? |
| Prohibition | Нельзя курить. *(Nelzyá kurít.)* | Smoking is not allowed. |

---

## 4. Impersonal Sentences with Dative + Adverb/Short Adjective

The **dative case** identifies the person experiencing the state:

### Physical sensations

**Мне холодно.** *(Mne khólodno.)* — I'm cold.

**Ему жарко.** *(Yemú zhárko.)* — He's hot.

**Ей плохо.** *(Yey plókho.)* — She feels bad/sick.

**Нам хорошо здесь.** *(Nam khoroshó zdes.)* — We feel good here.

**Детям весело.** *(Détyam véselo.)* — The children are having fun.

### Emotional and mental states

**Мне скучно.** *(Mne skúchno.)* — I'm bored.

**Ему стыдно.** *(Yemú stýdno.)* — He's ashamed.

**Ей интересно.** *(Yey interésno.)* — She's interested.

**Нам страшно.** *(Nam stráshno.)* — We're scared.

**Мне всё равно.** *(Mne vsyo ravnó.)* — I don't care. (It's all the same to me.)

## 5. Impersonal Constructions with Modal Words

### Можно / нельзя — permission and prohibition

**Здесь можно курить?** *(Zdes mózhno kurít?)* — Is it allowed to smoke here?

**Нельзя так говорить.** *(Nelzyá tak govorít.)* — You can't say that. / One mustn't say that.

**Здесь нельзя парковаться.** *(Zdes nelzyá parkovát'sya.)* — You can't park here.

**Можно мне выйти?** *(Mózhno mne výyti?)* — May I go out?

### Нужно / надо / необходимо — necessity

**Мне нужно поговорить с тобой.** *(Mne núzhno pogovorít s tobóy.)* — I need to talk to you.

**Нам надо торопиться.** *(Nam nádo toropítsya.)* — We need to hurry.

**Необходимо закончить проект к пятнице.** *(Neobkhodímo zakónchit proyékt k pyátnitse.)* — It's necessary to finish the project by Friday.

### Следует — "one should"

**Следует быть осторожным.** *(Sléduyet byt ostorózhnym.)* — One should be careful.

**Вам не следует так поступать.** *(Vam ne sléduyet tak postupát.)* — You shouldn't act that way.

---

## 6. Impersonal Sentences with Нет / Не было / Не будет

Existential negation in Russian is **always impersonal** — the "missing" thing goes into the genitive:

**В комнате нет никого.** *(V kómnate net nikogó.)* — There's nobody in the room.

**У нас не было выбора.** *(U nas ne bylo výbora.)* — We had no choice.

**Завтра не будет урока.** *(Závtra ne búdet uróka.)* — There will be no lesson tomorrow.

**Денег не хватает.** *(Déneg ne khvatáyet.)* — There's not enough money.

---

## 7. Impersonal Constructions with Infinitive

An infinitive alone (or with a dative subject) can form impersonal sentences expressing various meanings:

### Impossibility / inevitability

**Этого не изменить.** *(Étogo ne izmenít.)* — This cannot be changed.

**Ему не пройти мимо.** *(Yemú ne proytí mímo.)* — He cannot pass by (it's impossible for him).

### Rhetorical questions

**Что делать?** *(Chto délat?)* — What is to be done?

**Куда идти?** *(Kudá idtí?)* — Where should one go?

**Как быть?** *(Kak byt?)* — What's to be done? / How to be?

### With бы — wish or possibility

**Мне бы отдохнуть.** *(Mne by otdokhnút.)* — I wish I could rest.

**Нам бы успеть!** *(Nam by uspét!)* — If only we could make it in time!

## 8. The "Third-Person Plural" Passive (Indefinite Subject)

Russian frequently uses the **third-person plural without они** to express an action where the agent is unknown or unimportant — similar to English passive:

**Здесь говорят по-русски.** *(Zdes govoryát po-rússki.)* — Russian is spoken here. (lit. "Here they speak Russian.")

**Мне сказали, что он уехал.** *(Mne skazáli, chto on uyékhal.)* — I was told that he left. (lit. "They told me...")

**Его пригласили на вечеринку.** *(Yevó priglasíli na vecherínku.)* — He was invited to the party.

**Этот дом построили в 1990 году.** *(Étot dom postróili v 1990 godú.)* — This house was built in 1990.

**В газете написали, что...** *(V gazéte napisáli, chto...)* — It was written in the newspaper that...

> This is one of the **most natural** ways to express passive meaning in spoken Russian.

---

## 9. Choosing Between Passive Constructions

| Construction | Best for | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Short participle | Formal writing, stating results | Письмо написано. |
| -ся reflexive | Ongoing processes, general statements | Журнал издаётся в Москве. |
| 3rd person plural | Spoken Russian, unknown agent | Мне сказали... |
| Impersonal + dative | States, feelings, necessity | Мне холодно. Нужно работать. |

---

## 10. Common Mistakes

❌ **Книга была написанная Толстым.** — Don't use the full participle as predicate.
✅ **Книга была написана Толстым.** — The book was written by Tolstoy.

❌ **Мне есть холодно.** — Don't add есть in impersonal state sentences.
✅ **Мне холодно.** — I'm cold.

❌ **Я был сказан, что...** — This calque from English doesn't work in Russian.
✅ **Мне сказали, что...** — I was told that...

❌ **Дом строится рабочими.** — Uncommon: -ся passives rarely mention the agent.
✅ **Дом строится.** — The house is being built. / **Дом строят рабочие.** — Workers are building the house. (active)

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Short-form passive participles** are the standard written passive: «Книга написана».
2. **-Ся verbs** create passive meaning for ongoing/habitual actions.
3. **Impersonal sentences** with dative + adverb express states and feelings: «Мне холодно».
4. **Можно/нельзя/нужно** + infinitive are key impersonal modal constructions.
5. The **3rd person plural** without они is the most natural spoken "passive."
6. The agent in passive constructions takes the **instrumental case**.
