# Verbal Adverbs / Gerunds (Деепричастия)

Russian verbal adverbs (деепричастия) describe an **additional action** performed by the same subject as the main verb. They function like adverbs — they are invariable (do not change form) and modify the verb. English equivalents include "while doing," "having done," or "by doing."

---

## 1. What Are Verbal Adverbs?

A verbal adverb (деепричастие) combines features of a **verb** and an **adverb**. It tells us *how*, *when*, or *why* the main action happens by describing a secondary action performed by the **same subject**.

**Читая книгу, она пила чай.** *(Chitaya knigu, ona pila chay.)* — While reading a book, she drank tea.

Here, **читая** (reading) is the verbal adverb. The main action is **пила** (drank). The same person (она) does both.

There are two types:
1. **Imperfective verbal adverbs** — simultaneous action (while doing)
2. **Perfective verbal adverbs** — prior/completed action (having done)

---

## 2. Imperfective Verbal Adverbs (Несовершенный вид)

Imperfective verbal adverbs express an action happening **at the same time** as the main verb.

### Formation

From the **они** (3rd person plural) present tense, replace **-т** with **-я** (or **-а** after hissing consonants ж, ш, ч, щ):

| Verb | Они form | Verbal adverb | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| читать | читаю**т** | чита**я** | while reading |
| говорить | говоря**т** | говор**я** | while speaking |
| думать | думаю**т** | дума**я** | while thinking |
| улыбаться | улыбаю**т**ся | улыба**я**сь | while smiling |
| сидеть | сидя**т** | сид**я** | while sitting |
| держать | держа**т** | держ**а** | while holding |
| молчать | молча**т** | молч**а** | while being silent |
| слышать | слыша**т** | слыш**а** | while hearing |

### Examples

**Читая газету, он пил кофе.** *(Chitaya gazetu, on pil kofe.)* — While reading the newspaper, he drank coffee.

**Она шла по улице, думая о работе.** *(Ona shla po ulitse, dumaya o rabote.)* — She walked along the street, thinking about work.

**Улыбаясь, девушка ответила на вопрос.** *(Ulybays, devushka otvetila na vopros.)* — Smiling, the girl answered the question.

**Сидя у окна, я наблюдал за птицами.** *(Sidya u okna, ya nablyudal za ptitsami.)* — Sitting by the window, I watched the birds.

**Слушая музыку, она рисовала.** *(Slushaya muzyku, ona risovala.)* — While listening to music, she was drawing.

**Молча, он вышел из комнаты.** *(Molcha, on vyshel iz komnaty.)* — Silently (while being silent), he left the room.

## 3. Perfective Verbal Adverbs (Совершенный вид)

Perfective verbal adverbs describe an action that was **completed before** the main action.

### Formation

From the **past tense masculine** form, replace **-л** with **-в** (or **-вши** for reflexive verbs → **-вшись**):

| Verb | Past masc. | Verbal adverb | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| прочитать | прочита**л** | прочита**в** | having read |
| написать | написа**л** | написа**в** | having written |
| сделать | сдела**л** | сдела**в** | having done |
| вернуться | вернул**ся** | вернув**шись** | having returned |
| проснуться | проснул**ся** | проснув**шись** | having woken up |
| закрыть | закры**л** | закры**в** | having closed |
| узнать | узна**л** | узна**в** | having found out |
| увидеть | увиде**л** | увиде**в** | having seen |

Some verbs with stems ending in a consonant (no -л) use **-ши**: принёс → принёсши (archaic), but these are rare in modern Russian. Many such verbs use alternative constructions.

### Examples

**Прочитав письмо, она заплакала.** *(Prochitav pismo, ona zaplakala.)* — Having read the letter, she started to cry.

**Написав сочинение, он сдал тетрадь.** *(Napisav sochineniye, on sdal tetrad.)* — Having written the essay, he handed in the notebook.

**Вернувшись домой, мы поужинали.** *(Vernuvshis domoy, my pouzhynali.)* — Having returned home, we had dinner.

**Проснувшись рано, я пошёл на пробежку.** *(Prosnuvshis rano, ya poshyol na probezhku.)* — Having woken up early, I went for a run.

**Узнав правду, он был очень удивлён.** *(Uznav pravdu, on byl ochen udivlyyon.)* — Having learned the truth, he was very surprised.

**Закрыв дверь, она ушла.** *(Zakryv dver, ona ushla.)* — Having closed the door, she left.

## 4. Important Rules and Restrictions

### Rule 1: Same subject

The verbal adverb and the main verb **must** have the same subject. This is the most critical rule.

✅ **Читая книгу, я заснул.** — While reading a book, I fell asleep. (I was reading, I fell asleep.)

❌ **Читая книгу, мне стало скучно.** — Incorrect! "Читая" implies a nominative subject, but "мне" is dative.

✅ **Когда я читал книгу, мне стало скучно.** — Correct with a subordinate clause instead.

### Rule 2: Not all verbs form verbal adverbs

Some verbs **cannot** form imperfective verbal adverbs:
- Monosyllabic verbs: **пить**, **бить**, **лить**, **шить** (no standard forms)
- Verbs with stems ending in consonant clusters: **мёрзнуть**, **гаснуть**
- Some irregular verbs: **есть** (to eat), **хотеть** (to want)

### Rule 3: Punctuation

Verbal adverbs and verbal adverb phrases are **always set off by commas**:

**Увидев друга, я остановился.** — Having seen a friend, I stopped.

**Она работала, слушая радио.** — She worked, listening to the radio.

## 5. Verbal Adverbs That Became True Adverbs

Some verbal adverbs have become **fixed adverbs** through frequent use. They are no longer felt as verbal forms and are **not** set off by commas:

| Word | Origin | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| молча | молчать (to be silent) | silently |
| сидя | сидеть (to sit) | while sitting / seated |
| стоя | стоять (to stand) | while standing |
| лёжа | лежать (to lie) | while lying down |
| не спеша | спешить (to hurry) | unhurriedly |
| нехотя | хотеть (to want) | reluctantly |

**Он молча вышел.** *(On molcha vyshel.)* — He left silently.

**Мы ели стоя.** *(My eli stoya.)* — We ate standing up.

**Она не спеша пила чай.** *(Ona ne spesha pila chay.)* — She drank tea unhurriedly.

**Он нехотя согласился.** *(On nekhotya soglassilsya.)* — He reluctantly agreed.

## 6. Verbal Adverbs vs Participles

Both participles and verbal adverbs come from verbs, but they function differently:

| Feature | Participle (причастие) | Verbal adverb (деепричастие) |
|---|---|---|
| Functions as | Adjective | Adverb |
| Agrees with | Noun (gender, number, case) | Nothing (invariable) |
| Modifies | A noun | The verb / whole clause |
| Can be replaced by | который clause | когда / потому что clause |

**Студент, читающий книгу, сидит у окна.** (Participle modifies "студент")
→ Студент, который читает книгу, сидит у окна.

**Читая книгу, студент сидел у окна.** (Verbal adverb modifies "сидел")
→ Когда студент читал книгу, он сидел у окна.

## 7. Common Patterns in Usage

### Cause

**Устав после работы, он лёг спать рано.** *(Ustav posle raboty, on lyog spat rano.)* — Being tired after work, he went to bed early.

### Manner

**Она говорила, улыбаясь.** *(Ona govorila, ulybays.)* — She spoke, smiling.

### Sequence

**Сделав уроки, дети пошли гулять.** *(Sdelav uroki, deti poshli gulyat.)* — Having done their homework, the children went for a walk.

### Condition

**Зная русский язык, вы сможете читать Толстого.** *(Znaya russkiy yazyk, vy smozhete chitat Tolstogo.)* — Knowing Russian, you will be able to read Tolstoy.

### Concession (with negation)

**Не зная дороги, мы всё-таки добрались.** *(Ne znaya dorogi, my vsyo-taki dobralis.)* — Not knowing the way, we still made it.

---

## Key Takeaways

1. Verbal adverbs are **invariable** forms that modify the main verb.
2. **Imperfective** verbal adverbs (-я/-а) express **simultaneous** actions.
3. **Perfective** verbal adverbs (-в/-вши/-вшись) express **prior completed** actions.
4. The subject of the verbal adverb **must be the same** as the subject of the main verb.
5. Verbal adverbs are always **set off by commas** (except those that have become true adverbs).
6. They are more common in **written** Russian; speech prefers когда/потому что clauses.
