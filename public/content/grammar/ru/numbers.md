# Numbers & Counting

Numbers are essential for everyday communication — shopping, telling time, giving addresses, and discussing quantities. Russian numbers interact closely with the **case system**, making them trickier than in English. This lesson covers cardinal and ordinal numbers, their case agreements, and practical usage.

---

## Cardinal Numbers 1–10

Let's start with the building blocks. Memorize these thoroughly — every other number builds on them.

| Number | Cyrillic | Transliteration |
|--------|----------|-----------------|
| 1 | один / одна / одно | odin / odna / odno |
| 2 | два / две | dva / dve |
| 3 | три | tri |
| 4 | четыре | chetyre |
| 5 | пять | pyat' |
| 6 | шесть | shest' |
| 7 | семь | sem' |
| 8 | восемь | vosem' |
| 9 | девять | devyat' |
| 10 | десять | desyat' |

### Examples

- **Один студент сидит в классе.** *(Odin student sidit v klasse.)* — One student is sitting in the classroom.
- **Одна девушка поёт песню.** *(Odna devushka poyot pesnyu.)* — One girl is singing a song.
- **Два брата живут в Москве.** *(Dva brata zhivut v Moskve.)* — Two brothers live in Moscow.
- **Две сестры работают в школе.** *(Dve sestry rabotayut v shkole.)* — Two sisters work at a school.
- **У меня пять книг.** *(U menya pyat' knig.)* — I have five books.
- **Она купила три яблока.** *(Ona kupila tri yabloka.)* — She bought three apples.

> **Note:** The number **1** agrees in gender with the noun: **один** (masculine), **одна** (feminine), **одно** (neuter). The number **2** has two forms: **два** (masculine/neuter) and **две** (feminine).

## Cardinal Numbers 11–20

These are formed by combining the root of the single digit with **-надцать** (-nadtsat'), which historically comes from "на десять" (on ten).

| Number | Cyrillic | Transliteration |
|--------|----------|-----------------|
| 11 | одиннадцать | odinnadtsat' |
| 12 | двенадцать | dvenadtsat' |
| 13 | тринадцать | trinadtsat' |
| 14 | четырнадцать | chetyrnadtsat' |
| 15 | пятнадцать | pyatnadtsat' |
| 16 | шестнадцать | shestnadtsat' |
| 17 | семнадцать | semnadtsat' |
| 18 | восемнадцать | vosemnadtsat' |
| 19 | девятнадцать | devyatnadtsat' |
| 20 | двадцать | dvadtsat' |

### Examples

- **Мне двенадцать лет.** *(Mne dvenadtsat' let.)* — I am twelve years old.
- **В классе пятнадцать учеников.** *(V klasse pyatnadtsat' uchenikov.)* — There are fifteen students in the class.
- **Она прочитала тринадцать страниц.** *(Ona prochitala trinadtsat' stranits.)* — She read thirteen pages.
- **Мы ждали восемнадцать минут.** *(My zhdali vosemnadtsat' minut.)* — We waited eighteen minutes.
- **Ему девятнадцать лет.** *(Yemu devyatnadtsat' let.)* — He is nineteen years old.

## Tens, Hundreds, and Thousands

### Tens (20–90)

| Number | Cyrillic | Transliteration |
|--------|----------|-----------------|
| 20 | двадцать | dvadtsat' |
| 30 | тридцать | tridtsat' |
| 40 | сорок | sorok |
| 50 | пятьдесят | pyat'desyat |
| 60 | шестьдесят | shest'desyat |
| 70 | семьдесят | sem'desyat |
| 80 | восемьдесят | vosem'desyat |
| 90 | девяносто | devyanosto |

> **Watch out:** 40 (**сорок**) and 90 (**девяносто**) are irregular — they don't follow the pattern of the others.

### Hundreds (100–900)

| Number | Cyrillic | Transliteration |
|--------|----------|-----------------|
| 100 | сто | sto |
| 200 | двести | dvesti |
| 300 | триста | trista |
| 400 | четыреста | chetyresta |
| 500 | пятьсот | pyat'sot |
| 600 | шестьсот | shest'sot |
| 700 | семьсот | sem'sot |
| 800 | восемьсот | vosem'sot |
| 900 | девятьсот | devyat'sot |

### Thousands

- 1000 — **тысяча** *(tysyacha)*
- 2000 — **две тысячи** *(dve tysyachi)*
- 5000 — **пять тысяч** *(pyat' tysyach)*

### Examples

- **В городе сорок школ.** *(V gorode sorok shkol.)* — There are forty schools in the city.
- **Эта машина стоит триста тысяч рублей.** *(Eta mashina stoit trista tysyach rubley.)* — This car costs three hundred thousand rubles.
- **Он пробежал девяносто метров.** *(On probezhal devyanosto metrov.)* — He ran ninety meters.
- **В библиотеке пятьсот книг.** *(V biblioteke pyat'sot knig.)* — The library has five hundred books.
- **Население города — двести тысяч человек.** *(Naseleniye goroda — dvesti tysyach chelovek.)* — The city's population is two hundred thousand people.

## Case Agreement After Numbers

This is one of the trickiest aspects of Russian numbers. The noun form changes depending on the last digit of the number.

### Rule Summary

| Last digit | Noun case | Example with "книга" |
|-----------|-----------|---------------------|
| 1 | Nominative singular | одна **книга** |
| 2, 3, 4 | Genitive singular | две **книги** |
| 5–20 | Genitive plural | пять **книг** |
| 21 | Nominative singular | двадцать одна **книга** |
| 22–24 | Genitive singular | двадцать две **книги** |
| 25–30 | Genitive plural | двадцать пять **книг** |

> **Important exception:** Numbers 11–14 always take **genitive plural**, even though they end in 1–4. So: **одиннадцать книг** (not книга), **двенадцать книг**, etc.

### Examples

- **Двадцать один студент пришёл на лекцию.** *(Dvadtsat' odin student prishol na lektsiyu.)* — Twenty-one students came to the lecture.
- **У неё двадцать три кошки.** *(U neyo dvadtsat' tri koshki.)* — She has twenty-three cats.
- **В комнате пять стульев.** *(V komnate pyat' stul'yev.)* — There are five chairs in the room.
- **Мы купили двенадцать яиц.** *(My kupili dvenadtsat' yaits.)* — We bought twelve eggs.
- **На столе четыре чашки.** *(Na stole chetyre chashki.)* — There are four cups on the table.
- **Он прочитал тридцать одну страницу.** *(On prochital tridtsat' odnu stranitsu.)* — He read thirty-one pages.

## Gender Agreement with 1 and 2

### Один / Одна / Одно

The number **1** fully agrees in gender (and case) with the noun it modifies:

- **Один дом** *(Odin dom)* — one house (masculine)
- **Одна комната** *(Odna komnata)* — one room (feminine)
- **Одно окно** *(Odno okno)* — one window (neuter)

### Два / Две

The number **2** has two forms:

- **Два** — with masculine and neuter nouns: **два стола** *(dva stola)*, **два окна** *(dva okna)*
- **Две** — with feminine nouns: **две книги** *(dve knigi)*, **две руки** *(dve ruki)*

### Examples

- **Один мальчик играет в парке.** *(Odin mal'chik igrayut v parke.)* — One boy is playing in the park.
- **Одна женщина стоит у входа.** *(Odna zhenshchina stoit u vkhoda.)* — One woman is standing at the entrance.
- **Одно письмо пришло сегодня.** *(Odno pis'mo prishlo segodnya.)* — One letter arrived today.
- **У него два сына и две дочери.** *(U nego dva syna i dve docheri.)* — He has two sons and two daughters.
- **На столе два яблока.** *(Na stole dva yabloka.)* — There are two apples on the table.

## Ordinal Numbers

Ordinal numbers (first, second, third...) behave like **adjectives** in Russian — they agree in gender, number, and case with the noun.

| Ordinal | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter |
|---------|-----------|----------|--------|
| 1st | первый | первая | первое |
| 2nd | второй | вторая | второе |
| 3rd | третий | третья | третье |
| 4th | четвёртый | четвёртая | четвёртое |
| 5th | пятый | пятая | пятое |
| 6th | шестой | шестая | шестое |
| 7th | седьмой | седьмая | седьмое |
| 8th | восьмой | восьмая | восьмое |
| 9th | девятый | девятая | девятое |
| 10th | десятый | десятая | десятое |

### Examples

- **Это мой первый урок русского языка.** *(Eto moy pervyy urok russkogo yazyka.)* — This is my first Russian language lesson.
- **Она живёт на третьем этаже.** *(Ona zhivyot na tret'yem etazhe.)* — She lives on the third floor.
- **Второй вопрос был самым трудным.** *(Vtoroy vopros byl samym trudnym.)* — The second question was the hardest.
- **Мы сели в пятый вагон.** *(My seli v pyatyy vagon.)* — We got into the fifth car (train carriage).
- **Сегодня двадцать первое декабря.** *(Segodnya dvadtsat' pervoye dekabrya.)* — Today is the twenty-first of December.

> **Tip:** In compound ordinals (like 21st, 35th), only the **last** word is ordinal: двадцать **первый**, тридцать **пятый**.

## Telling Time and Dates

### Telling Time

Russian tells time using ordinal numbers for hours and cardinal numbers for minutes.

- **Сейчас два часа.** *(Seychas dva chasa.)* — It is two o'clock.
- **Сейчас пять часов.** *(Seychas pyat' chasov.)* — It is five o'clock.
- **Сейчас десять минут третьего.** *(Seychas desyat' minut tret'yego.)* — It is ten past two (lit. ten minutes of the third).
- **Без пятнадцати три.** *(Bez pyatnadtsati tri.)* — It is a quarter to three (lit. without fifteen, three).
- **Половина шестого.** *(Polovina shestogo.)* — It is half past five (lit. half of the sixth).

> **Note:** Russian time uses the genitive of the **next** hour for "past" times: десять минут **третьего** = 2:10 (ten minutes of the third hour).

### Dates

Dates use **ordinal numbers in the neuter genitive** for "on a date" and the **genitive of the month**:

- **Первое января.** *(Pervoye yanvarya.)* — January 1st.
- **Двадцать третье февраля.** *(Dvadtsat' tret'ye fevralya.)* — February 23rd.
- **Она родилась восьмого марта.** *(Ona rodilas' vos'mogo marta.)* — She was born on March 8th.
- **Мы приедем пятнадцатого июня.** *(My priyedem pyatnadtsatogo iyunya.)* — We will arrive on June 15th.
- **Экзамен будет десятого сентября.** *(Ekzamen budet desyatogo sentyabrya.)* — The exam will be on September 10th.

## Practice Summary

Here are the key rules to remember:

1. **After 1** → nominative singular (один дом, одна книга)
2. **After 2–4** → genitive singular (два дома, три книги)
3. **After 5–20** → genitive plural (пять домов, шесть книг)
4. **11–14** are exceptions — always genitive plural
5. **Один** agrees in gender; **два/две** depends on feminine vs. masculine/neuter
6. **Ordinals** decline like adjectives
7. **Dates** use neuter ordinal + genitive of the month

Keep practicing with real-world numbers — prices, ages, addresses, and phone numbers — to build fluency with Russian counting!
