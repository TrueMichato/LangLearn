# Short-Form Adjectives (Краткие прилагательные)

Russian adjectives have two forms: the **long form** (полная форма), which you already know from declension tables, and the **short form** (краткая форма). Short-form adjectives are used only as **predicates** (after the subject, like English "The book is interesting") and cannot be declined — they only change for **gender** and **number**.

---

## 1. Formation of Short-Form Adjectives

Short-form adjectives are created by removing the long-form ending and adding short endings:

| Gender/Number | Long form ending | Short form ending | Example (красивый) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Masculine | -ый / -ий / -ой | — (zero) | красив *(krasiv)* |
| Feminine | -ая / -яя | -а / -я | красива *(krasiva)* |
| Neuter | -ое / -ее | -о / -е | красиво *(krasivo)* |
| Plural | -ые / -ие | -ы / -и | красивы *(krasivy)* |

### More examples

| Long form | Masc. short | Fem. short | Neut. short | Pl. short |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| новый *(novyy)* — new | нов | нова | ново | новы |
| готовый *(gotovyy)* — ready | готов | готова | готово | готовы |
| здоровый *(zdorovyy)* — healthy | здоров | здорова | здорово | здоровы |
| счастливый *(schastlivyy)* — happy | счастлив | счастлива | счастливо | счастливы |
| нужный *(nuzhnyy)* — needed | нужен | нужна | нужно | нужны |

**Он готов.** *(On gotov.)* — He is ready.

**Она готова.** *(Ona gotova.)* — She is ready.

**Всё готово.** *(Vsyo gotovo.)* — Everything is ready.

**Мы готовы.** *(My gotovy.)* — We are ready.

---

## 2. Fleeting Vowels

Many masculine short forms insert a **fleeting vowel** (е or о) between the last two consonants to make pronunciation easier:

| Long form | Masc. short | Why |
|---|---|---|
| нужный *(nuzhnyy)* | нужен *(nuzhen)* | -жн- → -жен- |
| интересный *(interesnyy)* | интересен *(interesen)* | -сн- → -сен- |
| полный *(polnyy)* | полон *(polon)* | -лн- → -лон- |
| умный *(umnyy)* | умён *(umyon)* | -мн- → -мён- |
| больной *(bolnoy)* | болен *(bolen)* | -льн- → -лен- |
| сильный *(silnyy)* | силён *(silyon)* | -льн- → -лён- |
| краткий *(kratkiy)* | краток *(kratok)* | -тк- → -ток- |

This only affects the **masculine** form. The feminine, neuter, and plural do not have fleeting vowels:

**Он болен.** *(On bolen.)* — He is sick.

**Она больна.** *(Ona bolna.)* — She is sick.

**Они больны.** *(Oni bolny.)* — They are sick.

## 3. When to Use Short-Form vs. Long-Form Adjectives

This is the key question. Both forms can appear as predicates, but they carry different nuances:

### Short form — temporary state, specific situation, or formal tone

**Он болен.** *(On bolen.)* — He is sick (right now).

**Она занята.** *(Ona zanyata.)* — She is busy (at the moment).

**Я рад вас видеть.** *(Ya rad vas videt.)* — I'm glad to see you.

**Этот вопрос очень важен.** *(Etot vopros ochen vazhen.)* — This question is very important (in this context).

### Long form — permanent quality or general characteristic

**Он больной.** *(On bolnoy.)* — He is a sickly person (characteristically).

**Она занятая.** *(Ona zanyataya.)* — She is a busy person (in general).

**Этот вопрос важный.** *(Etot vopros vazhnyy.)* — This question is important (in general).

### Short form — "too much" with слишком or excess

**Этот костюм мне велик.** *(Etot kostyum mne velik.)* — This suit is too big for me.

**Эти туфли мне малы.** *(Eti tufli mne maly.)* — These shoes are too small for me.

**Юбка коротка.** *(Yubka korotka.)* — The skirt is too short.

> These short forms imply excess and cannot be replaced by long forms with the same meaning.

## 4. Adjectives Used Only in Short Form (as Predicates)

Some adjectives are **only** used in short form when serving as predicates:

| Short form | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| рад, рада, радо, рады | glad | Я рад! *(Ya rad!)* — I'm glad! |
| должен, должна, должно, должны | must/should | Он должен работать. *(On dolzhen rabotat.)* — He must work. |
| обязан, обязана, обязано, обязаны | obliged | Мы обязаны помочь. *(My obyazany pomoch.)* — We are obliged to help. |
| склонен, склонна, склонно, склонны | inclined | Она склонна согласиться. *(Ona sklonna soglasitsya.)* — She is inclined to agree. |
| намерен, намерена, намерено, намерены | intending | Я намерен уехать. *(Ya nameren uyekhat.)* — I intend to leave. |
| виноват, виновата, виновато, виноваты | guilty/at fault | Он виноват. *(On vinovat.)* — He is at fault. |
| жив, жива, живо, живы | alive | Она жива! *(Ona zhiva!)* — She is alive! |

**Я рад вас видеть!** *(Ya rad vas videt!)* — I'm glad to see you!

**Ты должен это сделать.** *(Ty dolzhen eto sdelat.)* — You must do this.

**Кто виноват?** *(Kto vinovat?)* — Who is to blame?

## 5. Short-Form Adjectives with Infinitives

Short-form adjectives often combine with infinitives to express ability, readiness, or obligation:

**Он готов помочь.** *(On gotov pomoch.)* — He is ready to help.

**Она способна решить эту задачу.** *(Ona sposobna reshit etu zadachu.)* — She is capable of solving this problem.

**Мы рады сообщить вам...** *(My rady soobshchit vam...)* — We are glad to inform you...

**Он склонен преувеличивать.** *(On sklonen preuvelichivat.)* — He is inclined to exaggerate.

**Ты волен выбирать.** *(Ty volen vybirat.)* — You are free to choose.

**Я вынужден отказаться.** *(Ya vynuzhden otkazatsya.)* — I am forced to refuse.

---

## 6. Short-Form Adjectives in Impersonal Constructions

Neuter short forms are widely used in **impersonal** (subjectless) sentences:

**Важно знать правду.** *(Vazhno znat pravdu.)* — It's important to know the truth.

**Трудно понять.** *(Trudno ponyat.)* — It's hard to understand.

**Интересно, что он скажет.** *(Interesno, chto on skazhet.)* — It's interesting what he will say.

**Необходимо закончить работу.** *(Neobkhodimo zakonchit rabotu.)* — It's necessary to finish the work.

**Очевидно, что он прав.** *(Ochevidno, chto on prav.)* — It's obvious that he is right.

**Странно, что никто не пришёл.** *(Stranno, chto nikto ne prishyol.)* — It's strange that nobody came.

---

## 7. Stress Shifts in Short Forms

Short-form adjectives often have **different stress** from their long forms. The feminine form frequently shifts stress to the ending:

| Long form (stress) | Masc. | Fem. | Neut. | Pl. |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| до́рогой (dorogóy) | до́рог | дорога́ | до́рого | до́роги |
| молодо́й (molodóy) | мо́лод | молода́ | мо́лодо | мо́лоды |
| хороши́й (khoróshiy) | хоро́ш | хороша́ | хорошо́ | хороши́ |
| у́мный (úmnyy) | умён | умна́ | умно́ | умны́ |

**Она молода́ и красива́.** *(Ona molodá i krasivá.)* — She is young and beautiful.

**Погода хороша́.** *(Pogoda khoroshá.)* — The weather is good.

## 8. Common Mistakes

❌ **Я рады вас видеть.** — Incorrect: рады is plural, but я is singular.
✅ **Я рад вас видеть.** — I'm glad to see you.

❌ **Этот костюм большой мне.** — Incorrect for "too big for me."
✅ **Этот костюм мне велик.** — This suit is too big for me.

❌ **Она должная работать.** — Incorrect: должен is only short form.
✅ **Она должна работать.** — She must work.

❌ **Он рад.** / **Он радый.** — "Радый" does not exist.
✅ **Он рад.** — He is glad. (Only short form exists.)

---

## Key Takeaways

1. Short-form adjectives are used **only as predicates** and change for gender and number (not case).
2. They often indicate a **temporary state** or **specific situation**, while long forms suggest **permanent qualities**.
3. Some adjectives (рад, должен, виноват) exist **only** in short form as predicates.
4. Masculine short forms often have **fleeting vowels** (нужен, полон, умён).
5. Neuter short forms are used in **impersonal constructions** (важно, трудно, интересно).
6. Watch for **stress shifts**, especially in the feminine form.
